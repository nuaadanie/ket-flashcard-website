# Journey to the West · Chapter Thirty-Five: A Visit to the King

## ①

> "Princess Hundred Flowers tricked me!" said the monster.

  He stamped his foot, then he sighed.

> "I can't be angry with her. I love her too much. But that pig got away. He'll go back to the king and troops will come."

  The monster thought for a minute. Finally, he had a plan. He went to the princess's room.

> "My sweet," said the monster. "I'm going to visit your father."

> "What?" said Princess Hundred Flowers in shock.

## ②

> "I'm going to tell him what a nice fellow I am," said the monster. "We can all be a happy family."

  Before the princess could say another word, the monster left the room.

> "I need a disguise," he said to himself.

  He recited a quick spell. Light sparkled. Now the monster looked like a handsome young man. He laughed.

## ③

  Bajie ran back to the Precious Image Kingdom. He told the king that he and Wu Jing couldn't save Princess Hundred Flowers. The pig hung his head.

> "As I ran away, Wu Jing was captured."

> "We must save my daughter," said the king. He turned to his general. "Gather the troops."

  A messenger ran into the court.

> "Your Majesty, your son-in-law is here to see you," said the messenger.

  The king was confused.

> "I don't have a son-in-law."

## ④

  A handsome young man walked into the hall.

> "Hello, Father. I'm sorry I haven't visited you until now."

> "Who are you?" asked the king.

> "I married Princess Hundred Flowers thirteen years ago," said the young man. "I found her in a forest just as a tiger was about to attack her. I saved her life and chased the tiger away."

  The king relaxed a little.

> "That was very brave."

> "Yes, it was very brave," said the young man. "However, that tiger has wanted revenge ever since. Some time ago it became a spirit. Now it disguises itself as a monk."

  The young man narrowed his eyes at the Tang Monk and pointed.

> "That monk!"

> "What?" cried the Tang Monk.

## ⑤

  The king frowned.

> "I don't believe you."

> "I'll prove it," said the young man.

  He pointed at the monk again and recited a spell. Light sparkled. Everybody gasped. Now a tiger sat in the Tang Monk's spot.

## ⑥

  Later, in the stable, a boy was giving hay to the Tang Monk's horse.

> "Did you hear about the Tang Monk?" asked another boy. "He was really a tiger spirit."

> "That young man changed him back and locked him up for him. Wow," said the first boy. "Where is the tiger now?"

> "He's locked up in a cage," said the other boy.

## ⑦

  That night, Bajie was sitting alone outside when he heard a voice.

> "Bajie."

  Bajie looked around.

> "Who's calling me?"

  He saw the horse peeking from behind a wall.

> "You're talking?" cried Bajie.

> "Yes," said the horse. "We have a problem. A young man turned the Tang Monk into a tiger. The monk is locked in a cage now."

> "Oh, I know!" cried Bajie. "It's terrible. Master is now a tiger and Wu Jing was captured by a monster." He sighed. "The journey is over. Let's just go home."

## ⑧

  The horse stamped on Bajie's foot with his hoof.

> "Ouch!" cried the pig. "Why did you do that?"

> "We're not going home," said the horse. "The young man is probably just the monster in disguise. We need to defeat him and save our companions."

> "But how?" asked Bajie.

  The horse thought for a minute.

> "You must find Wukong. He can help us."

  Bajie shook his head.

> "He won't help us."

# 🇨🇳 第三十五章：拜见国王


## ①

> "百花公主骗了我！"妖怪说道。

  他跺了跺脚，又叹了口气。

> "我对她生不起气来。我太爱她了。可是那头猪跑了，他会回去找国王，国王肯定会派兵的。"

  妖怪想了一会儿，终于有了主意。他来到公主的房间。

> "我的心上人，"妖怪说，"我要去见见你父亲。"

> "什么？"百花公主大吃一惊。

## ②

> "我要去告诉他我是多么好的一个人，"妖怪说，"我们大家都能成为幸福的一家人。"

  公主还没来得及说什么，妖怪已经转身离开了房间。

> "我得伪装一下，"他自言自语。

  他念了个快速咒语，光芒一闪，妖怪变成了一个英俊的年轻小伙子。他得意地笑了。

## ③

  八戒跑回宝象国，告诉国王他和悟净没能救出百花公主。八戒垂下了脑袋。

> "我跑的时候，悟净被妖怪抓住了。"

> "我们必须救回女儿，"国王说。他转向将军，"集合军队！"

  一名信使跑进大殿。

> "陛下，您的驸马来见您了。"信使说。

  国王一脸困惑。

> "我哪来的驸马？"

## ④

  一个英俊的年轻小伙子走进大殿。

> "岳丈大人好。很抱歉我一直没有来拜访您。"

> "你是谁？"国王问道。

> "我十三年前娶了百花公主，"年轻人说，"我在森林里发现她时，一只老虎正要扑向她。我救了她，把老虎赶跑了。"

  国王稍微放松了一些。

> "那倒是很勇敢。"

> "是的，非常勇敢，"年轻人说，"不过那只老虎从那以后一直想报仇。前阵子它修炼成了精，现在变成了一个和尚四处招摇撞骗。"

  年轻人眯起眼睛，指着唐僧。

> "就是那个和尚！"

> "什么？"唐僧大叫。

## ⑤

  国王皱起了眉头。

> "我不相信你。"

> "我来证明给你看，"年轻人说。

  他再次指向唐僧，念起了咒语。光芒一闪，众人大惊失色——坐在唐僧位置上的竟然是一只老虎。

## ⑥

  后来，在马厩里，一个小男孩正在给唐僧的马喂草料。

> "你听说唐僧的事了吗？"另一个男孩问道，"他原来是个老虎精。"

> "那个年轻人把他变回原形关了起来。哇，"第一个男孩说，"老虎现在在哪里？"

> "被关在笼子里了。"另一个男孩说。

## ⑦

  那天晚上，八戒独自坐在外面，忽然听到一个声音。

> "八戒。"

  八戒环顾四周。

> "谁在叫我？"

  他看到那匹马从墙后面探出头来。

> "你会说话？"八戒惊叫道。

> "是的，"马说，"我们遇到大麻烦了。一个年轻人把唐僧变成了老虎。那位师傅现在被关在笼子里。"

> "唉，我知道！"八戒大喊，"太可怕了。师父变成了老虎，悟净又被妖怪抓走了。"他叹了口气，"这趟旅程结束了，咱们还是各回各家吧。"

## ⑧

  马抬起蹄子踩了八戒一脚。

> "哎呦！"八戒叫了起来，"你为什么踩我？"

> "我们不回家，"马说，"那个年轻小伙子八成就是妖怪伪装的。我们必须打败他，救出同伴。"

> "可怎么打呢？"八戒问。

  马想了一会儿。

> "你必须去找悟空，只有他才能帮我们。"

  八戒摇了摇头。

> "他不会帮我们的。"
