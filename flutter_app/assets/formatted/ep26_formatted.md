# Journey to the West · Chapter Twenty-Six: The Monster in the River

## ①

> "I hope we reach the Western Paradise soon," said the Tang monk one morning.

  Wukong and his companions were eating breakfast. The monkey laughed.

> "The journey will take many years, Master. And you must face many challenges along the way. Buddha doesn't give the true scriptures to just anyone. You must prove your worth by surviving a long and difficult journey."

  The monk sighed. Bajie put down his bowl.

> "I'm tired of this journey!" he cried. "My legs hurt and we never have enough food."

> "Stop thinking about food, Bajie," said Wukong. "We're Buddhist monks now. We should think only about reaching the Western Paradise."

  The pig sighed.

## ②

  After he washed the bowls, the three companions headed down the road. They soon reached a river. The water splashed and a purple monster leaped from the waves.

> "Master, stay back!" shouted Wukong. He pulled out his iron bar and blocked the monster's spear.

  *Clang!*

  Bajie lunged at the monster, swinging his rake. The monster dove back into the river.

> "Bajie, my powers aren't strong underwater," said Wukong. "Dive in and get him to chase you out of the river. Then I'll surprise him."

> "Okay," said the pig. "Let's wait until he comes all the way up on shore, though. Then I'll block his path so he can't go back in the river."

## ③

  Bajie dived into the water. Wukong waited. A moment later, the pig was back with the monster following. The monkey swung his iron bar and missed. The monster dived back underwater.

> "You didn't wait until he was on shore," said Bajie. "You're too impatient."

  Wukong shrieked in laughter.

> "Let's yell at the monster. Maybe he'll come back."

  Wukong and Bajie yelled at the river for more than an hour. But the monster did not come back. Finally, the monkey sighed.

> "We can't cross this river with the monster in there. I'll ask Guanyin for help."

## ④

  Wukong went to Mount Potalaka and told Guanyin about the monster.

> "His name is Sha Wujing. He was waiting for you. He's going to help the Tang monk reach the Western Paradise."

> "I didn't know that!" cried Wukong.

  Guanyin went back to the river with Wukong.

> "Sha Wujing!" she called. "The Tang monk is here."

  The monster jumped out of the river and bowed.

> "I'm sorry, Bodhisattva," he said. "I didn't know he'd arrived."

> "These two are his companions," said Guanyin. She waved her hand toward Wukong and Bajie.

  Guanyin looked surprised.

> "They attacked me!"

> "You attacked us first!" shouted Bajie.

## ⑤

> "Stop arguing," said Guanyin. "Wujing, you will help the Tang monk reach the west."

> "Yes, Bodhisattva," said Wujing.

  Guanyin disappeared, and Wujing joined the group. He led the horse while the Tang monk rode.

> "I was being punished for breaking a glass in heaven," said Wujing one day. "When we reach the Western Paradise, I will be forgiven."

> "Bajie and I were punished for crimes in heaven too," said Wukong. "So is the horse."

> "Did you know the horse is really a dragon?"

  Wujing looked doubtful.

> "This horse is too slow to be a dragon."

> "Oh, really?" said the monkey. "Watch this."

  He struck the horse. The horse shot down the road as fast as lightning. Its reins were ripped from Wujing's hands.

> "Hey!" cried the Tang monk.

  When the horse finally stopped, the Tang monk was in a dark forest. He gasped in fear. The horse was filled with wolves, bears, and tigers.

# 🇨🇳 第二十六章：河中妖怪


## ①

> "但愿我们快点到达西天吧，"一天早上唐僧说。

  悟空和同伴们正在吃早饭。猴子笑了起来。

> "这段路要走上好几年呢，师父。一路上您还得经历许多磨难。佛祖不会随便把真经给人的。你必须通过漫长而艰难的旅程来证明你的诚意。"

  和尚叹了口气。八戒放下了碗。

> "这趟路我走累了！"他叫道，"我的腿好疼，而且我们总是吃不饱。"

> "八戒，别老想着吃，"悟空说，"我们现在是佛门弟子了。心里只该想着去西天取经。"

  猪叹了口气。

## ②

  八戒洗完碗后，三个同伴上了路。他们很快来到了一条河边。河水翻涌，一个紫色的妖怪从波浪中跃了出来。

> "师父，退后！"悟空大喊。他掏出铁棒挡住了妖怪的长枪。

  铛！

  八戒挥着钉耙朝妖怪扑去。妖怪一头扎回了河里。

> "八戒，我的水下功夫不厉害，"悟空说，"你下水去引他追你上岸。然后我出其不意地打他。"

> "好，"八戒说，"不过我们先等他完全上了岸再说。我堵住他的路，不让他再回到水里。"

## ③

  八戒纵身跳入水中。悟空在岸上等。不一会儿，八戒跑了回来，妖怪跟在后面。猴子挥起铁棒，却打空了。妖怪又钻回了水里。

> "你没等他上岸就打，"八戒说，"你也太心急了。"

  悟空大叫着笑了。

> "咱们对着河里喊，说不定妖怪会再出来。"

  悟空和八戒对着河面喊了一个多钟头。可妖怪就是不出来了。终于，猴子叹了口气。

> "河里有妖怪挡着，我们过不了河。我去请观音菩萨帮忙。"

## ④

  悟空来到普陀山，把妖怪的事告诉了观音菩萨。

> "他叫沙悟净，在等你呢。他会帮助唐僧到达西天。"

> "我不知道呀！"悟空叫道。

  观音和悟空一起回到了河边。

> "沙悟净！"她喊道，"唐僧到了。"

  妖怪从河中跳出来，行了个礼。

> "菩萨，对不起，"他说，"我不知道他来了。"

> "这两位是他的同伴，"观音说，她朝悟空和八戒挥了挥手。

  观音露出惊讶的表情。

> "他们打我！"

> "是你先动手的！"八戒叫道。

## ⑤

> "别吵了，"观音说，"悟净，你要帮助唐僧一路向西。"

> "是，菩萨，"悟净说。

  观音消失了，悟净加入了队伍。他牵着马，唐僧骑在马上。

> "我在天上打碎了一只玻璃盏，所以被罚下凡，"一天悟净说，"等我们到了西天，我的罪就赎完了。"

> "八戒和我也都是在天上犯了罪被惩罚的，"悟空说，"还有这匹马。"

> "你可知道这匹马其实是一条龙？"

  悟净一脸怀疑。

> "这匹马这么慢，怎么可能是龙。"

> "哦，是吗？"猴子说，"你看好了。"

  他朝马打了一下。马飞快地窜了出去，快如闪电。缰绳从悟净手中挣脱。

> "哎呀！"唐僧叫道。

  马终于停下来时，唐僧已经身处一片黑暗的森林里。他吓得倒抽了一口冷气。四周到处都是狼、熊和老虎。
