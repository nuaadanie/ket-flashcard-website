# Journey to the West · Chapter Thirteen: The True Scriptures

## ①

  Five hundred years passed. Wukong stayed trapped under the mountain. A thick layer of moss covered the mountain.

  One day Buddha sat in the Thunderclap Monastery in the Western Paradise. He was talking to his students.

  "The people in the East, in the Tang Empire, need our help," said Buddha. "They lie, cheat and steal. They are greedy and constantly fighting." He held up a bundle of scrolls. "These are the true Scriptures. They will teach the people in the East to act better."

  "Wonderful," said one student. "Let us bring the Scriptures to the Tang Empire."

  Buddha shook his head. "No," he said. "A true believer, a monk, needs to come here to get them. Will anyone go to the East to find a worthy monk?"

  Guanyin stepped forward. "I will go."

## ②

  The Tang Emperor sat as his head monk read to a small group of people. The monk was reading old religious texts. A woman approached the monk.

  "Why are you reading those old texts?" she asked. "Don't you know about the true Scriptures?"

  The Emperor stood up. "How dare you interrupt my—"

  "It is okay," said the monk. He walked over to the woman. "What are the true Scriptures?"

  The woman looked at the monk carefully. "They contain many lessons. People who learn the lessons never have to suffer again."

  "Do you have them here?" asked the monk.

## ③

  "No," said the woman. "They are with Buddha in the Western Paradise. Someone must travel to the Thunderclap Monastery to get them. But only a true believer may make the journey."

  She took a robe out of her bag. "Put this on."

  The monk put on the robe. Beams of light shone from it.

  "You are a true believer," said the woman. "Will you make the journey?"

  "Yes," said the monk, looking down at the glowing robe. "It will be an honor."

  "The journey will be very difficult," said the woman. "Wide rivers and steep mountains will block your path. Fierce creatures and evil demons will try to attack you. But do not fear. You will have help from me."

  Suddenly the room filled with blinding light. The woman rose into the air and changed form.

  "It is the Bodhisattva Guanyin!" someone cried.

  The Emperor and everyone else in the room bowed. Guanyin rose higher and then disappeared.

## ④

  The next morning there was a huge feast. The Tang Emperor sat next to the monk.

  "Your journey will help our empire greatly," said the Emperor. "No longer will our people suffer or treat each other badly. From now on I will think of you as my brother. You will be called the Tang Monk."

  "Thank you, Your Majesty," said the Tang Monk.

  "How long will the journey take?" asked the Emperor.

  "I will return in a few years," said the monk.

  Later that day the Tang Monk got ready for his journey. He put the special robe into a bag. He put dry foods into another bag. A big crowd gathered to say goodbye.

  "Good luck, my brother," said the Tang Emperor.

  The Tang Monk climbed onto a horse and rode toward the west.

## ⑤

  He had traveled for over a month when he came to a big mountain. Slowly his horse walked up a steep trail.

  "I have a bad feeling," said the Tang Monk. He looked around uneasily. "I hope there are not any demons here."

  Suddenly a voice boomed. "Who is walking on my mountain?"

# 🇨🇳 第十三章：真经

## ①

  五百年过去了。悟空被困在山下整整五百年。山上覆盖着厚厚的一层青苔。

  有一天，佛祖坐在西天极乐世界的雷音寺中，正和弟子们讲话。

  "东土大唐的人需要我们帮忙，"佛祖说，"他们撒谎、行骗、偷盗，贪婪成性，争斗不断。"他举起一卷经书，"这些就是真经。它们可以教导东土的人民向善。"

  "太好了，"一名弟子说，"我们把经书送到大唐去吧。"

  佛祖摇了摇头。"不行，"他说，"必须要一位虔诚的信徒，一位僧人，亲自来这里取经。谁能去东土找到合适的人选？"

  观音菩萨走上前来。"我去。"

## ②

  大唐皇帝坐在那里，听他的御弟法师给一小群人诵经。那位法师正在诵读古老的经文。一位女子走到法师面前。

  "你为什么读这些旧经文？"她问，"你不知道有真经吗？"

  皇帝站了起来。"你怎么敢打断朕的——"

  "没关系，"法师说。他走过去问那个女子。"什么是真经？"

  女子仔细打量了法师一番。"真经里有许多道理。懂得这些道理的人就再也不用受苦了。"

  "你们这里有吗？"法师问。

## ③

  "没有，"女子说，"真经在西天极乐世界的如来佛祖那里。必须要有人到雷音寺去取。可是只有虔诚的信徒才能走到那里。"

  她从包中取出一件袈裟。"穿上吧。"

  法师披上袈裟。袈裟上放射出万道金光。

  "您是一位虔诚的信徒，"女子说。"您愿意去取经吗？"

  "我愿意，"法师低头望着发光的袈裟说，"这是我的荣幸。"

  "这条路会很艰难，"女子说，"宽阔的江河、陡峭的高山会挡住你的去路。凶猛的野兽、邪恶的妖怪会来攻击你。但是不要怕，我会帮助你的。"

  忽然房间里亮起耀眼的光芒。女子腾空而起，变成了菩萨的法相。

  "是观音菩萨！"有人喊道。

  皇帝和房间里的所有人都跪下叩拜。观音菩萨越升越高，然后消失了。

## ④

  第二天早晨，宫中设下了盛大的宴会。唐太宗与法师并肩而坐。

  "你的旅程会大大地帮助我们的国家，"皇帝说，"从此再也不会让百姓受苦、再也不会让别人欺负自己人了。从今以后我把你当做御弟，你就叫唐僧吧。"

  "感谢陛下，"唐僧说。

  "这趟路要走多久？"皇帝问。

  "几年后我会回来，"法师说。

  当天晚些时候，唐僧收拾行装，将袈裟装进一个包袱，又把干粮装进另一个包袱。一大群人赶来送行。

  "一路平安，好兄弟，"唐太宗说。

  唐僧翻身上马，向西面驰去。

## ⑤

  走了一个多月，唐僧来到了一座大山前。他的马缓缓走在陡峭的山路上。

  "我有一种不祥的预感，"唐僧说。他不安地环顾四周，"我希望这里没有妖怪。"

  突然，一个声音轰然响起。"是什么人在我山上走动？"
