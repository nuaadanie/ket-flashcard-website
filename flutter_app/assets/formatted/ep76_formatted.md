# Journey to the West · Chapter Seventy-Six: The Iron Immortal

## ①

  Wukong was once again traveling west with the Tang monk.

> "Master," said Wukong. "While I was at Mount Potalaka, I thought a lot. I realize now that it's wrong to harm people. I won't ever do it again."

> "I'm glad, Wukong," said the Tang monk.

  Bajie moaned.

## ②

> "Can we please slow down?"

  Wukong laughed.

> "Not much has changed. Bajie still complains all the time."

  Wujing said with a frown, "It is hot. Maybe we should take a break."

> "Yes," said the Tang monk. "But I don't understand why it's so hot. It's not summer."

## ③

  The travelers soon reached a village. An old man offered to take them in for the night. Later that evening they were all eating dinner.

> "Isn't it always so hot here?" asked Bajie.

  The man nodded.

> "The Mountain of Flames is near here. It's always burning."

> "Well, I'll be happy when we continue on our journey," said the pig. "Then we can get away from this heat."

## ④

> "Where are you going?" asked the man.

> "We're going to the Western Paradise," said the Tang monk.

  The man frowned.

> "You can't travel west from here."

  Everyone stopped eating and looked at the old man.

> "Why not?" asked the Tang monk.

## ⑤

> "The Mountain of Flames is to the west," said the man. "It's impossible to pass."

> "We'll go around it," said the Tang monk.

  The old man shook his head.

> "All the land around the mountain is covered in flames. The land stretches to the north and the south. It's impossible to go around."

> "Then we must wait for a strong rainstorm," said Bajie. "That will put out the flames."

## ⑥

> "No," said the man. "The flames are magical. There's just one spirit who can put them out."

> "Oh?" asked Wukong.

> "She's called the Iron Immortal," said the man. "She has a special fan that can put out the flames. It's made from a plantain leaf."

  Wukong stood up.

> "Where does the Iron Immortal live? I'll ask her to put out the flames."

## ⑦

> "He won't listen to you!" said the man. "She is very powerful."

  Wukong folded his arms in front of his chest.

> "I am as powerful as a god. Just tell me where she lives."

  The old man told Wukong where the Iron Immortal lived. The monkey leaped into the air. The man gasped.

> "He's a spirit!"

## ⑧

  Wukong was soon on the Iron Immortal's mountain. He found a cave door and knocked.

> "Open up!" he demanded.

  The door flew open and a tall woman appeared. She held a large fan.

> "Who are you?" she asked.

> "I am Sun Wukong. I need to borrow your fan."

## ⑨

> "Sun Wukong? Oh!" cried the Iron Immortal. "You are my worst enemy!"

  Wukong was confused.

> "I am? Why?"

  The Iron Immortal glared at the monkey.

> "My husband is the Bull Demon King, who used to be your friend. And our son is Red Boy. Because of you, Red Boy is a prisoner of the Bodhisattva Guanyin."

## ⑩

  Wukong laughed.

> "Red Boy isn't a prisoner. He is Guanyin's student. He isn't evil anymore."

  The Iron Immortal shrieked.

> "That's even worse!"

  Wukong sighed.

> "Can I please just borrow your fan for a little while?"

> "No," said the woman.

## ⑪

> "Well then, can I speak with the Bull Demon King?" asked the monkey.

> "He's at a banquet," said the Iron Immortal. "Now, leave!"

  Wukong grew angry.

> "I'm not leaving until I borrow your fan!"

> "Mush!" The Iron Immortal waved the fan.

  A furious wind blew, and Wukong was gone.

# 🇨🇳 第七十六章：铁扇仙

## ①

  悟空又和唐僧一起踏上西行的路。

> "师父，"悟空说，"我在普陀山待了这段时间想了很多。我现在明白了伤害人是不对的，我再也不会那样做了。"

> "悟空，你能这样想我就放心了。"唐僧说。

  八戒在一旁哼哼唧唧。

## ②

> "我们能走慢点吗？"

  悟空笑了。

> "真是一点都没变，八戒还是整天抱怨。"

  悟净皱了皱眉说：

> "实在太热了。我们歇一歇吧。"

> "好，"唐僧说，"可我真不明白为什么这么热，现在又不是夏天。"

## ③

  一行人来到一座村庄，一位老人留他们过夜。傍晚时分，大家围在一起吃晚饭。

> "这里一直这么热吗？"八戒问。

  老人点了点头。

> "附近有座火焰山，山上一直在燃烧。"

> "那我们继续赶路就好了，"八戒说，"就能离开这个鬼地方了。"

## ④

> "你们要去哪里？"老人问。

> "我们要去西天取经。"唐僧说。

  老人皱起了眉头。

> "你们从这里是过不了火焰山的。"

  大家都停下来吃饭，望着老人。

> "为什么？"唐僧问。

## ⑤

> "火焰山就在西边，"老人说，"根本过不去。"

> "我们可以绕过去。"唐僧说。

  老人摇了摇头。

> "火焰山四面都是火，南北也一样。根本绕不过去。"

> "那等一场大雨，"八戒说，"雨就能把火浇灭。"

## ⑥

> "不行，"老人说，"那火是魔火，浇不灭的。只有一个神仙能灭那火。"

> "哦？"悟空问。

> "她叫铁扇仙，"老人说，"她有一把神奇的芭蕉扇，能熄灭火焰山的火。"

  悟空站起身来。

> "她住在哪里？我去请她灭火。"

## ⑦

> "你请不动她的！"老人说，"她法力高强。"

  悟空双手抱在胸前。

> "我和神仙一样厉害。你只管告诉我她在哪里就行。"

  老人告诉了悟空铁扇仙的住处。悟空纵身一跃飞上天空。老人倒吸一口气。

> "原来是个神仙！"

## ⑧

  悟空很快到了铁扇仙的山上。发现一个洞口，他上前敲门。

> "快开门！"他喝道。

  门猛地开了，一个高个女人出现了，手里拿着一把大扇子。

> "你是谁？"她问。

> "我是孙悟空，来借你的芭蕉扇一用。"

## ⑨

> "孙悟空？哎呀！"铁扇仙惊叫起来，"你是我的死对头！"

  悟空糊涂了。

> "我是？为什么？"

  铁扇仙怒视着猴子。

> "我丈夫是牛魔王，以前是你的朋友。我儿子是红孩儿。因为你的缘故，红孩儿被观音菩萨收走了。"

## ⑩

  悟空大笑。

> "红孩儿不是被关押，他是观音菩萨的弟子，已经改邪归正了。"

  铁扇仙尖声叫道：

> "那更糟！"

  悟空叹了口气。

> "那你能不能把扇子借我用一用？"

> "不行！"女人断然拒绝。

## ⑪

> "那我跟牛魔王说行不行？"悟空问。

> "他去赴宴了，"铁扇仙说，"快走！"

  悟空恼了。

> "借不到扇子我绝不走！"

> "休想！"铁扇仙一扇扇子。

  一阵狂风刮来，悟空被吹得无影无踪。
