# Journey to the West · Chapter Six: The Dragon King

## ①

  > "What's wrong with our swords and spears?" asked the king.
  > "They're fine for ordinary monkeys and apes," said Wukong, "but I am very strong and need a much heavier weapon."
  The king thought for a moment.
  > "A Dragon King lives at the bottom of the Eastern Sea," he said. "Maybe he has a weapon you can use."
  > "That's a good idea," said Wukong. "I'll go right away and be back soon."
  He cast a quick spell so he could breathe underwater, then dived into the stream. He swam to the ocean and went deeper and deeper. Soon he arrived at a grand palace.

## ②

  The Dragon King and the Dragon Queen were sitting at a table, drinking tea. When Wukong walked in, the Dragon King looked over.
  > "Who are you?" asked the Dragon King.
  > "I am the king of the Fruit and Flower Mountain," replied Wukong. "My name is Sun Wukong."
  > "Nice to meet you," said the Queen. "Would you like some tea?"
  > "No, thank you," said Wukong. "I need a heavy weapon because my strength is like that of a god. I would like one of yours."
  > "Well," said the Dragon King, "I suppose we could find a weapon for you."
  He glanced at a turtle soldier.
  > "Find a heavy weapon for Sun Wukong."

## ③

  The turtle left the room. Soon he returned, slowly dragging an enormous sword. He dropped it and then fell, out of breath.
  Wukong picked up the sword with no trouble at all.
  Swish! Swish! He waved the sword through the air, pretending to fight.
  > "This sword is too light," said Wukong. "Find me a better weapon."

## ④

  > "This monkey is rude," the Dragon Queen whispered to her husband.
  > "You're right, dear," the Dragon King whispered back. "Let's find him something quickly so he'll leave."
  A few minutes later, several turtles and octopuses dragged an enormous spear into the room.
  > "There," said the Dragon King. "That looks like a very heavy weapon, right, Wukong?"
  Wukong lifted the spear and stabbed at the air a few times. He shook his head.
  > "This is still too light."
  The Dragon King snorted.
  > "That spear is very heavy. How can you say it's too light?"
  > "To me it's light," said Wukong. "Find me something else."
  > "We don't have anything else," said the Dragon King. "That's the heaviest weapon we have."
  Wukong stomped his foot.
  > "I'm not leaving until I get what I want!"
  > "This is an outrage!" cried the Dragon King.
  The Queen whispered to him again.
  > "What about the magical iron bar in the treasury? I'm sure that's heavy enough."
  > "We can't give you that," said the Dragon King. "It's a treasure from heaven."
  > "Who cares?" said the Queen. "I just want this monkey out of my palace."
  The Dragon King sighed.
  > "Wukong, we might have something heavy enough for you."
  > "Bring it here," said Wukong.
  > "We can't," replied the Dragon King. "It's too heavy for anyone to lift. But if you can lift it, we'll let you have it."
  > "Show me where it is," said Wukong.
  The group went to the treasury. In the center of the room stood an enormous iron pillar, radiating beams of golden light.
  > "That pillar was used to measure all the oceans and rivers," said the Dragon King.
  Wukong went closer and looked up at the massive piece of iron. The Dragon King folded his arms.
  > "If you can pick it up, you can have it."

# 🇨🇳 第六章：龙王相助

## ①

  > "我们的刀剑和长矛怎么了吗？"猴王问。
  > "它们对普通猴子、猿猴来说够用，"悟空说，"但我非常有力，需要更重的武器。"
  猴王思索片刻。
  > "东海龙王住在海底，"他说，"他也许有你能用的武器。"
  > "好主意，"悟空道，"我这就去，很快回来。"
  他念了一道咒语，以便在水中呼吸，然后潜入溪流，游向大海，越潜越深。不一会儿，来到一座宏伟的宫殿。

## ②

  龙王与龙后正坐在桌旁品茶。悟空走进来时，龙王抬眼望去。
  > "你是谁？"龙王问。
  > "我是花果山之王，"悟空答，"我叫孙悟空。"
  > "幸会，"龙后说道，"您要喝杯茶吗？"
  > "不用了，多谢，"悟空说，"我需要一件沉重的武器，因为我的力量如神一般。我想要你们的一件兵器。"
  > "好吧，"龙王道，"我派人给你找找看。"
  龙王看向一只龟将。
  > "给孙悟空找一件沉重的武器。"

## ③

  龟将退出房间。很快又回来了，慢慢拖着一把巨剑。他丢下剑，便累得气喘吁吁。
  悟空轻而易举地举起剑。
  嗖！嗖！他在空中挥舞了几下，假装战斗。
  > "这剑太轻了，"悟空道，"给我找件更好的。"

## ④

  > "这猴子真无礼，"龙后低声对丈夫说。
  > "你说得对，亲爱的，"龙王低声回应，"快点给他找件东西，让他赶紧走人。"
  几分钟后，几只海龟和章鱼拖着一杆巨矛进入房间。
  > "这个，"龙王说，"看起来是很沉的武器，对吧，悟空？"
  悟空举起长矛，朝空中刺了几下，摇摇头。
  > "还是太轻。"
  龙王哼了一声。
  > "那杆矛非常沉，你怎么说太轻？"
  > "对我来说，它还是轻，"悟空道，"再找别的。"
  > "我们没有什么别的了，"龙王说，"这是最重的武器了。"

## ⑤

  悟空跺跺脚。
  > "我拿不到想要的武器就不走！"
  > "真是无理取闹！"龙王怒道。
  龙后再次在他耳边低语。
  > "宝库里的那根定海神针呢？那肯定够沉。"
  > "我们不能给你，"龙王道，"那是天界的宝物。"
  > "谁在乎？"龙后说，"我只要这猴子离开我的宫殿。"
  龙王叹了口气。
  > "悟空，我们也许有足够沉的东西适合你。"
  > "拿来看看，"悟空说。
  > "我们拿不动，"龙王回答，"它太重了，没人能举起。但如果你能举起，我们就送给你。"
  > "带我去看看，"悟空道。
  一行人来到宝库。房间中央矗立着一根巨大的铁柱，金光闪闪。
  > "这根铁柱是用来测量四海四方的，"龙王说。
  悟空走近，仰望着巨大的铁块。龙王双手抱胸。
  > "如果你能拿得起来，就归你了。"
