# Journey to the West · Chapter Eight: A Job in Heaven

## ①

  The Jade Emperor was sitting on his throne in the Cloud Palace. A messenger ran into the court.
  > "Your Majesty!" he reported. "The Dragon King of the Eastern Sea requests an audience."
  > "Let him in," said the Emperor.
  The Dragon King entered and bowed.
  > "Your Majesty, I have a matter to report. Sun Wukong came to my palace and demanded I give him a weapon. He was very rude."
  Another messenger came in.
  > "The Judge of the Dead wishes to see you, Your Majesty."
  The Judge entered and bowed.
  > "There is a monkey named Sun Wukong who crossed out his name in the directory. I beg you to punish him."
  The Jade Emperor rubbed his chin.
  > "Who is this Sun Wukong? And how did he become so powerful?"
  The Emperor's captain stepped forward.
  > "Sun Wukong is the monkey who was born from a rock 300 years ago. I don't know how he became so powerful."
  > "I remember him now," said the Jade Emperor. "I don't care how powerful he is. He has caused too much trouble on earth. Gather troops and go down to the Fruit and Flower Mountain. I want Sun Wukong arrested."

## ②

  The spirit of Venus stepped forward.
  > "Your Majesty," said Venus. "Perhaps we could give Wukong a position here in heaven."
  The Jade Emperor raised his eyebrows.
  > "Why would we do that?"
  > "If he's here, we can keep an eye on him," said Venus. "It will be easy to keep him out of trouble."
  > "Very well," said the Emperor. "Invite Wukong to come and live in heaven. He will be put in charge of the royal peach garden."

## ③

  Wukong was telling his subjects about his adventure in the Land of Darkness.
  > "After I crossed out my name," he said, "I crossed out all of your names too. We will all live forever."
  The monkeys and apes cheered.
  When Wukong returned, he saw a spirit standing nearby. He pulled out his iron bar.
  > "Who are you?" he demanded.
  > "I am Venus," said the spirit. "I have an invitation from the Jade Emperor. He would like you to come and live in heaven."
  > "Wukong smiled. 'At last he has invited me!'"
  When Wukong arrived at the royal court in the Cloud Palace, he did not bow.
  > "You're supposed to bow before the Emperor," Venus whispered.

## ④

  > "I'm just as powerful as he is. Maybe he should bow before me."
  Everyone but the Jade Emperor gasped. Wukong looked at the Emperor.
  > "I'm glad you invited me. I guess you finally realized I should be living up here with all of you."
  > "Show some respect," said Venus quietly.
  The Jade Emperor looked at Venus and smiled.
  > "Wukong, I know you. He can be excused for not acting the right way," he said, looking at Wukong. "I am putting you in charge of the royal peach garden. You will have a home here in heaven. I hope you won't cause any more trouble."

## ⑤

  > "That sounds perfect," said Wukong.
  The next morning, a gardener showed Wukong the peach garden. Bright, glowing peaches hung from every branch.
  > "The peaches in this garden are not like peaches on earth," said the gardener. "These peaches take thousands of years to become ripe. Anyone who takes a bite of one will live forever."
  Each morning Wukong inspected the trees in the garden. He took his job very seriously.
  > "These peaches look delicious," he thought. He looked at the tree. He looked around. None of the gardeners were in sight.
  > "Maybe I'll eat one."

# 🇨🇳 第八章：天庭封官

## ①

  玉皇大帝正坐在云宫宝座上。一位使臣气喘吁吁跑进大殿。
  > "陛下，"他禀报道，"东海龙王求见。"
  > "让他进来，"皇帝说道。
  龙王进宫，躬身行礼。
  > "陛下，臣有一事禀告。孙悟空跑到我龙宫，强索兵器，态度极其无礼。"
  又来了一位使臣。
  > "阎君求见陛下。"
  阎君进来，鞠躬行礼。
  > "有一只名叫孙悟空的猴子，在生死簿上勾掉了自己的名字。恳请陛下治他的罪。"
  玉皇大帝抚摸下巴。
  > "这孙悟空究竟是何方神圣？竟有如此神通？"
  皇帝的将领站出来。
  > "孙悟空是三百年前从石头里诞生的那只猴子。我不知道他如何获得如此法力。"
  > "我想起来了，"玉帝说，"不管他有多厉害，他在人间惹是生非太多。集合天兵，下到花果山，把孙悟空给我抓来。"

## ②

  金星的仙灵走上前来。
  > "陛下，"金星说道，"也许我们可以给悟空在天庭安排一个职位。"
  玉皇大帝扬起眉毛。
  > "我们为何要这么做？"
  > "如果他在这里，我们可以就近监视他，"金星说，"这样容易防止他生事。"
  > "好，"玉帝道，"邀请悟空来天庭居住。就让他管理王母的蟠桃园。"

## ③

  悟空正在向子民们讲述他在地府的经历。
  > "我勾掉自己的名字后，"他说，"也把你们所有人的名字都勾掉了。我们大家都能长生不死了。"
  猴子、猿猴们欢呼雀跃。
  悟空返回来时，看见附近站着一位仙灵。他抽出铁棒。
  > "你是谁？"
  > "我是金星，"仙灵答道，"我受玉帝之邀，来请你上天庭居住。"
  > "悟空笑道，'他终于邀请我了！'"
  悟空来到云宫大殿时，并未行礼。
  > "见到陛下理应行礼，"金星小声提醒。

## ④

  > "我的法力与他相当，说不定他该向我行礼才是。"
  除玉帝外，所有人都倒吸一口凉气。悟空看着皇帝。
  > "很高兴你能邀请我。我想你终于意识到我该和你们一起住在天上了。"
  > "放尊重些，"金星低声说道。
  玉帝看了看金星，微微一笑。
  > "悟空，我了解你。他不懂规矩，姑且原谅。不过，"玉帝看着悟空，"我委派你管理蟠桃园。你在天庭已有居所。希望你不要再生事端。"

## ⑤

  > "听起来很不错，"悟空答道。
  第二天一早，一位园丁带悟空参观蟠桃园。光芒闪闪的桃子挂满枝头。
  > "这院子里的桃子与凡间不同，"园丁说，"这些桃子要经过千年方能成熟。任何人只要咬一口，就能长生不老。"
  悟空每天早上都要巡视园中桃树。他工作非常认真。
  > "这些桃子看起来真好吃，"他想。他看了看桃树，又环顾四周，不见园丁踪影。
  > "也许我可以尝一个。"
