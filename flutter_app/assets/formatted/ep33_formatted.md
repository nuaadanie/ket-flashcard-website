# Journey to the West · Chapter Thirty-Three: Bajie Takes a Nap

## ①

  After the Tang Monk sent Wukong away, he and the others continued west. Later that day they stopped to rest. Bajie sat down in the grass next to the road and let out a great yawn.

> "Why don't you go find us some food?" said the Tang Monk. "Wu Jing and I will wait here."

  The pig moaned.

> "I just sat down," he said.

  He got back up.

> "This journey is too difficult," he said.

  Then he wandered off.

## ②

  After a few minutes of walking, Bajie looked around. He was all alone.

> "I'm too tired to look for food," he said to himself. "But if I go back now, Master will be angry. I'll take a nap. When I wake up, I'll go back to Master. I'll tell him I searched everywhere but couldn't find any food."

  The pig lay down behind some bushes. Soon he was snoring loudly.

## ③

  An hour later, the Tang Monk and Wu Jing were still waiting by the road.

> "What is taking Bajie so long?" asked the monk.

  Wu Jing looked annoyed.

> "He probably found food and is now eating it all himself. I'll go and look for him."

  Wu Jing got up and headed down the road. Suddenly, he heard a voice from ahead.

> "Yummy noodles," said a voice.

  Wu Jing frowned.

> "That's Bajie. He did find food and he's not sharing it with us."

## ④

  Meanwhile, the Tang Monk became bored. He stood up to stretch his legs and looked around.

> "This is a beautiful forest," he said to himself. "I'll take a short walk."

  The monk walked quietly through the woods, admiring flowers and plants as he went. He went deeper and deeper into the forest until, in the midst of all the trees, he saw a pagoda.

> "This is a strange place for a pagoda," he thought. "Maybe we can all stay here tonight."

  The Tang Monk went to the door of the pagoda and opened it. He gasped. A monster was sleeping inside. The monster had a purple beard and long sharp fangs. The Tang Monk tried to walk away, but the monster woke up.

## ⑤

> "Who was there?" called the monster. He stood up.

  The Tang Monk turned around, shaking with fear.

> "I didn't mean to wake you," he said. "I..."

  The monster came closer and smiled.

> "You're a monk," he said.

  He licked his lips.

> "Yes," said the Tang Monk. "I'm from the Tang Empire. I'm traveling..."

  The monster's eyes lit up.

> "You're the Tang Monk!"

## ⑥

  Meanwhile, Wu Jing stomped toward Bajie's voice. He peered behind some bushes and saw the pig fast asleep.

> "...And I have more tofu," mumbled Bajie in his sleep. "Yes, please cook lots more. I will eat it all."

  Wu Jing kicked the pig.

> "Wake up, Bajie!"

  Bajie jumped to his feet, wiping drool from the side of his mouth.

> "How dare you sleep while we wait for you," said Wu Jing. "Now let's go back to Master."

  The pig sighed. With his head hung low, he followed Wu Jing back to the road. But when they got there, the Tang Monk was gone.

## ⑦

> "Maybe he went for a walk," said Wu Jing.

  They wandered through the woods looking for the monk. Soon they saw the pagoda. They knocked on the door and the monster answered.

> "Who are you?" asked the monster.

  Wu Jing told the monster who they were.

> "We're looking for a monk."

  The monster laughed.

> "I have a monk here. I'm going to eat him for dinner. I'm going to eat him!"

# 🇨🇳 第三十三章：八戒贪睡


## ①

  唐僧赶走悟空后，就和徒弟们继续向西走去。那天晚些时候，他们停下来休息。八戒坐在路边的草地上，打了个大哈欠。

> "你去给我们找点吃的吧？"唐僧说，"悟净和我在这里等你。"

  猪头哼哼唧唧地抱怨着。

> "我刚坐下来，"他说。

  他又站了起来。

> "这趟路也太难走了，"他说。

  然后他独自走开了。

## ②

  走了几分钟后，八戒环顾四周，发现只有他一个人。

> "我太累了，走不动找吃的，"他自言自语，"可要是现在回去，师父肯定要生气。我还是睡一觉吧。等醒了再回去，就跟师父说我到处都找过了，可什么吃的也没找到。"

  八戒躺在一丛灌木后面，很快就打起了呼噜。

## ③

  一个小时过去了，唐僧和悟净还在路边等着。

> "八戒怎么去了这么久？"唐僧问道。

  悟净一脸不悦。

> "他八成是找到吃的，正一个人全吃光呢。我去找他。"

  悟净站起身，沿着路走去。突然，他听到前方传来一个声音。

> "好吃的面条啊，"一个声音说。

  悟净皱起眉头。

> "是八戒。他找到吃的了，却不分给我们。"

## ④

  与此同时，唐僧觉得无聊。他站起来活动活动腿脚，环顾四周。

> "这片树林真美，"他自言自语，"我去走走。"

  唐僧静静地在树林中走着，欣赏着花草树木。他越走越深，直到在树木之间看到了一座塔。

> "这地方怎么会有座塔？"他想，"也许我们今晚都能住在这里。"

  唐僧走到塔门前，推开了门。他倒吸一口冷气——里面竟然睡着一个妖怪！那妖怪长着紫色的胡子和尖利的长牙。唐僧转身想走，可妖怪已经醒了。

## ⑤

> "是谁在外面？"妖怪叫道。他站了起来。

  唐僧转过身，吓得浑身发抖。

> "我不是故意吵醒您的，"他说，"我......"

  妖怪走近了一些，露出笑容。

> "你是个和尚啊，"他说。

  他舔了舔嘴唇。

> "是的，"唐僧说，"我是大唐来的僧人，我......"

  妖怪的眼睛一亮。

> "你就是唐僧！"

## ⑥

  与此同时，悟净循着声音走去。他拨开灌木丛一看，只见八戒正呼呼大睡。

> "......再来碗豆腐，"八戒梦里嘟囔着，"对，多煮点，我全包了。"

  悟净踢了他一脚。

> "快起来，八戒！"

  八戒跳了起来，抹了抹嘴角的口水。

> "你怎么敢睡大觉，让我们等你？"悟净说，"赶紧回师父那里去。"

  八戒叹了口气，垂头丧气地跟着悟净回到路上。可是到了那里一看——唐僧不见了。

## ⑦

> "也许他去散步了，"悟净说。

  两人在树林中四处寻找唐僧。不久，他们看到了那座塔。他们敲了敲门，妖怪来应门。

> "你们是谁？"妖怪问道。

  悟津告诉妖怪他们是谁。

> "我们在找一个和尚。"

  妖怪哈哈大笑。

> "我这里倒是有一个和尚，我正准备拿他当晚饭吃掉呢！我要吃掉他！"
