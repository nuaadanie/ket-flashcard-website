# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred and Eight: Rewards

## ①

  The travelers flew through the sky over the Tang Empire. Tears of happiness rolled down the Tang monk's cheeks.

> "I see the Emperor's palace," said the monk.

  "I will wait here while you all deliver the true scriptures," said the guard.

  The Tang monk and his companions went down to earth and entered the palace.

## ②

  The Emperor's eyes lit up.

> "My brother!"

  He embraced the monk.

> "For many years I wondered if I'd ever see you again. Did you get the true scriptures?"

> "Yes," said the monk. "But please forgive me. The journey took much longer than I expected."

> "There's no need to apologize," said the Emperor.

## ③

  Then he noticed the monk's companions.

> "Who are they?"

  Sun Wukong stepped forward.

> "I am Sun Wukong, the Monkey King. I once defeated Heaven's entire army."

> "And I am Zhu Bajie," said the pig.

> "My name is Sha Wujing," said the purple spirit.

## ④

> "They protected me during the journey," the monk explained. "I couldn't have reached the Western Paradise without them."

  The Emperor bowed to Wukong and the others.

> "You don't need a bow," the monkey laughed. "We were happy to help."

  "Well, Wujing and I were," Bajie complained with a snort. "There were a lot of battles that night."

## ⑤

  The pig snorted. The Emperor asked the monk many questions about the journey. He listened in amazement to the monk's stories.

> "I will build a magnificent pagoda," the Emperor declared. "The true scriptures will be kept safe inside it."

  He put his hand on the monk's shoulder.

> "You will always be remembered as the brave monk who traveled to the West. Because of you, our people won't have to suffer anymore."

## ⑥

  After the feast was eaten, the Emperor asked the monk to read from the scriptures. The Tang monk smiled.

> "I would be happy to read from them."

## ⑦

  The monk sat on a platform where the scriptures had been placed in baskets. He unrolled a scroll. Suddenly, light filled the room. The Tang monk and his companions rose into the sky.

> "We should return to the Thunderclap Monastery," said the guard. "Buddha is waiting for you."

> "Yes, let's go," said the monk.

## ⑧

  The travelers were soon bowing before Buddha again. Other Buddhas and bodhisattvas stood behind him. Buddha addressed the horse first.

> "You carried the Tang monk on the journey and earned forgiveness for your crimes. You will now be a golden dragon in heaven."

  Light flashed as a large golden dragon soared into the sky.

## ⑨

> "Sha Wujing," said Buddha. "You too have earned forgiveness for your past crimes. You will now be a golden deity."

  Wujing's body turned gold. The spirit bowed.

> "Thank you, Buddha."

> "You have earned forgiveness, Bajie," said Buddha. "You will be an altar cleaner, an important deity who looks after Buddhist altars."

> "Thank you, Buddha," Bajie said.

## ⑩

  Buddha next turned to the Tang monk.

> "Because of you, the people in the East will never have to suffer again. You made a journey that no other person could make. You are now a Buddha."

  Light glowed around the monk, who bowed before Buddha.

> "Thank you, Buddha," said the monk.

## ⑪

  Finally Buddha turned to the monkey.

> "And you, Wukong," He began. "Your crimes long ago were terrible. But since then you have used your tremendous powers to do good deeds. You too are now a Buddha."

  Wukong bowed.

> "Thank you, Buddha."

## ⑫

  *Hey!* The monkey suddenly noticed that his head felt different. The headband was gone. Buddha smiled.

> "You don't need it anymore."

  Filled with joy, Wukong and the Tang monk joined the other Buddhas and bodhisattvas in the West. They have been in the Western Paradise ever since.

# 🇨🇳 第一百零八章：封号

## ①

  旅人们飞越大唐帝国的上空，唐僧激动得热泪盈眶。

> "我看到皇帝的宫殿了，"唐僧说。

> "我在这里等着，你们去送真经吧，"护法说。

  唐僧和徒伴们降落到地面，走进了皇宫。

## ②

  皇帝的双眼亮了起来。

> "御弟！"

  他拥抱了唐僧。

> "这么多年了，我一直在想还能不能再见到你。你取到真经了吗？"

> "取到了，"唐僧说，"但请陛下原谅我。这趟旅程比我预想的长了很多。"

> "不必道歉，"皇帝说。

## ③

  然后他注意到了唐僧身边的三位徒伴。

> "他们是谁？"

  孙悟空走上前来。

> "我是孙悟空，齐天大圣。我当年曾打败天庭千军万马。"

> "我是猪八戒。"八戒说。

> "我叫沙悟净。"悟净说。

## ④

> "这一路上他们保护了我，"唐僧解释道，"没有他们，我到不了西天。"

  皇帝向悟空等人鞠躬。

> "陛下不必行此大礼，"猴子笑道，"能帮上忙，我们很高兴。"

> "哼，悟净和我是很高兴。"八戒嘟囔了一声，"可那些打打杀杀的差事，我可吃了不少苦。"

## ⑤

  八戒哼了一声。皇帝问了唐僧许多关于旅途的问题，听他讲述一路上的故事，惊叹不已。

> "我要修建一座宏伟的宝塔，"皇帝宣布道，"真经将安放在塔中保存。"

  他把手搭在唐僧肩上。

> "你将永远被人们铭记，因为你是一位前往西方取经的英勇僧人。因为有您，我们的百姓再也不必受苦了。"

## ⑥

  盛宴之后，皇帝请唐僧诵读经书。唐僧微笑。

> "我愿意为大家诵读。"

## ⑦

  唐僧坐在讲台上，面前是经卷筐。他展开了一卷经书。突然，光芒充满了整间大殿。唐僧和他的徒伴们升上了天空。

> "我们应该回雷音寺了，"护法说，"佛祖在等着你们受封。"

> "好，走吧，"唐僧说。

## ⑧

  唐僧师徒又跪拜在了佛祖面前。佛祖身后站着众佛和菩萨。佛祖先对白龙马说道：

> "你一路上驮着唐僧取经，为你的罪过赎了罪。如今你将为天庭的一条金龙。"

  金光一闪，一条巨大的金龙飞上了天空。

## ⑨

> "沙悟净，"佛祖说，"你也为过去的罪过赎了罪。你将为天庭的南无八宝金身罗汉菩萨。"

  悟净的全身变得金光闪闪。他行了一个大礼。

> "感谢佛祖。"

> "猪八戒，你也赎了罪，"佛祖说，"你将为净坛使者，负责打扫和守护佛前供品。"

> "感谢佛祖。"八戒说。

## ⑩

  佛祖接下来转向唐僧。

> "因为有您，东土百姓再也不必受苦了。您走了一段无人能及的旅程。您现在是旃檀功德佛。"

  金光照亮了唐僧。他在佛祖面前行礼。

> "感谢佛祖。"唐僧说。

## ⑪

  最后佛祖转向了猴子。

> "悟空，"佛祖说，"你当年的罪行极为严重。但从此之后你用你莫大的法力做了无数善事。你现在是斗战胜佛。"

  悟空行了一个大礼。

> "感谢佛祖。"

## ⑫

  *咦！*猴子突然感觉到头上不一样了。头上的紧箍圈不见了。佛祖微笑了。

> "你不再需要它了。"

  满怀着喜悦，悟空和唐僧与其他佛和菩萨一起留在了西天。从此以后，他们便一直在西天极乐世界。
