# Journey to the West · Chapter Seventy-Two: A Surprise at the Fruit and Flower Mountain

## ①

  Wukong went straight to Mount Potalaka. The Bodhisattva Guanyin was in her garden.

> "Fala Wukong. How was the journey going?"

  Wukong threw up his hands.

> "The Tang monk is crazy."

> "Why?" asked the Bodhisattva.

> "He told me to leave," said the monkey. "He was mad because I was going to hurt some robbers."

## ②

  Guanyin frowned.

> "It's wrong to hurt people, Wukong. You know that."

> "I hurt demons all the time," said Wukong.

> "That's different," said Guanyin. "Demons are evil, and you're doing a good thing when you get rid of them. But you must not hurt people."

## ③

  The monkey sat down and put his head in his hands.

> "Dear Bodhisattva, you're right."

  He started to cry.

> "This is terrible. Now I'll never reach the Western Paradise with the Tang monk. I wanted to earn forgiveness."

> "Don't cry, Wukong," said Guanyin. "You will still help the Tang monk reach the Western Paradise. He will seek your help soon. In the meantime, you may stay here with me."

## ④

  Bajie dropped the bags.

> "Let's rest here," he said. "I'm starving."

  The Tang monk climbed down from his horse and stretched. The horse drank from a stream while Bajie looked in a bag.

> "No food," said the pig.

> "We'll have to beg for food somewhere," said Wujing. He looked at the monk. "Master, you wait here while Bajie and I find a village."

## ⑤

  The monk sat down in the grass next to the road and closed his eyes. He had nearly dozed off when he heard a voice.

> "Master!"

  It was Wukong. The Tang monk glared at the monkey.

> "Wukong! Why are you here?"

  The monkey fell to his knees.

> "I'm begging you, please let me join you again!"

## ⑥

> "No," said the monk. "You're still evil, Wukong. You can't go to the Western Paradise with me. Now leave, before I recite the tight headband spell."

  The monkey was furious. He pulled out his iron bar, which quickly grew. Before the monk could recite the spell, Wukong struck him and knocked him out. Quickly the monkey grabbed the bags and then vanished.

## ⑦

  Wujing and Bajie soon returned. They found the Tang monk sitting by the road, rubbing his head.

> "Master!" cried Wujing. "What happened? Where are the bags?"

  Bajie looked around.

> "Did Wukong return?"

  Said the Tang monk.

> "He begged me to let him join us again. I said no, and then he attacked me."

## ⑧

> "That's terrible," said Wujing. "That rotten monkey!"

  Bajie stamped his foot.

> "We can't let him get away with this. He probably went home to the Fruit and Flower Mountain. I'll go there and deal with him," said Wujing.

  Bajie gave Wujing directions to the Fruit and Flower Mountain.

## ⑨

  When Wujing arrived, he found Wukong near the waterfall.

> "Wukong!" shouted Wujing. "Why did you attack Master?"

  The monkey looked at Wujing blankly.

> "Who are you?"

  Wujing raised an eyebrow.

> "What are you talking about? You know who I am."

## ⑩

> "No, I don't," said Wukong.

> "I'm Sha Wujing. We were traveling to the west together."

> "I'm going to the west but not with you," said Wukong. He pointed to a group of figures. "I'm going with them."

  Wujing looked over. A monk who looked exactly like the Tang monk sat on a horse. A pig stood next to the horse.

## ⑪

> "Bajie?" said Wujing.

  Just then he saw a purple figure walk over to the horse. Wujing gasped in shock.

> "Wujing?"

  The purple figure was an exact copy of himself.

# 🇨🇳 第七十二章：花果山惊变

## ①

  悟空直奔普陀山。观音菩萨正在花园里。

> "悟净，这一路怎么样？"

  悟空举起双手，无奈地说：

> "唐僧疯了。"

> "怎么回事？"菩萨问。

> "他让我走人，"猴子说，"就因为我要收拾几个强盗，他生气了。"

## ②

  观音皱起眉头。

> "悟空，伤害人是不对的，这你知道。"

> "我杀的可是妖怪啊。"悟空说。

> "那不一样，"观音说，"妖怪作恶，你除掉它们是做好事。但你绝不能伤害人。"

## ③

  猴子坐下来，双手抱头。

> "菩萨，您说得对。"

  他哭了起来。

> "这下糟了，我再也到不了西天了，也再也不能陪唐僧取经了。我想求得原谅的。"

> "别哭了，悟空，"观音说，"你还是会帮助唐僧到达西天的，他很快就会来请你帮忙。在此期间，你就住在我这里吧。"

## ④

  八戒放下了行李袋。

> "我们歇一会儿吧，"他说，"我饿坏了。"

  唐僧从马上下来，伸了伸懒腰。白龙马喝着溪中的水，八戒翻找着行囊。

> "没吃的了，"八戒说。

> "我们得去找个村子化点斋饭，"悟净看了看唐僧说，"师父，您在这里等着，我和八戒去找村子。"

## ⑤

  唐僧在路边的草地上坐了下来，闭上眼睛。他正要打盹，忽然听到一个声音。

> "师父！"

  是悟空。唐僧瞪着猴子。

> "悟空！你怎么来了？"

  猴子扑通跪倒在地。

> "求求师父，重新让我跟你们走吧！"

## ⑥

> "不行，"唐僧说，"悟空，你恶性难改。你不能跟我去西天了。快走吧，否则我就念紧箍咒了。"

  悟空怒火中烧。他掏出金箍棒，迎风一晃便变长了。趁唐僧还没来得及念咒，悟空一棒打去，将唐僧打晕在地，然后一把抢走行囊，消失得无影无踪。

## ⑦

  悟净和八戒很快回来了。他们看到唐僧坐在路边揉着头。

> "师父！"悟净惊叫道，"出什么事了？行李呢？"

  八戒环顾四周。

> "悟空回来了？"

  唐僧说：

> "他求我收留他，我拒绝了，他就打晕了我。"

## ⑧

> "太过分了，"悟净说，"这遭瘟的猴子！"

  八戒跺了跺脚。

> "不能让他这么跑了。他多半回老家花果山去了。我去花果山收拾他，"悟净说。

  八戒给悟净指了去花果山的路。

## ⑨

  悟净来到花果山，看见悟空站在瀑布前。

> "悟空！"悟净大喊，"你为什么要打师父？"

  猴子茫然地看着悟净。

> "你是谁？"

  悟净挑了挑眉毛。

> "你说什么？你认识我的。"

## ⑩

> "不，不认识。"悟空说。

> "我是沙悟净，我们一起去西天取经的。"

> "我是要去西天，但不是跟你一起，"悟空指着远处的一队人说，"我是跟他们走。"

  悟净放眼望去，一个和唐僧长得一模一样的和尚骑着马，旁边还站着一头猪。

## ⑪

> "八戒？"悟净惊诧道。

  就在这时，一个紫色的身影朝马走去。悟净倒吸一口凉气。

> "悟净？"

  那紫色的身影竟然是自己的翻版。
