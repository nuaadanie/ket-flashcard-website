# Journey to the West · Chapter Seventy-Three: Two Wukongs

## ①

  Wujing watched as the fake Wujing fed the horse some hay. The purple spirit spoke.

> "I don't know how you made fake versions of Wukong, Bajie, and the Tang monk, but you won't get the true scriptures without the real Wukong."

  Wukong folded his arms.

> "Oh yes I will."

  Wujing leaped into the air and flew south.

> "I'll tell the Bodhisattva what Wukong is doing," he thought. "She can stop him."

## ②

  When Wujing arrived at Mount Potalaka, he was even more shocked. Wukong was meditating in the Bodhisattva's garden.

> "Wukong!" cried Wujing. "How did you get here so fast?"

> "What are you talking about?" asked the monkey.

## ③

  Guanyin came out of her pagoda.

> "Wujing, what are you doing here?"

  The purple spirit bowed.

> "Bodhisattva, I must speak with you. Wukong has made fake versions of me, Bajie, and the Tang monk. He's planning to make the journey without us."

> "What?" shouted Wukong.

## ④

  Wujing glanced at the monkey.

> "I just saw him on the Fruit and Flower Mountain. I don't know how he got here before me."

> "You're lying!" yelled Wukong.

  Guanyin smiled.

> "You're mistaken, Wujing. Wukong has been here the whole time."

  Wujing was surprised.

> "He has?"

  Guanyin nodded.

## ⑤

  Wukong made a fist.

> "Someone is pretending to be me, and he's living on my mountain. I'm going there right now. I'm going to destroy him."

> "I'll go with you," said Wujing.

  Wukong and Wujing soon arrived on the Fruit and Flower Mountain. The monkey's eyes burned with anger when he saw the fake Wukong.

> "Who are you?" demanded Wukong.

> "Who are you?" asked the fake Wukong.

## ⑥

> "I am Sun Wukong, King of the Fruit and Flower Mountain!"

> "No," said the other monkey. "I am Sun Wukong."

  The fake Wukong pulled out his iron bar and swung. The other Wukong blocked the bar and swung back.

> "Wujing, help me!" cried one Wukong.

  Wujing raised his spear and was about to enter the fight.

## ⑦

  The other Wukong shouted.

> "No! Wujing, help me!"

  Wujing looked from one Wukong to the other.

> "Who is the real Wukong?"

> "I am!" shouted one Wukong as he struck at the other.

> "No!" shouted the other Wukong, blocking the first one's attack. "I am!"

> "Don't listen to him!" cried one monkey.

> "Don't listen to him!" cried the other.

## ⑧

  Wujing stamped his foot.

> "Stop fighting!" he shouted.

  Instantly both monkeys stopped fighting. They looked at the purple spirit.

> "I don't know which one of you is the real Wukong! Go to Mount Potalaka. Guanyin will be able to tell who is who. I will return to the Tang monk to tell him what's going on."

## ⑨

  The two Wukongs glared at each other.

> "That's a good idea," said one of them. "Guanyin will easily see that I am the real Wukong."

> "It is a good idea," the other agreed. "Guanyin will easily see that I am the real Wukong."

  Soon the two Wukongs stood before Guanyin. She looked carefully at each one.

> "I can't tell which one of you is the real Wukong," she said.

## ⑩

  One Wukong took a deep breath.

> "Use the tight headband spell. The spell will work only on my headband -- the real headband."

> "Yes," said the other one. "Use the tight headband spell. The spell will only work with my headband -- the real headband."

  Guanyin recited the tight headband spell. Both monkeys fell to the ground, howling in pain.

> "Stop the spell!" they cried.

  Guanyin stopped reciting, and the two monkeys stood back up.

## ⑪

> "I still don't know which of you is the real Wukong," the Bodhisattva said. "You should both go to Heaven. The real Wukong used to live there. Maybe the Jade Emperor can tell who is who."

> "Good idea," said one Wukong. He pointed at the other. "The Jade Emperor will see that this monkey is a fake."

> "I'm not a fake!" cried the other monkey. "You are!"

  He tried to strike the other Wukong, but that one dodged the blow and swung back.

# 🇨🇳 第七十三章：真假悟空

## ①

  悟净眼睁睁看着那个假悟净在给马喂草。紫色的身影对他说：

> "我不知道你怎么变出了悟空、八戒和唐僧的假身，但没有真的悟空，你们取不到真经。"

  悟空抱起双臂。

> "哦，我偏要。"

  悟净纵身一跃，向南飞去。

> "我要去告诉菩萨悟空在做什么，"他想，"她能制止他。"

## ②

  悟净来到普陀山，眼前的景象让他更加震惊。悟空竟然坐在菩萨的花园里打坐。

> "悟空！"悟净惊叫道，"你怎么这么快就来了？"

> "你说什么呢？"猴子问道。

## ③

  观音从宝塔里走出来。

> "悟净，你怎么来了？"

  紫色的身影向菩萨行礼。

> "菩萨，我必须跟您说一件事。悟空变出了我、八戒和唐僧的假身，他要甩开我们自己去取经。"

> "什么？"悟空大喝一声。

## ④

  悟净瞥了猴子一眼。

> "我明明在花果山看到他的，不知道他怎么赶在我之前到这里了。"

> "你撒谎！"悟空大喊。

  观音微微一笑。

> "悟净，你弄错了。悟空一直在这里。"

  悟净大吃一惊。

> "他一直在这里？"

  观音点了点头。

## ⑤

  悟空紧握拳头。

> "有人假冒我，还占了我的花果山。我现在就要回去收拾他。"

> "我跟你一起去。"悟净说。

  悟空和悟净很快来到了花果山。看到那个假悟空时，悟空怒目圆睁。

> "你是谁？"悟空厉声喝道。

> "你是谁？"假悟空反问。

## ⑥

> "我是孙悟空，花果山美猴王！"

> "不对，"另一个猴子说，"我才是孙悟空。"

  假悟空掏出铁棒就打。真悟空举棒格挡，又反击回来。

> "悟净，快来帮我！"一个悟空喊道。

  悟净举起降妖杖，正要加入战斗。

## ⑦

  另一个悟空大叫道：

> "不！悟净，帮我！"

  悟净左看看右看看，分不清谁是真的。

> "到底谁才是真的悟空？"

> "我！"一个悟空边打边喊。

> "不对！"另一个悟空挡住攻击，"我才是！别听他的！"一个猴子叫道。

> "别听他的！"另一个也喊道。

## ⑧

  悟净一顿脚。

> "别打了！"他大喊道。

  两个猴子立刻停手，齐刷刷看向悟净。

> "我不知道你们谁是真的！去普陀山找观音菩萨吧，她一定分辨得出来。我回去禀报师父。"

## ⑨

  两个悟空怒目相视。

> "好主意，"其中一个说，"观音菩萨一眼就能看出我才是真的悟空。"

> "好主意，"另一个也表示同意，"观音菩萨一看就知道我才是真的。"

  两个悟空很快来到观音面前。菩萨仔细端详了两人。

> "我也分不出你们谁是真的。"

## ⑩

  一个悟空深吸一口气。

> "念紧箍咒吧。紧箍咒只对真的金箍起作用。"

> "对，念紧箍咒，"另一个也说，"咒只对真的金箍有效。"

  观音念起紧箍咒，两个猴子同时倒地，痛得直叫。

> "别念了！"他们齐声叫道。

  观音停下咒语，两个猴子又站了起来。

## ⑪

> "我还是分辨不出谁是真的，"菩萨说，"你们去天庭吧。玉帝或许能分辨出来。"

> "好主意，"一个悟空说，指着另一个，"玉帝一看就知道这个猴子是假的。"

> "我不是假的！"另一个猴子叫道，"你才是！"

  他挥棒打向另一个悟空，对方闪身躲过，又反击回来。
