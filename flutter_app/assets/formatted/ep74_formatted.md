# Journey to the West · Chapter Seventy-Four: Curious Ears

## ①

  The two monkeys were still fighting when they arrived at the southern gate of Heaven. The Heavenly guard narrowed his eyes.

> "Wukong? Is that you?"

> "Yes, it's me," said both of the Wukongs.

> "I must speak with the Jade Emperor," said one monkey. He pointed at the other. "He's a fake Wukong."

> "I'm not a fake Wukong," said the other monkey. "I'm the real one."

## ②

> "I must speak with the Jade Emperor," said the second monkey.

  The guard allowed both Wukongs to enter Heaven.

  Crash! Bang! The two Wukong fought all over Heaven. The monkeys knocked over statues and trees. Still fighting, they stormed into the Cloud Palace. When the Jade Emperor saw them, he was furious.

> "What's going on?" he boomed.

## ③

> "Your Majesty," said one of the Wukongs. "Please punish this monkey. He's pretending to be me."

> "Punish him!" cried the other. "He's pretending to be me!"

> "Stand still, both of you," said the Jade Emperor. "Let me get a good look at you."

  The two Wukongs stood still as the Jade Emperor looked carefully at them. Finally, the Emperor shrugged.

> "You look exactly alike. I can't tell who is who."

## ④

  One of the Wukongs swung at the other with his iron bar.

> "I'm the real Wukong!"

> "No." The other Wukong blocked the bar and swung back. "I'm the real Wukong!"

> "Both of you leave at once!" thundered the Jade Emperor. "One Wukong is bad enough. I don't want two of you around. Go fight somewhere else!"

  The two monkeys kept fighting as they headed out of the palace.

## ⑤

  The spirit of the planet Venus followed them.

> "Wukong," said the spirit.

  Both monkeys stopped to look at him.

> "Go to the Land of Darkness," said Venus. "Maybe the judge of the dead can tell you apart."

## ⑥

  Soon the two Wukongs were standing before the judge of the dead. The judge shook his head.

> "You look exactly alike!"

> "Check the directory," said one of the Wukongs. "Maybe it will have some information about the fake Sun Wukong."

> "Yes!" said the other Wukong. "Check the directory!"

  The judge nodded and opened the giant book, reading carefully. He turned the pages, studying them. Finally, he closed it.

## ⑦

> "There's no fake Sun Wukong in the directory," said the judge. "I'm sorry, but I have no idea which one of you is the real Wukong."

  A spirit was sitting at a nearby desk.

> "I believe my pet beast, Di Ting (Curious Ears), can help," said the spirit.

  He took a step back, and a small beast crept out from under the desk.

> "Curious Ears can tell the true from the false," said the spirit.

  The beast stared hard at the two Wukongs. Finally, it spoke.

> "I know who the fake Sun Wukong is."

## ⑧

  The two Wukongs stared at Curious Ears.

> "I'm the real Wukong!" shouted one monkey.

> "No, it's me!" shouted the other.

> "I know which of you is the fake Sun Wukong," said Curious Ears. "But I won't say who it is. The fake Sun Wukong has all the same powers as the real one. If I point him out, he'll get angry. He could destroy the entire palace!"

## ⑨

> "It's true," said one Wukong. "I could destroy this whole palace if I wanted to."

> "Do you mean I could destroy it?" said the other Wukong.

> "No!" yelled the other. "I could!"

  The two Wukongs began swinging their iron bars at each other.

> "Please, stop!" cried the judge of the dead. "You'll destroy the whole palace!"

  The monkeys stopped.

## ⑩

> "But I can help you," said Curious Ears. "The Buddha will be able to see which of you is the real Wukong, and he's powerful enough to destroy the fake one."

> "Of course!" cried one Wukong. "Let's go see Buddha."

> "Oh, um, sure," said the other Wukong. He looked worried.

## ⑪

> "You're nervous," said the first Wukong. "That's because you know Buddha is going to see who you really are."

> "No!" shouted the other Wukong. He swung his iron bar furiously.

> "Get out!" cried the judge of the dead. "Take your battle somewhere else."

> "Go to the Western Paradise," said Venus.

  The two Wukongs left the Land of Darkness. Clouds swirled and thundered as the two mighty spirits battled across the sky.

## ⑫

  They soon arrived in the Western Paradise. A guard rushed out of the Thunderclap Monastery.

> "Stop fighting immediately!" he called up to them. "Tell me who you are."

  The two Wukongs landed in front of the guard. The guard looked surprised.

> "Wukong! There are two of you."

> "Yes," said one of the Wukongs. "I must speak with Buddha right away."

  The other Wukong scratched his head.

> "If Buddha is busy, we can just come back another time."

  The first Wukong shot a look at the other.

> "No, we can't."

## ⑬

> "You may enter," said the guard. "But you must behave yourselves while inside."

  As the first Wukong started to enter the monastery, the other one backed away.

> "You are coming with me!" said the first Wukong.

  He grabbed the other monkey by the arm and dragged him into the monastery. In front of Buddha, the first Wukong bowed. The other Wukong hesitated a moment and then bowed also.

## ⑭

  Greetings, Buddha. Said the first Wukong. I must ask for your help.

> "I know why you're here, Wukong," said Buddha. He looked at the other monkey. "And I know that you are a fake."

  The other Wukong gasped and then vanished. Buddha held a bowl up in the air and then placed it upside down on a table.

> "I've trapped the fake Wukong under this bowl," said Buddha.

  When Buddha lifted the bowl, a tiny monkey spirit lay on the table.

## ⑮

  Wukong leaned closer to see.

> "He was a monkey who became an evil spirit," said Buddha. "He wanted to be you because you're so powerful."

  Wukong laughed.

> "Many spirits want to be me."

  The monkey thanked Buddha and then flew back to Mount Potalaka.

## ⑯

  Guanyin decided it was time for Wukong to rejoin his companions on the journey. She and the monkey found the Tang monk and the others along the road to the west. The Bodhisattva told the Tang monk that it wasn't Wukong who attacked him.

> "You must keep Wukong with you," explained Guanyin.

> "Yes, Bodhisattva," said the Tang monk, bowing.

## ⑰

> "Thank you, Bodhisattva," said Wukong. "I'll make sure the Tang monk reaches the Western Paradise."

# 🇨🇳 第七十四章：谛听辨真假

## ①

  两个悟空一路打到南天门外。天将眯起眼睛。

> "悟空？是你吗？"

> "是我是我。"两个悟空齐声说。

> "我要见玉帝，"一个猴子说，指着另一个，"他是个冒牌货。"

> "我才是真的，"另一个猴子说。"我才是真悟空。"

## ②

> "我也要见玉帝。"第二个悟空说。

  天将放两个悟空进了天庭。

  哗啦！乒乒乓乓！两个悟空在天庭到处打，推翻了雕像和树木。打着打着，他们闯进了灵霄宝殿。玉帝看到他们，勃然大怒。

> "怎么回事？"他厉声喝道。

## ③

> "陛下，"一个悟空说，"请惩罚这个猴子，他假冒我。"

> "惩罚他！"另一个叫道，"他才是假冒的！"

> "你们两个都别动，"玉帝说，"让我好好看看。"

  两个悟空站定，玉帝仔细端详。最后，玉帝耸了耸肩。

> "你们长得一模一样，我分辨不出来。"

## ④

  一个悟空挥棒打向另一个。

> "我才是真悟空！"

> "不对！"另一个挡住金箍棒反击，"我才是真悟空！"

> "你们两个都给我滚出去！"玉帝雷霆大怒，"一个悟空就够烦的了，我才不要两个。你们去别处打！"

  两个猴子一边打一边出了殿。

## ⑤

  太白金星跟在后面。

> "悟空，"太白金星说。

  两个猴子停下来看他。

> "去地府看看吧，"金星说，"也许阎王能分辨你们。"

## ⑥

  很快两个悟空站在了阎王面前。阎王摇了摇头。

> "你们长得一模一样！"

> "查生死簿，"一个悟空说，"里面可能有关于假孙悟空的信息。"

> "对！查生死簿！"另一个也说。

  阎王点了点头，打开生死簿，仔细地翻看着，最后合上了书。

## ⑦

> "生死簿上没有假孙悟空的记录，"阎王说，"抱歉，我实在分不清你们谁是真的。"

  旁边案上坐着一位天神。

> "我相信我的神兽谛听可以帮忙，"那神仙说。

  他退了一步，一只小兽从桌下爬了出来。

> "谛听能辨别真假，"神仙说。

  神兽仔细盯着两个悟空看了半天。终于开口了。

> "我知道谁是假孙悟空。"

## ⑧

  两个悟空盯着谛听。

> "我才是真悟空！"一个猴子大叫。

> "不，是我！"另一个也喊。

> "我知道谁是假孙悟空，"谛听说，"可我不敢说。假孙悟空跟真的本事一样大，我要是当面指出来，他会发怒，说不定把我的地府全毁了！"

## ⑨

> "没错，"一个悟空说，"我要想拆了这地府易如反掌。"

> "你说谁能拆？"另一个悟空说。

> "我！"另一个大喊。

  两个悟空又挥棒打了起来。

> "别打了，求你们！"阎王大喊道，"会把地府全毁的！"

  两个猴子停下手。

## ⑩

> "不过我可以帮你们，"谛听说，"如来佛祖能分辨你们谁是真悟空，而且法力高强，足以降服假的。"

> "当然！"一个悟空叫道，"我们找佛祖去！"

> "行、好吧，"另一个悟空说，脸上露出一丝担忧。

## ⑪

> "你心虚了，"第一个悟空说，"因为你知道到了佛祖面前你就原形毕露了。"

> "才不是！"另一个悟空大喊，挥棒就打。

> "滚出去！"阎王大怒，"你们去别处打！"

  两个悟空离开了地府，两团云雾翻腾，电闪雷鸣，两个神通广大的妖精在空中激战。

## ⑫

  他们很快到了西天。一个守卫从雷音寺冲出来。

> "立刻住手！"他朝上大喊，"报上名来。"

  两个悟空落在守卫面前。守卫大吃一惊。

> "悟空！怎么有两个你？"

> "是的，"一个悟空说，"我要立刻见佛祖。"

  另一个悟空挠了挠头。

> "佛祖要是忙的话，我们改天再来也行。"

  第一个悟空瞪了他一眼。

> "不行，今天必须见。"

## ⑬

> "你们可以进去，"守卫说，"但在里面不许动手。"

  第一个悟空正要进寺，另一个想溜。

> "你跟我一起进去！"第一个悟空一把抓住他的胳膊，拖着进了雷音寺。在佛祖面前，第一个悟空行礼。另一个犹豫了一下，也施了礼。

## ⑭

> "佛祖在上，"第一个悟空说，"我来求您帮忙。"

> "悟空，我知道你为什么来，"佛祖说，目光转向另一个猴子，"我也知道，你是个假货。"

  另一个悟空倒吸一口凉气，随即消失了。佛祖举起一个钵盂，然后倒扣在桌上。

> "我把假悟空扣在这个钵下面了，"佛祖说。

  佛祖揭开钵盂，桌上一只小猴妖趴在那里。

## ⑮

  悟空凑近来看。

> "他是一只修炼成精的猴子，"佛祖说，"因为你法力高强，他想取而代之。"

  悟空大笑。

> "想取代我的妖精还真不少。"

  悟空谢过佛祖，飞回了普陀山。

## ⑯

  观音觉得是时候让悟空回到取经队伍中了。她和猴子在西行路上找到了唐僧和众人。菩萨告诉唐僧，之前打他的那个并非真悟空。

> "你千万要留住悟空，"观音吩咐道。

> "是，菩萨。"唐僧恭敬地行礼。

## ⑰

> "谢谢菩萨，"悟空说，"我发誓一定保师父到达西天。"
