# Journey to the West · Chapter Three: Subodhi
## ①

  The monkey drifted across the ocean for a long time and finally set foot on a new land. He traveled for many years, visiting towns and meeting many people, but never found a sage. Later he came to a dense forest on a high mountain. He followed a steep mountain trail and soon heard the sound of chopping. A woodcutter was chopping wood.
  > "Hello," called the monkey. "Are there any sages in these mountains?"

## ②

  The woodcutter dropped his axe.
  > "You're a monkey," he said. "And you can talk."
  > "Yes," replied the monkey. "I want to live forever. Do you know where I can find a sage?"
  The woodcutter scratched his head.
  > "Well, yes," he said. "Follow me."

## ③

  > "A sage named Subhuti lives on this mountain," said the woodcutter. "He is very wise."
  Monkey was excited.
  > "Where can I find him?" he asked.
  > "Just follow this trail and you will come to his home," said the woodcutter.
  > "Thank you," said the monkey.
  The monkey thanked the woodcutter and ran up the trail. Soon he saw a temple built into the side of the mountain. Subhuti was sitting with his students in the main room of his temple, meditating.

## ④

  Subhuti looked at his oldest student.
  > "Please go to the door," said Subhuti. "A new student is about to arrive. Let him in."
  The student looked confused.
  > "Okay, master," he said.
  The student got up and went to the door of the temple. He looked outside.
  > "Please come in," he said.

## ⑤

  Monkey stood outside the door and called, "I have heard that the great sage Subhuti is here."
  A moment later, Monkey was standing before the sage.
  > "I want to be your student," said Monkey.
  > "You're just a monkey," said Subhuti. "How can you be my student?"
  > "I may be a monkey," said Monkey, "but I can walk and talk and do many other things."

## ⑥

  > "I traveled almost 13 years to get here. I'm sure I can be your student," said Monkey.
  Subhuti stared at Monkey for a moment.
  > "Who are your parents?" he asked.
  > "I don't have parents," said Monkey. "I was born from a rock."
  > "Interesting," said Subhuti.

## ⑦

  > "You were probably created by heaven and earth. I may accept you as a student, but I need to give you a name," said Subhuti.
  He thought for a moment.
  > "I will call you Sun Wukong."
  For the next seven years, Sun Wukong lived in Subhuti's temple. The monkey spent his days meditating, listening to his teacher's lessons, and doing chores.
  One day, while Subhuti was giving a lesson, Wukong started to dance.
  Subhuti stood up.
  > "How dare you dance while I'm teaching?" he shouted.

## ⑧

  > "Master, please calm your anger," said Wukong. "I was so happy I couldn't help dancing."
  Subhuti's tone softened.
  > "You're happy?" he asked.
  > "Yes," said Wukong. "I love studying with you."
  Subhuti continued:
  > "Wukong, you've been here a long time. What would you like to learn from me?"

## ⑨

  Subhuti asked.
  Wukong shrugged.
  > "I don't know," he said. "You tell me what I should learn."
  > "I can teach you to tell the future," said Subhuti. "Would you like that?"
  > "Maybe," asked Wukong. "Will it make me live forever?"

## ⑩

  > "Of course not," replied Subhuti.
  > "Then forget it," said Wukong. "Teach me something else."
  > "How about some magic breathing exercises?" said Subhuti.
  > "Will they make me live forever?" asked Wukong.
  > "No," said Subhuti.

## ⑪

  > "Then I'm not interested," said Wukong.
  Subhuti flew into a rage. He stood up and knocked Wukong on the head three times, then stormed out of the room.
  > "Wukong," said another student, "you are very rude to Master."
  The other students all agreed.

## ⑫

  Wukong, however, smiled. None of the other students knew why.
  > "Let's all slowly reveal our true forms," said Wukong.
  Another student chimed in, "Alright, let's show our forms."
  Yet another student said, "Okay."
  Afterwards, each displayed their abilities, cheering together.

# 🇨🇳 第三章：菩提祖师

## ①

  猴子在海上漂泊了很久，终于踏上一片新大陆。他走了许多年，游历城镇，遇见许多人，却始终未遇仙人。后来他来到茂林深山，沿着陡峭山路前行，不久便听见砍柴声。一位樵夫正在砍柴。
  > "你好，"猴子叫道，"这山里可有什么仙人？"

## ②

  樵夫放下斧头。
  > "你是一只猴子，"他说，"而且你会说话。"
  > "是的，"猴子回答，"我想长生不老。你知道哪里能找到仙人吗？"
  樵夫挠了挠头。
  > "嗯，有。跟我来。"

## ③

  > "这山上有位仙人叫须菩提，非常高明。"
  猴子兴奋地问：
  > "他在哪儿？"
  > "沿着这条路走，就能到他住处，"樵夫说。
  > "谢谢。"猴子道谢后沿路跑去。
  不久，见一座寺庙嵌在山腰上。须菩提正与弟子们在寺中打坐。

## ④

  须菩提看向年长的弟子。
  > "去开门，"须菩提说，"有新弟子到了，请进。"
  弟子一脸茫然。
  > "师父，好的。"
  弟子起身走到寺门前，探望一番后说："请进。"

## ⑤

  猴子在门外喊道："晚辈听说须菩提大仙在此。"
  片刻，猴子便站到仙人面前。
  > "我想做您的弟子，"猴子说。
  > "你只是一只猴，"须菩提道，"如何能做我的弟子？"
  > "我虽是猴，却能走、能言，还会许多伎俩。"

## ⑥

  > "我走了将近十三年才到这儿，一定能成为好弟子。"
  须菩提盯着猴子瞧了一会儿。
  > "你父母是谁？"他问。
  > "我没有父母，"猴子说，"我是从石头里蹦出来的。"
  > "有趣。"

## ⑦

  须菩提说道："你应是天地所生。我可收你为徒，不过得给你取个名字。"
  他想片刻。
  > "你就叫孙悟空吧。"
  此后七年，孙悟空住在须菩提的寺庙里，每日打坐、听课、劳作。一日，须菩提讲经时，悟空高兴得手舞足蹈。须菩提起身怒喝：
  > "你怎敢在我讲学时跳舞？"

## ⑧

  > "师父息怒，"悟空忙说，"弟子太高兴，忍不住舞了起来。"
  须菩提语气稍缓。
  > "你很开心？"他问。
  > "是的，"悟空道，"弟子爱极了学习。"
  须菩提又道：
  > "悟空，你来了这么久，想跟我学点什么？"

## ⑨

  须菩提问。
  悟空耸耸肩。
  > "弟子不知，请师父示知。"
  > "那我教你未卜先知如何？"须菩提说。
  > "能长生吗？"悟空问。
  > "也许吧，"悟空说，"这能长生不老吗？"

## ⑩

  > "不能。"须菩提答。
  > "那算了，"悟空说，"教点别的。"
  > "嗯，教你些呼吸法术如何？"须菩提道。
  > "能长生吗？"悟空问。
  > "不能。"须菩提皱眉道。

## ⑪

  > "那我更没兴趣了。"悟空说。
  须菩提大怒，起身在悟空头上连敲三下，然后拂袖离去。
  > "悟空，"有弟子斥责，"你对师父太无礼了。"
  其他弟子纷纷附和。

## ⑫

  悟空却笑了。众人皆不知其意。
  > "咱们慢慢显形吧，"悟空说。
  另一弟子也应和："好，咱们显形吧。"
  又有弟子说："好。"
  随后大家各自展现本领，齐声喝彩。
