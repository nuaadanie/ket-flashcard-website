# Journey to the West · Chapter Twenty-Seven: The Jinsing Fruit

## ①

  The Tang monk trembled with fear. The animals showed their fangs.

> "Faster!" cried Wukong. The monkey galloped the horse faster. He swung his iron bar and the animals ran back into the forest.

  Wukong smiled.

> "Sorry for making the horse run, Master. I thought it would be funny."

  The Tang monk frowned.

  "It wasn't funny at all. Those beasts were going to attack me."

> "Nonsense," said Wukong. "You never have to fear any wild animals. You have three powerful companions."

## ②

  Bajie and Wujing soon caught up to them. They all continued along the road. The Tang monk pointed to a building up ahead.

> "That looks like a Taoist abbey. I'm sure the abbey's priests will let us rest for a while," said Wujing.

  When they reached the abbey, Bajie knocked on the door. Two priests opened it.

> "I'm here with the Tang monk," said the pig. "May we come in?"

  The priests looked at the Tang monk.

> "Our master told us about you," said one of them. "He said you would travel past our abbey someday. Please come in."

> "Who's your master?" asked the Tang monk. "How did he know about me?"

> "Our master is the Great Immortal," said the first priest. "He is very wise and powerful."

  Wukong laughed.

> "I've never heard of him. I doubt he's very powerful."

  The Tang monk scowled.

> "Show some respect, Wukong," he whispered.

## ③

  The priests looked annoyed.

> "Our master is visiting friends in heaven right now. If he wasn't powerful, he wouldn't be able to do that."

  Wukong shrugged.

> "I used to live in heaven. The place isn't that great."

> "Wukong," said the Tang monk, "please go outside and tend the horse."

  Wukong went back outside. Wujing went out to get the bags.

> "May I use the kitchen?" asked Bajie. "I want to cook some food for the Tang monk."

> "It's through that door," said one priest. He pointed to a door in the back of the room.

## ④

  Bajie went into the kitchen while the Tang monk talked to the priest.

> "There's a ginseng tree behind the abbey," said one priest. "Its fruit takes ten thousand years to become ripe. According to legend, one bite of the fruit will make you live forever. Would you like one?"

> "A fruit like that is a true treasure," said the Tang monk. "But would your master be upset if I eat it?"

> "Our master said we could give you one," said the other priest. "We'll go and pick one down."

  The two priests went outside. A moment later they came back in with a tray. The Tang monk screamed. On the tray lay a tiny sleeping baby.

> "I can't eat that!" cried the monk. "It's a baby!"

## ⑤

> "It's not a baby," said one priest. "It's a ginseng fruit. It only looks like a baby to you because you're mortal."

> "Please take it away!" cried the Tang monk.

  The priests tried to convince him that the fruit wasn't a baby. But the monk just shook his head. The priests took the ginseng fruit out of the room and into a hallway.

> "We shouldn't waste this fruit," said one priest.

> "No," said the other. "It is too delicious and valuable. Let's eat it ourselves."

  The two priests shared the ginseng fruit. Bajie was in the kitchen nearby. He had heard everything they'd said. His stomach growled.

> "That fruit sounds wonderful," said Bajie to himself. "I want some too."

# 🇨🇳 第二十七章：人参果


## ①

  唐僧吓得直发抖。野兽们露出了獠牙。

> "快跑！"悟空大喊，骑着马跑得更快了。他挥起铁棒，把野兽们赶回了森林。

  悟空笑了笑。

> "对不起让您受惊了，师父。我觉得挺好玩的。"

  唐僧皱起了眉头。

> "一点都不好玩！那些野兽差点就攻击我了。"

> "别担心，"悟空说，"您再也不用怕任何野兽了。您有三位神通广大的徒弟呢。"

## ②

  八戒和悟净很快就赶了上来。大家继续赶路。唐僧指着前方的一座建筑。

> "那看起来是一座道观，"悟净说，"观里的道长一定会让我们歇一会儿的。"

  到了道观，八戒敲了敲门。两个道士开了门。

> "我和唐僧一起来的，"八戒说，"我们能进来吗？"

  道士们打量着唐僧。

> "我们师父跟我们提起过您，"一个道士说，"他说您有一天会从我们观前经过。请进来吧。"

> "你们的师父是谁？"唐僧问，"他怎么会知道我？"

> "我们师父是镇元大仙，"第一个道士说，"他法力高强，非常有智慧。"

  悟空哈哈一笑。

> "我可从没听说过他。我不觉得他有多大能耐。"

  唐僧瞪了他一眼。

> "悟空，放尊重点，"唐僧低声说。

## ③

  道士们脸上现出不悦之色。

> "我们师父这会儿正到天上去拜访朋友呢。要是他没本事，哪去得了天上去。"

  悟空耸了耸肩。

> "我以前也住过天上。那地方也没什么了不起。"

> "悟空，"唐僧说，"你出去把马照顾好。"

  悟空又回到了外面。悟净去拿行李了。

> "我能用一下厨房吗？"八戒问，"我想给唐僧做点吃的。"

> "从那扇门进去，"一个道士说。他指了指屋子后面的一扇门。

## ④

  八戒进了厨房，唐僧和道士说着话。

> "观后面有一棵人参果树，"一个道士说，"它的果子要一万年才能成熟。据传说，吃一口就能长生不老。您来一个吗？"

> "这样的果子可是宝贝啊，"唐僧说，"可你们师父会怪我吃了它吗？"

> "我们师父说可以给您一个，"另一个道士说，"我们去摘一个来。"

  两个道士出了门。不一会儿他们端着一个托盘回来。唐僧惊叫起来。托盘上躺着一个熟睡的小婴儿。

> "我可不能吃这个！"唐僧叫道，"那是婴儿啊！"

## ⑤

> "这不是婴儿，"一个道士说，"这是人参果。只是因为您是凡人才看着像婴儿。"

> "请把它拿走！"唐僧叫道。

  道士们想向唐僧解释果子不是婴儿。可是唐僧只是摇头。道士们把人参果端出房间，放到走廊里。

> "这么好的果子不该浪费，"一个道士说。

> "是啊，"另一个说，"又好吃又珍贵，我们自己吃吧。"

  两个道士分吃了人参果。八戒就在不远处的厨房里，把他们的谈话听得一清二楚。他的肚子咕噜咕噜地叫起来。

> "那果子听上去可真棒，"八戒自言自语道，"我也想吃。"
