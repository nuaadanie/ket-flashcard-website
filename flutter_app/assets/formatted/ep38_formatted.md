# Journey to the West · Chapter Thirty-Eight: Bajie Tries to Sleep

## ①

  Time passed and it was soon spring. The air grew warm, flowers bloomed, and birds swooped from tree to tree. The road Wukong and the others were traveling on began to go up a mountain. Wukong stopped the group.

> "Bajie, go ahead and search the mountain. There might be a demon up there and I don't want Master to get hurt. Move, pig!"

> "You make me do all the hard work," grumbled the pig.

  He headed up the mountain road.

## ②

> "I'm going to follow the pig," Wukong turned to the Tang Monk and Wu Jing. "I want to make sure he really searches the mountain."

  The monkey turned invisible and followed Bajie up the road. The pig walked for a few minutes. When he was sure his companions wouldn't hear him, he turned around.

> "You dummies," said Bajie. "You make me do all the hard work. Well, guess what? I'm not going to search this mountain. I'm going to take a nap instead. And when I come back, I'll lie and say I searched the mountain."

  Bajie found a big log. He laid down behind it and fell fast asleep.

## ③

> "This pig is so lazy!" thought Wukong. "How could you manage him?"

  The monkey turned himself into a woodpecker and pecked the log. Bajie rolled over and mumbled. Wukong pecked again. The pig sat up.

> "Quit tapping, you annoying woodpecker! I'm trying to sleep."

  He fell asleep again. Wukong landed on Bajie's head. *Tap, tap, tap, tap, tap.*

> "Ouch!" cried Bajie, jumping to his feet. "Who..." The woodpecker flew away. Bajie rubbed his head. "I can't even take a nap. I guess I'll go back down."

## ④

  When Bajie found his companions, he lied.

> "I searched the entire mountain," he said. "It's safe."

> "Did you see any demons or monsters?" asked Wukong.

  Bajie shook his head.

> "How about woodpeckers?" asked the monkey. "Did you see any of them?"

  The pig looked surprised.

> "What? How did you...?"

> "That was me, Bajie!" shouted Wukong. "You took a nap. You didn't search the mountain at all."

## ⑤

  Bajie hung his head.

> "You should be ashamed of yourself, Bajie," said Wu Jing.

  The Tang Monk frowned.

> "Please go back and search the mountain, Bajie."

  The pig headed back up the road. He had walked for a while when he saw a fox.

> "I know that's you, Wukong!" he shouted. He swung his rake. The fox trotted away.

  A robin flew past Bajie's head.

> "Get away from me, Wukong!" yelped the pig, as he waved the rake at the bird.

  Bajie tripped and fell.

## ⑥

> "Wukong!" shouted the pig. "I know you're here. You're invisible and you just tripped me."

  He got back up and stood still for a moment. When he didn't see or hear Wukong, he continued walking. Every time Bajie saw an animal, he waved his rake and yelped.

  The road became very steep. The pig soon came to a cave. In front of the cave was a demon. Bajie held out his rake.

> "Tell me who you are!" he demanded.

> "My name is Golden Horn," said the demon. "Who are you?"

## ⑦

> "I am Zhu Bajie," said the pig. "I'm traveling to the Western Paradise with the Tang Monk."

> "Oh, the Tang Monk." A smile curled along the demon's lips. "I have heard that anyone who eats the Tang Monk will live forever."

# 🇨🇳 第三十八章：八戒想睡觉


## ①

  时光飞逝，转眼到了春天。天气变暖了，百花盛开，鸟儿在树枝间飞来飞去。悟空一行人走的路开始向山上延伸。悟空让大伙儿停了下来。

> "八戒，你去前面搜一下这座山。上面可能有妖怪，我可不想师父出什么事。快去吧，猪头！"

> "干吗总是让我干苦活？"八戒嘟囔着。

  他沿着山路往上走去。

## ②

> "我要跟着这头猪，"悟空对唐僧和悟净说，"我得确保他真上山搜索了。"

  猴子隐了身，跟着八戒上了山。八戒走了几分钟，确认同伴们听不到他说话了，就转了身。

> "一群笨蛋，"八戒说，"老是让我干苦活。你们猜怎么着？我才不去搜这座山呢。我要睡一觉去。等我回去，就说整座山都搜遍了。"

  八戒找到一根大圆木，躺在后面呼呼大睡起来。

## ③

> "这猪也太懒了吧！"悟空心想，"拿他真没办法。"

  猴子变成了一只啄木鸟，去啄那根木头。八戒翻了个身，嘟囔了几句。悟空又啄了一下。八戒坐了起来。

> "别啄了，你这只讨厌的啄木鸟！我正要睡觉呢。"

  他又躺下睡着了。悟空落在八戒的头顶上。笃、笃、笃、笃、笃。

> "哎呦！"八戒跳了起来，"谁......"啄木鸟飞走了。八戒揉着脑袋说，"连个觉都睡不成。唉，我还是下山回去吧。"

## ④

  八戒找到同伴们，撒了个谎。

> "整座山我都搜过了，"他说，"安全得很。"

> "有没有看到什么妖怪魔鬼？"悟空问。

  八戒摇了摇头。

> "有没有看到啄木鸟？"猴子又问，"看到过吗？"

  八戒一脸惊讶。

> "什么？你怎么......？"

> "那就是我，八戒！"悟空大喊道，"你睡了一觉。你压根儿就没搜山。"

## ⑤

  八戒耷拉着脑袋。

> "八戒，你自己应该感到惭愧，"悟净说。

  唐僧皱起了眉头。

> "请重新回去搜一下山吧，八戒。"

  八戒又沿着山路往上走。走了一会儿，他看到一只狐狸。

> "我知道是你，悟空！"他大叫一声，挥起钉耙砸去。狐狸一溜烟跑了。

  一只知更鸟从八戒头上飞过。

> "滚开，悟空！"八戒一边朝鸟挥舞钉耙一边喊。

  八戒一不留神，绊倒在地。

## ⑥

> "悟空！"八戒大喊，"我知道你在这里。你隐身了，刚才绊倒的就是你！"

  他爬起来，站了一会儿。既没看到悟空，也没听到悟空的动静，于是他继续往前走。一路上，八戒每看到一只动物，就挥着钉耙大叫。

  路变得越来越陡。八戒不久就到了一个洞口，洞前站着一个妖怪。八戒举起钉耙。

> "报上你的名号！"他大声喝道。

> "我叫金角大王，"妖怪说，"你又是谁？"

## ⑦

> "我是猪八戒，"八戒说，"我跟唐僧一起去西天取经。"

> "哦，唐僧。"妖怪的嘴角浮起一丝微笑，"我听说吃了唐僧肉就能长生不老。"
