# Journey to the West · Chapter Twenty-Eight: Wukong Leaves His Body

## ①

  Bajie looked out the kitchen window. Wukong and Wujing were outside talking.

> "Wukong," Wujing said. The pig quietly added, "Come over here. I want to tell you something."

  The two companions came over to the window.

> "What is it?" asked Wukong.

> "I heard that you're pretty hungry, Wukong," said the pig. "There's a ginseng tree at this abbey. They say its fruit is delicious and valuable."

  Wukong smiled.

> "The fruit must taste wonderful. We should eat some."

> "We should eat some," said the pig.

## ②

  Bajie rubbed his belly.

> "Yes," he said. "We should."

> "You two wait here," said Wukong. "I'll go pick us some of that fruit."

  The monkey went behind the abbey and found the giant ginseng tree. The fruits sparkled in the sunlight. He plucked three pieces from the tree's branches and went back to the kitchen. He handed Bajie and Wujing each a ginseng fruit. Wujing took a bite.

> "This fruit is delicious."

> "Yes," said Wukong. "It truly is."

## ③

  Bajie opened his mouth wide and threw his ginseng fruit in. He gulped it down.

> "Bajie, you should be ashamed of yourself," said Wukong. "You didn't even chew."

> "I was too hungry to chew," said the pig. He groaned. "And now I wish I had another ginseng fruit. I didn't even taste that one."

## ④

  At that moment, the two priests were walking past the kitchen.

> "Did you hear what the pig said?" whispered one priest. "I think the Tang monk's companions are eating the ginseng fruit."

  The two priests ran outside and searched the ginseng tree. Three pieces of fruit were gone.

> "This is terrible," said one priest. "The Great Immortal will be very upset."

  They ran inside and found the Tang monk.

> "Your companions are criminals!" shouted one priest.

> "What are you talking about?" asked the monk.

> "They stole some of our ginseng fruit," said the other priest.

## ⑤

  Just then Wukong, Bajie, and Wujing walked in.

> "Thieves!" shouted the first priest.

> "How dare you call us thieves?" shouted Bajie. "What did we steal?"

> "Don't pretend you don't know," said the other priest. "You ate ginseng fruit from our master's tree."

  Wujing wiped some juice from the side of his mouth. Wukong giggled.

> "What is so funny?" asked the other priest.

  Wukong laughed harder and pointed at Bajie.

> "It was his idea."

> "You're the one who stole the fruit," shouted the pig.

> "You're all thieves and liars," shouted one of the priests. "We invited you in as guests. And you repay us by stealing from us!"

## ⑥

  The two priests yelled and yelled. They called Wukong and his companions all sorts of terrible names.

> "I don't want to listen to this," thought the monkey.

  He recited a quick spell and changed into a spirit form. He left his body standing in the room and went out behind the abbey. Wukong stood before the ginseng tree.

> "Those priests are rude," he said to himself. "So what if we ate some ginseng fruit? They have no right to call us names."

## ⑦

  Wukong grew very angry. Finally he took out his iron bar and struck the tree. There was a loud crack and the entire tree crashed to the ground. Wukong put the iron bar back in his ear and returned to his body. The priests were still yelling.

> "Stop yelling," commanded Wukong.

  The priests stopped yelling and looked at the monkey.

> "I think you made a mistake," said the Wukong with a big smile. "Why don't you go outside to your ginseng tree and count the fruit again?"

# 🇨🇳 第二十八章：悟空离身


## ①

  八戒从厨房的窗户往外看。悟空和悟净正在外面说话。

> "悟空，"悟净说。八戒压低声音插了一句，"过来一下，我有话告诉你们。"

  两个同伴走到了窗户边。

> "什么事？"悟空问。

> "悟空，我看你肚子挺饿的，"八戒说，"这道观里有人参果树。听说那果子又好吃又珍贵。"

  悟空笑了笑。

> "那果子一定很好吃。咱们应该吃几个。"

> "确实该吃，"八戒说。

## ②

  八戒摸了摸肚子。

> "是啊，"他说，"咱们吃吧。"

> "你们俩在这儿等等，"悟空说，"我去摘几个来。"

  猴子跑到道观后面，找到了那棵巨大的人参果树。果子在阳光下闪闪发光。他从树枝上摘了三个果子，回到厨房，给八戒和悟净各递了一个。悟净咬了一口。

> "这果子真好吃！"

> "是啊，"悟空说，"确实不错。"

## ③

  八戒张大嘴巴，把整个人参果一口吞了下去。

> "八戒，你真该害臊，"悟空说，"你连嚼都没嚼一下。"

> "我太饿了，顾不上嚼啊，"八戒说。他咕哝了一声，"唉，要是再有一个就好了。刚才那个我连什么味道都没尝出来。"

## ④

  就在这时，两个道士从厨房外走过。

> "你听见那头猪说什么了吗？"一个道士小声说，"我觉得唐僧的同伴在偷吃人参果。"

  两个道士跑到外面去看人参果树。发现少了三个果子。

> "这太糟了，"一个道士说，"大仙回来一定会非常生气的。"

  他们跑进屋找到了唐僧。

> "您的同伴是贼人！"一个道士喊道。

> "你说什么？"唐僧问。

> "他们偷了我们的人参果，"另一个道士说。

## ⑤

  正说着，悟空、八戒和悟净走了进来。

> "贼人！"第一个道士叫道。

> "你怎么敢叫我们贼人？"八戒吼道，"我们偷什么了？"

> "别装不知道，"另一个道士说，"你们吃了我们师父树上的人参果。"

  悟净擦了擦嘴角的汁水。悟空咯咯地笑出了声。

> "你笑什么？"另一个道士问。

  悟空笑得更厉害了，指着八戒。

> "是他出的主意！"

> "是你偷的果子！"八戒叫道。

> "你们全是贼人骗子！"一个道士吼道，"我们把你们当客人请进来，你们倒偷我们的东西！"

## ⑥

  两个道士不停地叫骂，把悟空和他同伴骂了个遍。

> "我才懒得听这些！"猴子心想。

  他念了个法诀，化作元神出了窍。他把肉身留在屋里，元神飞到了道观后面。悟空站在人参果树前。

> "这些道士太无礼了。就算吃了几个果子又怎样？他们也犯不着骂人啊。"

## ⑦

  悟空越说越气。终于他掏出铁棒朝树打去。只听一声巨响，整棵树轰然倒地。悟空把铁棒塞回耳朵里，元神回到肉身。道士们仍在叫骂。

> "别喊了！"悟空命令道。

  道士们闭了嘴，看着猴子。

> "我看你们弄错了，"悟空笑着说，"你们怎么不去外面看看那棵人参果树，再数数果子呢？"
