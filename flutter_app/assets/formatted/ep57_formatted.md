# Journey to the West · Chapter Fifty-Seven: A Guessing Game

## ①

  "Don't make any rain yet," Wukong repeated. "But we must!" said the old woman of the wind. "Someone on Earth just prayed for it!"

  "And the Jade Emperor heard the prayers," added the Dragon King of the Eastern Sea.

  "The person who recited the prayers is Bad," said Wukong. "He's the tiger immortal, and he plans to make the Tang Monk a slave."

  The Dragon King looked concerned. "That's terrible. We all want the Tang Monk to reach the Western Paradise."

  "But we must obey the Jade Emperor's orders," said the woman.

  "Yes, wait a moment," said the monkey. "I'll go back down to the ground now. When I raise my iron bar, you can make it rain."

  The woman and the Dragon King agreed to wait a moment.

## ②

  Wukong returned to his body. The stiffened tiger immortal shook with anger.

> "Why didn't my spell of water work?" he cried.

  Bajie laughed at him. "Some rain! You couldn't even summon one drop."

> "Don't try again!" said the tiger immortal.

  "No, you won't," said Wukong. "You had a chance to summon rain, and you obviously can't. Now it's my turn."

## ③

  The monkey raised his iron bar into the air. Instantly clouds moved back in, and thunder boomed. Rain began to pour down. The crowd cheered.

  The Tang Monk sighed. "Wonderful. Now all the Buddhist monks will be free. And we can continue our journey."

## ④

  The tiger immortal stomped over to Wukong.

> "I want another contest."

  Wukong laughed. "Why would you want another contest? You'll lose again."

  The tiger immortal ignored the monkey's question. "I challenge the Tang Monk to a guessing game."

  The Tang Monk's mouth fell open. "But..."

  "We accept your challenge," said the monkey with a smile.

  "Good!" said the tiger immortal. "I will be right back." He walked away.

## ⑤

> "Wukong, why did you agree to this contest?" asked the Tang Monk.

  "I will win. Don't worry, Master," said the monkey. "I'll help you."

  The tiger immortal soon came back with a small box.

> "If you can guess what's in this box, I will let you all go free."

  As the Tang Monk stared at the box, Wukong plucked a hair. Using his magic, he made the hair look like himself. Then he made himself tiny and squeezed through a crack in the box. Inside the box was a ripe peach. Quickly, he ate the peach, leaving only the pit. A moment later, he was standing next to the Tang Monk again.

## ⑥

  Wukong leaned toward the monk and whispered:

> "It's a peach pit."

> "Are you sure?" whispered the monk. "How do you know?"

> "Stop talking to each other!" said the tiger immortal. "What's in the box?"

> "I think it's a peach pit," said the monk nervously.

  "You're wrong, monk," said the tiger immortal. "It's not just a peach pit. It's a whole peach. I'll show you."

## ⑦

  The tiger immortal opened the box and showed it to everyone.

> "See?"

  The Tang Monk smiled. "I was right. It is a peach pit."

  "What?" cried the tiger immortal. He looked in the box and gasped. "Who ate the peach?"

  Wukong laughed.

> "Wukong, with the Tang Monk, guessed correctly. I guess we can leave now," said Wujing.

  "Yes, but we should eat some dinner first," said Bajie. "All this talk about peaches has made me hungry."

  Wukong looked at the tiger immortal. "You will set the Buddhist monks free now, right?"

## ⑧

  The tiger immortal glared at the monkey.

> "I challenge you to another contest!"

# 🇨🇳 第五十七章：猜谜游戏


## ①

  "先别下雨，"悟空重复道。"但我们必须下！"风婆婆说。"地上有人祈雨了！"

  "玉帝听到了祈祷，"东海龙王补充道。

  "祈雨的是虎力大仙，"悟空说。"他是只虎精，他想让唐僧做他的奴隶。"

  龙王看起来很担心。"太可怕了。我们都希望唐僧能到达西天。"

  "但我们必须遵从玉帝的命令，"风婆婆说。

  "好，等一下，"悟空说。"我现在回到地面去。等我举起金箍棒，你们就下雨。"

  风婆婆和龙王同意等一等。

## ②

  悟空回到了自己的身体里。僵住的虎力大仙气得发抖。

> "我的求雨法术怎么不灵了？"他叫道。

  八戒嘲笑他。"还下雨呢！你连一滴雨都求不来。"

> "别再试了！"虎力大仙说。

  "不，该你了，"悟空说。"你有机会求雨，可你明显不行。现在轮到我。"

## ③

  悟空将金箍棒举向空中。顿时乌云密布，雷声轰鸣，大雨倾盆而下。人群欢呼起来。

  唐僧叹了口气。"太好了。现在所有佛教僧侣都自由了，我们可以继续赶路了。"

## ④

  虎力大仙气冲冲地走到悟空面前。

> "我要再比一场。"

  悟空笑了。"你为什么还要比？你又会输的。"

  虎力大仙不理会悟空的话。"我挑战唐僧，比猜物！"

  唐僧张大了嘴。"可是……"

  "我们接受你的挑战，"悟空笑着说。

  "好！"虎力大仙说。"我马上回来。"他走开了。

## ⑤

> "悟空，你为什么答应这个比赛？"唐僧问。

  "我们会赢的。别担心，师父，"悟空说。"我会帮你的。"

  虎力大仙很快带着一个小匣子回来了。

> "如果你们能猜出这匣子里装的是什么，我就放你们走。"

  唐僧盯着匣子，悟空拔了一根毫毛，用毫毛变了个自己，然后把自己变小，从匣子的裂缝钻了进去。匣子里是一颗熟透的桃子。悟空迅速把桃子吃了，只剩桃核。一会儿，他又回到了唐僧身边。

## ⑥

  悟空凑近唐僧低声说：

> "是桃核。"

> "你确定吗？"唐僧低声问。"你怎么知道的？"

> "别互相说话了！"虎力大仙说。"匣子里是什么？"

> "我觉得是桃核，"唐僧紧张地说。

  "你猜错了，和尚，"虎力大仙说。"不只是桃核，是一整个桃子。我给你们看。"

## ⑦

  虎力大仙打开匣子给大家看。

> "看到了吧？"

  唐僧笑了。"我猜对了，就是桃核。"

  "什么？"虎力大仙叫道。他看了看匣子，大吃一惊。"谁把桃子吃了？"

  悟空哈哈大笑。

> "悟空和唐僧猜对了。我想我们现在可以走了吧，"悟净说。

  "可以，但我们应该先吃晚饭，"八戒说。"说了这么多桃子，我都饿了。"

  悟空看着虎力大仙。"你现在就释放那些佛教僧侣，对吧？"

## ⑧

  虎力大仙怒视着悟空。

> "我再挑战你们比一场！"
