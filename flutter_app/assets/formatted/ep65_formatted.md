# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixty-Five: The Metal Ring

## ①

  The demon tied up the Tang Monk in a dark room. Then the demon dragged in Bajie and Wujing. They were still trapped in the vests. The demons dumped them in a corner and approached the monk.

> "I've been hoping you would pass my mountain," the demon said with an evil smile.

> "Please let us go," said the Tang Monk. "We're trying to reach the Western Paradise."

## ②

> "I know where you're going," said the demon. "I also know that I'll live forever if I eat you."

> "Who are you?" demanded Wujing from the corner.

  The demon turned to face him.

> "I am One-Horned Buffalo."

> "Not friendly at all," said Bajie. "He's very powerful. And when he learns that you kidnapped us, he'll destroy your entire mountain."

## ③

  One-Horned Buffalo scrunched up his face.

> "Who is your friend?"

> "His name is Sun Wukong," said Bajie.

  The demon's smirk disappeared.

> "Is he the Sun Wukong who caused trouble in Heaven?"

  The demon sounded nervous.

## ④

> "Yes, he is," said Wujing.

> "Heaven's entire army couldn't defeat him," Bajie added. "Even the Jade Emperor is afraid of him."

  One-Horned Buffalo stood taller.

> "Well, I'm not afraid of him."

## ⑤

  The demon walked out of the room. Once he was away from the others, though, he grew worried again.

> "That monkey is powerful," he said to himself. "I'm going to need my special weapon to defeat him."

  He lifted his sleeve, revealing a metal ring around his arm.

## ⑥

  Carrying food, Wukong returned to where he left his friends. When he didn't see them, he looked up toward the monastery. The building was gone. In its place was a dark cave.

  Wukong shook his head.

> "That monastery was an illusion. A demon must have made it to trick people."

  He stamped his foot.

> "They never believe me when I warn them about demons."

  He put down the food and rushed to the cave.

## ⑦

> "Open up!" shouted the monkey. "If you don't, I'll turn this entire mountain into a pile of rubble!"

  The door flung open, and One-Horned Buffalo stepped out.

> "Why are you yelling?" demanded the demon. "It's rude."

> "Release my friends," yelled Wukong.

> "No." One-Horned Buffalo folded his arms. "I'm not going to release your friends. They're criminals. They stole my valuable silk vests."

## ⑧

> "That's a lie," said Wukong. "The Tang Monk is a good and honest man. He would never steal. I can't believe you would even suggest it."

> "Oh." The monkey scratched his head. "Well, then I believe you. But that doesn't matter."

## ⑨

  Wukong pulled out his iron bar and swung it at the demon. One-Horned Buffalo dodged the bar, which smashed into the mountainside. Rocks exploded and tumbled down the mountain slope.

  The demon glared at Wukong.

> "I remember you. The Jade Emperor may fear you, but I don't."

## ⑩

  He rushed at Wukong and the monkey swung his iron bar again. The demon blocked it with his arm.

> "Man, ow," cried One-Horned Buffalo, rubbing his arm.

  He fell to his knees and turned his back to the monkey.

## ⑪

> "My iron bar is not an ordinary weapon," said Wukong. "It's extremely heavy."

  He noticed the demon reaching up his sleeve.

> "What are you doing?"

  One-Horned Buffalo spun around, holding up the metal ring.

## ⑫

  Instantly, Wukong felt himself being pulled toward the ring. Using all of his strength, the monkey reached into the air. But the bar was wrenched from his hands. It flew into the metal ring and vanished.

# 🇨🇳 第六十五章：金钢琢


## ①

  妖怪把唐僧绑在一间暗室里，然后拖进了八戒和悟净——他们还被困在丝织背心里呢。妖怪把他们扔在角落里，然后走向唐僧。

> "我一直盼着你们经过我的山，"妖怪邪恶地笑着说。

> "请放了我们吧，"唐僧说，"我们是要去西天的。"

## ②

> "我知道你们要去哪儿，"妖怪说，"我也知道如果吃了你，我就能长生不老。"

> "你是什么人？"悟净在角落里质问道。

  妖怪转身面向他。

> "我是独角牛魔王。"

> "这家伙一点不友善，"八戒说，"他可是很厉害的。等他知道了你绑架了我们，他一定会把整座山都踏平。"

## ③

  独角牛魔王皱了皱眉头。

> "你的朋友是谁？"

> "他叫孙悟空，"八戒说。

  妖怪的得意笑容消失了。

> "他就是那个在天宫闹过事的孙悟空？"

  妖怪的语气变得有些紧张了。

## ④

> "没错，就是他，"悟净说。

> "天宫的千军万马都打不过他，"八戒补充道，"连玉皇大帝都怕他。"

  独角牛魔王挺直了腰杆。

> "哼，我才不怕他呢。"

## ⑤

  妖怪走出了房间。可一离开其他人，他的担忧又重新浮现。

> "那只猴子确实厉害，"他自言自语道，"我非得用上我的特殊兵器才能打败他。"

  他抬起袖子，露出了手臂上的一个金属圈。

## ⑥

  悟空带着食物回到他放下同伴的地方。当他发现同伴不见了，便抬头朝寺院的方向望去。那栋建筑已经不见了，取而代之的是一座黑暗的山洞。

  悟空摇了摇头。

> "那座寺院是个幻象，一定是妖怪变出来的，用来骗人。"

  他跺了跺脚。

> "每次我提醒他们有妖怪，他们偏偏不信。"

  他放下食物，朝山洞冲去。

## ⑦

> "开门！"猴子大喊道，"再不开门，我就把整座山夷为平地！"

  门猛地开了，独角牛魔王走了出来。

> "你嚷嚷什么？"妖怪质问道，"太没礼貌了。"

> "放出我的朋友！"悟空大喊。

> "不行。"独角牛魔王双手交叉在胸前，"我不会放你的朋友的。他们是罪犯，偷了我珍贵的丝绸背心。"

## ⑧

> "胡说八道，"悟空说，"唐僧是个善良正直的人，他绝不会偷东西，我不相信你会这么认为。"

> "哦。"猴子挠了挠头，"好吧，我相信你。但这也不重要了。"

## ⑨

  悟空掏出金箍棒朝妖怪挥去。独角牛魔王闪身一躲，金箍棒重重地砸进了山壁里，石块爆炸般向山坡上翻滚而去。

  妖怪瞪着悟空。

> "我记得你。玉皇大帝可能怕你，但我不怕。"

## ⑩

  他朝悟空扑去，猴子再次挥出金箍棒。妖怪用胳膊挡住了金箍棒。

> "哎哟，"独角牛魔王捂着胳膊叫了一声。

  他跪倒在地，背对着猴子。

## ⑪

> "我的金箍棒可不是普通的兵器，"悟空说，"它重极了。"

  他注意到妖怪把手伸进了袖子里。

> "你在干什么？"

  独角牛魔王猛地转过身来，举起了那个金属圈。

## ⑫

  刹那间，悟空感觉自己正被金属圈吸过去。猴子用尽全身力气挣扎着，手臂向空中乱抓。但金箍棒还是脱手而去，飞进了金属圈里，消失不见。
