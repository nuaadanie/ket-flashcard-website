# Journey to the West · Chapter Twenty-Nine: The Magic Flute

## ①

> "I know we counted the fruit correctly," said one priest. "But we'll count again anyway."

  The two angry priests went outside and gasped. The ginseng tree was lying on the ground. It was grey and all its leaves were gone.

> "The tree is dead!" cried one priest. "That monkey must have done this!"

> "But the monkey was inside the whole time," said the other priest. "How could he have been out here also?"

> "He's a spirit," said the first priest. "He must have used magic."

  He looked at the tree and shook his head.

> "This tree was older than heaven and earth."

## ②

> "The monkey must be very powerful. It would be dangerous for us to fight him," said the other priest. "Let's wait for our master, the Great Immortal, to return."

  Inside the abbey, the Tang monk was scolding his companions.

> "We are guests here," he said. "I am very upset you stole fruit from our hosts."

  The priests came back in.

> "We owe you an apology," said one. "None of the ginseng fruit is missing after all."

> "Oh!" said the Tang monk. "I'm glad to hear that."

> "Thank you for your apology," said Bajie.

  Wukong gave the priest a suspicious look. He knew they were lying, but he didn't know why.

## ③

> "You're all welcome to spend the night," said one of the priests. "Follow us."

  The priest led the group to a small room in the abbey. The Tang monk and his companions went inside.

> "This is a nice room," said Bajie.

  Wukong gave the priest a suspicious look. The priest didn't come in. He was about to say something when one priest saw an audible shot.

> "A lock! Quick!"

  *Click!*

> "Wait!" cried Bajie. "What kind of hosts are you? You locked us in!"

  *Bam!* He kicked the door.

> "Ow, my foot!"

  Bajie fell to the floor, rubbing his foot.

## ④

> "Why did they lock us in here?" asked the Tang monk.

> "They're afraid of us," said Wukong. "I knocked down their ginseng tree."

> "What?" cried the Tang monk.

> "I was angry at them," said Wukong. "They're probably planning to keep us locked in here until their master comes back."

  The Tang monk glared at his companions.

> "How will I ever reach the Western Paradise with companions like you?" he asked.

  Bajie stood up.

> "It's not my fault. I was hungry."

  Wujing looked at the ground.

> "Master, don't worry," said Wukong. "I'll get us out of here. When the priests go to sleep, I'll use my lock-picking magic. Then we'll be on our way."

## ⑤

  The four travelers escaped that night and headed west. The next day they saw an old man on the road.

> "Excuse me," said the man. "Did you come from the abbey?"

  The Tang monk glanced nervously at his companions.

> "Um, yes," he said.

> "I thought so," said the man. He suddenly stood up much taller. "I am the Great Immortal. You destroyed my ginseng tree. You're not going any farther until you give me a new one."

## ⑥

  Wukong pulled out his iron bar. Bajie and Wujing raised their weapons. The Great Immortal rose into the air and landed on a cloud.

  Wukong laughed.

> "You think you're special just because you can fly? We can do that too!"

  The Great Immortal raised an eyebrow.

> "Can you do this?" he said. "It's called Magic of the Cosmos in the Sleeve."

  The Great Immortal waved his arm. His sleeve swooped down toward Wukong and his companions. Instantly they were all swept up in it.

# 🇨🇳 第二十九章：魔笛


## ①

> "果子明明数过没错的，"一个道士说，"不过我们还是再去数一遍。"

  两个气急败坏的道士跑到外面，顿时倒吸一口凉气。人参果树倒在地上，灰不溜秋的，叶子全掉光了。

> "树死了！"一个道士叫道，"一定是那猴子干的！"

> "可那猴子一直待在屋里呀，"另一个道士说，"他怎么可能跑到外面来呢？"

> "他是精怪，"第一个道士说，"一定是用了法术。"

  他望着那棵树摇了摇头。

> "这棵树比天地还要古老啊。"

## ②

> "这猴子一定法力高强，我们跟他斗太危险了，"另一个道士说，"还是等我们师父大仙回来了再说吧。"

  观里，唐僧正在训斥徒弟们。

> "我们在这里是客人，"他说，"你们偷主人的果子，让我太失望了。"

  道士们走了回来。

> "是我们要向你们道歉，"一个道士说，"原来人参果一个也没少。"

> "哦！"唐僧说，"那我就放心了。"

> "谢谢你们的道歉，"八戒说。

  悟空狐疑地看了道士一眼。他知道他们在说谎，但不知道为什么要说谎。

## ③

> "诸位今晚就在这歇息吧，"一个道士说，"请跟我们走。"

  道士领着众人到了观里一间小屋。唐僧和同伴们走了进去。

> "房间不错，"八戒说。

  悟空狐疑地看看道士。道士没进来。他正要说什么，另一个道士突然喊道：

> "锁门！快！"

  咔哒！

> "等等！"八戒叫道，"你们这是什么待客之道？把我们锁起来！"

  砰！他用力踢门。

> "哎呀，我的脚！"

  八戒倒在地上，揉着脚丫子。

## ④

> "他们为什么把我们锁在这里？"唐僧问。

> "他们怕我们，"悟空说，"我把他们的人参果树打倒了。"

> "什么？"唐僧惊呼。

> "我当时太生他们的气了，"悟空说，"他们大概是打算把我们关在这儿，等他们师父回来。"

  唐僧怒视着他的徒弟们。

> "有你们这样的同伴，我什么时候才能到得了西天？"他问。

  八戒站了起来。

> "不怪我，我当时太饿了。"

  悟净低头看着地面。

> "师父别担心，"悟空说，"我会带大家出去的。等道士们睡着了，我就用开锁的法术。到时候我们就可以继续赶路了。"

## ⑤

  四个旅人当天夜里逃了出去，一路向西。第二天，他们在路上碰到了一个老头。

> "打扰了，"老头说，"你们是从那观里出来的吗？"

  唐僧紧张地看了看身旁的同伴。

> "嗯，是的，"他说。

> "我就知道，"老头说。他突然长高了许多，"我就是镇元大仙。你们毁了我的人参果树，不给我赔一棵新树，你们休想再往前走一步。"

## ⑥

  悟空掏出了铁棒。八戒和悟净也亮出了兵器。大仙纵身飞上天空，落在一朵云上。

  悟空大笑起来。

> "你会飞就了不起了？我们也会！"

  大仙挑了挑眉毛。

> "那你会这个吗？这叫袖里乾坤。"

  大仙一挥衣袖。袖子猛地向悟空和他的同伴们扑了下去。一瞬间，他们全被卷进了袖子里。
