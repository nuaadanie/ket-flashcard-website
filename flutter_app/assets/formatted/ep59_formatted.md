# Journey to the West · Chapter Fifty-Nine: Scared Villagers

## ①

> "Can we please stop and rest?" moaned Bajie one day.

  The group had spent the morning walking down a long mountain trail.

> "My knees are weak," cried the pig.

  "We'll rest in a little while, Bajie," said the Tang Monk.

  As they walked, the travelers started to hear the sound of rushing water. It grew louder and louder.

> "We're approaching a river," said Wujing.

  The trail went around a hill. A moment later, the travelers saw the river.

> "How will we cross this river?" said the Tang Monk. "It's so wide, I can't see the other side."

  Wukong leaped into the air and looked around. "There's a small village nearby," he said. "Maybe someone there has a boat."

## ②

  The group walked to the village. The street was deserted. A few villagers peeked out of windows and from behind doors. They looked scared.

  A man was getting water from a well.

> "Hello?" said Wujing.

  Startled, the man dropped his bucket down the well and spun around. "Who are you?"

  The Tang Monk stepped forward. "I'm from the Tang Empire. We're traveling to the Western Paradise to get scriptures from Buddha."

## ③

  The man relaxed a bit.

  "We need a boat to get across the river," said Bajie.

> "I don't have a boat," said the man. "But someone in the village might. Please come with me. I'll make some tea. My name is Chen."

  The travelers followed Chen to his home. Inside they sat down as Chen boiled water for tea.

> "Why does everyone in this village seem so scared?" asked the Tang Monk.

  Chen sighed and sat down. He started to cry.

> "Dear man," said the Tang Monk. "What's wrong? Did I upset you?"

> "No," said Chen through his tears. "It's just that life is very bad in this village."

## ④

  "A few years ago, a terrible demon arrived. He's always treated us badly, but now things are worse. He demanded that we give him a child. If we don't, he'll destroy our entire village. Tomorrow morning, I must hand over my son."

  Chen burst into tears all over again.

  Wukong giggled and then laughed.

  The Tang Monk glared at the monkey.

> "Wukong! Why are you laughing?"

  Bajie shook his head. "He's cruel, Master. You should recite the Tight-Fillet Spell," he said.

  "No need for that," said Wukong, still laughing.

## ⑤

  He turned to Chen. "I'm not laughing at you, sir. I'm laughing because today is the luckiest day of your life."

  Chen raised his eyebrows. "Oh, am I lucky?"

> "You've probably never heard of me," said Wukong. "But I am Sun Wukong, the Monkey King. I once defeated the Jade Emperor's entire army. And tomorrow I will save your son's life."

> "Are you a spirit?" asked Chen.

  "Yes, a very powerful spirit," said Wukong. "Bring your son out here. I need to look at him."

  Chen left the room for a moment. When he came back, a small boy was with him.

> "This is my son," said Chen.

  Wukong walked around the boy, examining him closely. Finally, the monkey folded his arms and said:

> "I have a plan."

## ⑥

  The next morning the boy climbed onto a small platform. Several villagers carried the platform to the edge of the river. They placed the platform on the ground and then backed away. For a moment everything was quiet. The boy sat alone on the platform.

  Suddenly a dark cloud approached. A fierce-looking demon emerged from the cloud. It had shark teeth.

  The boy smiled at the demon.

> "Hello?"

# 🇨🇳 第五十九章：受惊的村民


## ①

> "我们能停下来休息一会儿吗？"八戒抱怨道。

  一行人一上午都在沿着一条长长的山路走着。

> "我的腿都软了，"八戒叫道。

  "过一会儿就休息，八戒，"唐僧说。

  他们走了一会儿，开始听到哗哗的水声。声音越来越大。

> "快到一条河了，"悟净说。

  小路绕过一座小山。不久，一行人看到了那条河。

> "我们怎么过河呀？"唐僧说。"河太宽了，我都看不到对岸。"

  悟空腾空而起，看了看四周。"附近有个小村庄，"他说。"也许那里有人有船。"

## ②

  一行人走到村里。街上空无一人。几个村民从窗户和门后偷偷张望，看起来很害怕。

  一个男人正在井边打水。

> "你好？"悟净打招呼。

  男人吓了一跳，水桶掉进了井里，转身问道："你们是谁？"

  唐僧走上前。"我来自大唐。我们要去西天拜佛求经。"

## ③

  男人放松了一些。

  "我们需要一条船过河，"八戒说。

> "我没有船，"男人说。"但村里也许有人有。请跟我来，我给你们沏茶。我叫陈澄。"

  一行人跟着陈澄回到家。他们坐下来，陈澄烧水泡了茶。

> "为什么这个村里的人看起来都这么害怕？"唐僧问。

  陈澄叹了口气，坐下来。他哭了起来。

> "好人啊，"唐僧说。"怎么了？我惹你不高兴了吗？"

> "不是，"陈澄流着泪说。"只是我们村的日子太苦了。"

## ④

  "几年前来了一个可怕的妖怪。他一直欺负我们，但现在更糟糕了。他逼我们给他送一个孩子。如果不送，他就会毁掉我们整个村子。明天早上，我必须把我儿子交给他。"

  陈澄又放声大哭起来。

  悟空偷偷笑了起来，随即哈哈大笑。

  唐僧怒视着悟空。

> "悟空！你笑什么？"

  八戒摇了摇头。"他太坏了，师父。您该念念紧箍咒了。"

  "不用念，"悟空还在笑。

## ⑤

  他对陈澄说："我不是在笑话您。我笑是因为今天是您最幸运的日子。"

  陈澄挑起眉毛。"哦，是吗？"

> "您可能没听说过我，"悟空说。"但我就是齐天大圣孙悟空。我当年打败过玉帝的整支天兵天将。明天我就能救您儿子的命。"

> "您是神仙吗？"陈澄问。

  "是的，一个非常厉害的神仙，"悟空说。"把您儿子带出来吧，我得看看他。"

  陈澄离开房间一会儿。回来的时候，身边跟着一个小男孩。

> "这是我儿子，"陈澄说。

  悟空绕着男孩走了一圈，仔细查看。最后，悟空抱起双臂说：

> "我有个计划。"

## ⑥

  第二天早上，男孩爬上了一个小平台。几个村民把平台抬到了河边。他们把平台放在地上，然后退开。四周安静了片刻。男孩独自坐在平台上。

  突然一朵乌云逼近。一个面目狰狞的妖怪从云中现身，长着鲨鱼般的牙齿。

  男孩对着妖怪笑了笑。

> "你好？"
