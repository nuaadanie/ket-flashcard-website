# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixteen: Who Will Come Get Sticks

## ①

  The monkey grabbed the sides of his head and fell over. His eyes bulged.

  "He is going to explode!" he cried. He saw the Tang Monk talking quietly.

  "What? What are you saying?" cried Wukong.

  The Tang Monk stopped talking and the pain stopped.

  "The Bodhisattva Guanyin taught me a spell," said the monk. "She gave me the headband to force you to be good."

  Wukong pulled at the headband. But it would not come off.

## ②

  "Good gosh!" cried the monkey. He stamped his foot. "You tricked me!"

  "It is for your own good," said the monk. "You were being violent."

  Wukong pulled out his iron bar and lunged at the Tang Monk. The monk recited the Tight Headband Spell again. Wukong fell to the ground and rolled around in pain.

  "My head hurts!" he cried. "Stop. I will not try to hurt you again."

  The Tang Monk stopped and the monkey stood up.

  "Now will you cook some rice?" asked the monk.

  "Start a fire first," said Wukong.

## ③

  The monkey waited until the Tang Monk had turned his back. Again, he raised the iron bar. But the Tang Monk saw Wukong's shadow.

  "Wukong!" cried the Tang Monk once again, and Wukong fell to the ground.

  The Tang Monk looked furious as he recited the spell. Wukong's head pounded.

  "Stop!" cried the monkey.

  The Tang Monk stopped reciting the spell.

  "Do not try to hit me again," said the monk. "If you do, I will recite the spell until your head really does explode."

  "I am sorry, Master," said Wukong. "I will be good."

## ④

  The two traveled for a few more weeks. They arrived at a wide river.

  "How will we cross this river?" asked the monk.

  "It is too bad you are not a spirit," said Wukong. "I can leap over this river, but I cannot take you with me."

  "Why not?" asked the Tang Monk.

  "You are a human," said the monkey. "So you must make the journey all by yourself. I can only protect you. I cannot carry you."

  "What will we do then?" asked the monk.

## ⑤

  Before Wukong could answer, the water swelled and a dragon leaped from it.

  "Look out!" cried the monkey.

  He yanked the Tang Monk off the horse as the dragon struck. But by the time Wukong reacted, it was too late. The dragon swallowed the horse and dived back under water.

# 🇨🇳 第十六章：谁愿来取经

## ①

  悟空双手抱住脑袋，摔倒在地。他的眼睛鼓得大大的。

  "他要爆炸了！"他大喊着，看见唐僧在一旁小声念着什么。

  "什么？你在念什么？"悟空叫道。

  唐僧收了咒，悟空也就不疼了。

  "是观音菩萨教了我一段咒，"和尚说，"她把头箍交给我，就是为了让你守规矩。"

  悟空用力去拽头箍，可是怎么也摘不下来。

## ②

  "天哪！"悟空大叫。他跺了跺脚。"你骗我！"

  "这是为了你好，"唐僧说，"你刚才又想动粗。"

  悟空掏出金箍棒，朝唐僧打去。唐僧又念起紧箍咒来。悟空摔在地上，疼得直打滚。

  "疼死我了！"他叫道，"别念了，我再也不敢伤害你了。"

  唐僧停了咒，悟空站了起来。

  "这下你肯去煮饭了吧？"唐僧问。

  "你先去生火，"悟空嘟囔着。

## ③

  悟空等唐僧转过身去，又偷偷举起了金箍棒。可是唐僧从地上看到了悟空的影子。

  唐僧大喝一声，再次念起咒语，悟空又摔倒在地。

  唐僧满脸怒容，不停地念着咒。悟空头痛欲裂。

  "住口！"悟空大叫。

  唐僧停了咒。

  "你下次再敢打我，"和尚说，"我就把咒一直念到你脑袋真的炸开为止。"

  "师父，我错了，"悟空说，"我一定乖。"

## ④

  两个又走了几个星期，来到了一条宽阔的大河前。

  "这河怎么过呢？"唐僧问。

  "你不是神仙，真不方便，"悟空说，"我一个筋斗就能翻过去，可是带不了你。"

  "为什么？"唐僧问。

  "你是凡人，"悟空说，"取经的路只能你自己一步一步走。我只能保护你，不能背你。"

  "那怎么办？"唐僧发愁了。

## ⑤

  悟空还没来得及回答，河水忽然翻涌，一条白龙从水中腾空而起。

  "小心！"悟空大喊。

  他一把拽下唐僧，可是已经来不及了。白龙一口吞下了坐骑白马，转身又一头扎回了水里。
