# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred and One: Arrested

## ①

> "This is ridiculous," said the Tang monk. "Why are we under arrest?"

  Soldiers were bringing the travelers back to the town.

> "You robbed Squire Kou's house last night," said one of the soldiers.

> "What?" cried Bajie. "We would never rob somebody. Especially not the Squire. He was kind to us."

## ②

> "You had all the stolen items," replied the soldier. "You also had the two horses that were stolen from his stable."

> "We got everything from the real robbers," said the Tang monk. "We were going to return it all to Squire Kou."

  The soldier laughed.

> "Oh, you got everything from the real robbers. Tell me — where are the real robbers now?"

## ③

> "I let them go," said Wukong.

  The soldier laughed again.

> "Oh, that was very kind of you."

  Wukong could tell that the soldier didn't believe him. By now they had reached the town. A crowd of people had gathered to watch. The soldiers brought the travelers into the courthouse.

## ④

  The judge frowned at the monk and the others.

  *Clang!* The lock shut on the cell door.

> "This is a mistake," the Tang monk pleaded. "We can't go to jail. We're making an important journey to visit Buddha."

## ⑤

  The judge shook his head.

> "Buddha doesn't allow killers in the Western Paradise."

> "Killers?" said Wujing. "What are you talking about?"

> "You killed Squire Kou," said the judge.

  The Tang monk's face went pale.

> "The Squire is dead?"

## ⑥

  The judge narrowed his eyes at the monk. The travelers were shoved into a jail cell and the door was slammed shut.

> "This is terrible," the monk started to cry.

> "Lady Kou framed us," said Wujing. "But why?"

> "I'll find out tonight," promised Wukong.

## ⑦

  The Tang monk looked grateful.

> "We must prove our innocence."

  That night Wukong turned into a bee and flew out of the jail to Squire Kou's house. He found Lady Kou mourning over a coffin. The monkey changed back into himself and hid behind a curtain.

  Imitating the Squire's voice, he said:

## ⑧

> "My lady, it's me — your husband."

  Lady Kou's eyes went wide.

> "Is it really you?"

> "Yes," said Wukong.

> "Why did you tell the judge that the Tang monk and his companions killed me?"

## ⑨

  Lady Kou hesitated.

> "I was angry with them. You wanted them to stay here, but they insisted on leaving. That was an insult to us."

> "You have done a terrible thing!" thundered Wukong.

  The walls of the house shook and Lady Kou trembled.

## ⑩

  The monkey continued.

> "Tomorrow you must tell the judge that the Tang monk and his companions are innocent. If you don't, I will haunt you for the rest of your life."

  Tears poured from the woman's eyes.

> "I will talk to the judge tomorrow. I promise. Please don't haunt me."

## ⑪

  Wukong flew back to the courthouse. He saw the judge sitting at a desk reading some papers. Wukong flew over the roof, then he turned into an enormous spirit body and lowered one foot.

  When the judge saw the giant foot, he gasped. The papers fell from his hands.

## ⑫

  Wukong's voice boomed.

> "I am the wandering spirit, and I will send you to see Buddha! Lady Kou lied to you. The Tang monk did not kill Squire Kou. He is on an important journey. If you don't free him and his companions, I will lower my other foot. Then I will stomp all over this town until there is nothing more."

# 🇨🇳 第一百零一章：被捕

## ①

> "这太荒唐了，"唐僧说，"为什么要把我们抓起来？"

  士兵们押着旅人返回镇上。

> "你们昨晚抢劫了寇员外的家，"一个士兵说。

> "什么？"八戒大叫，"我们才不会抢劫。更不会抢劫寇员外。他对我们那么好。"

## ②

> "赃物全在你们手上，"士兵回答说，"从他们家偷的两匹马也在你们手上。"

> "这些东西是从真正的强盗那里得来的，"唐僧说，"我们正准备把东西全部还给寇员外。"

  士兵大笑。

> "哦，从真正的强盗那里得来的。那我倒要问——真正的强盗现在在哪里？"

## ③

> "我放了他们。"悟空说。

  士兵又笑了。

> "哦，你可真够仁慈的呀。"

  悟空看得出士兵根本不信他们。到了镇上，街上已经围满了看热闹的人。士兵把旅人押进了公堂。

## ④

  县官一脸怒色地瞪着唐僧等人。

  锁链锁上了牢房的门。

> "这真是一场误会，"唐僧恳求说，"我们不能坐牢啊。我们正在赶路去拜见佛祖。"

## ⑤

  县官摇了摇头。

> "佛祖可不容许杀人犯进入西天。"

> "杀人犯？"悟净说，"你在说什么？"

> "你们杀了寇员外，"县官说。

  唐僧脸色苍白。

> "员外死了？"

## ⑥

  县官瞪视着唐僧，旅人被推进了牢房，牢门"砰"的一声关上了。

> "这太糟了，"唐僧哭了起来。

> "是寇夫人诬陷了我们，"悟净说，"可她为什么要这样做？"

> "我今晚就去查明真相。"悟空说。

## ⑦

  唐僧感激地看着悟空。

> "我们必须证明自己的清白。"

  到了夜里，悟空变成一只蜜蜂飞出牢房，飞到了寇员外家。他看见寇夫人正对着一口棺材哭泣。猴子又变回原形，躲在帘子后面。

  他模仿着员外的声音说：

## ⑧

> "夫人，是我——你的丈夫。"

  寇夫人大惊失色。

> "真的是你吗？"

> "是我，"悟空说。

> "你为什么去公堂对大人说唐僧师徒杀了我？"

## ⑨

  寇夫人犹豫了。

> "我对他们生气。你想留他们多住几天，他们却偏要走。这不是不给我们面子吗？"

> "你做了一件大恶事！"悟空声如雷霆。

  房屋四壁摇晃，寇夫人吓得浑身发抖。

## ⑩

  猴子继续说道。

> "明天你必须到县官面前说唐僧师徒是清白的。如果你不去，我下半辈子都会缠着你。"

  泪水从寇夫人眼中滚滚流出。

> "我明天就去向县官禀明真相。我保证。求求你别来缠我。"

## ⑪

  悟空飞回县衙。他看见县官正坐在桌子前看文书。悟空从屋顶上飞过，然后变为一个巨大的灵体形象，把一只大脚伸到了屋顶上。

  县官看到那只大脚，倒抽了一口冷气。手中的文书撒落一地。

## ⑫

  悟空声音如雷。

> "我是游荡的英魂，我要带你去见佛祖！是寇夫人对你说了谎。唐僧并未杀害寇员外，他正在去西天取经的路上。如果你不立刻释放唐僧师徒，我就把另一只脚也踩下来。我会踏平这座城镇，不留一物！"
