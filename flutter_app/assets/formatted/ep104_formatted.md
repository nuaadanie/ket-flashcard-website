# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred and Four: The Tang Monk and the River

## ①

  Wukong and his companions walked through the Western Paradise. The road wove through a field of flowers and towards the Spirit Mountain.

  Bajie looked up at the huge mountain and shook his head.

> "Will it be easy to climb that?"

  Wukong laughed.

> "Don't ask me to carry you."

## ②

  The travelers soon arrived at a river with a strong current. Waves crashed into jagged rocks, spraying water everywhere.

> "This river looks dangerous," said Wujing. "But we must cross it to get to the Spirit Mountain."

  Bajie sighed.

> "We will never be able to cross this river safely. Let's just go home."

> "Nonsense," said Wukong. "Look over there."

## ③

  The monkey pointed upstream. A thin log lay across the river. It wobbled and shook as the water rushed under it. The Tang monk looked at the log.

> "Don't be silly, Wukong. We can't walk across that log. It's too thin, and it looks quite slippery."

> "But that log is the only way to reach the Spirit Mountain," said Wukong. "You must believe that it's safe. Watch me."

## ④

  The monkey hopped onto the log, ran across the river, and ran back.

> "See?" he said. "It's safe."

  Bajie snorted.

> "You don't weigh much, Wukong. But if I step on that log, it will break."

> "I believe Wukong," said Wujing. "We must walk across the log. Follow me, Master," said Wukong.

## ⑤

  He pulled the Tang monk towards the log. The monk trembled with fear.

> "I'll fall into the river and drown!" said the Tang monk.

> "Let's just fly over the river," suggested Bajie. "We can carry Master and the horse."

> "No," insisted Wukong.

## ⑥

> "The Tang monk must make the entire journey by himself. We're here to protect him, not to carry him. Buddha won't allow Master to have the true scriptures if he doesn't take every step himself. He can't take every step himself..."

## ⑦

  Just then an old man appeared in a boat.

> "I can take you across the river," said the man.

> "Thank you," said the Tang monk.

  He walked to the riverbank.

> "My companion wanted me to walk across that log, but —"

  The monk stopped.

> "There's something wrong with your boat. It has no bottom. Water is flowing right through it."

## ⑧

> "The boat is safe," said the man. "Please climb in. Buddha is waiting."

  With a bit more hesitation, the Tang monk stepped into the boat. He was shocked to find that he didn't sink. He sat on the edge of the boat as Wujing led the horse aboard. Bajie and Wukong followed. The boat glided swiftly across the river.

## ⑨

  Suddenly there was a splash. The Tang monk looked at the water and saw a body floating downstream. He screamed.

> "A man is drowning!"

  Wukong put his hand on the monk's shoulder and smiled.

> "It's you, Master."

## ⑩

> "Who asked?" said the monk. "Me?"

> "Well, Wukong is right!" cried Bajie. "It is you. Look again, Master," said Wujing, smiling.

## ⑪

  The Tang monk looked at the body more carefully. It was wearing the same Buddhist robe that he was wearing.

> "It's me," said the monk quietly. "But how?"

> "You got rid of your mortal body, Master," said Wukong. "You're a spirit now. You're one of us."

  Bajie beamed.

## ⑫

  The boat soon reached the other riverbank and the travelers stepped out. The boat and the old man vanished.

  The Tang monk laughed in amazement.

> "I feel so alive," he said. "I feel like I could run right up this mountain."

> "Good," said Bajie. "Perhaps you could carry me."

  Everybody laughed as they continued up the mountain.

# 🇨🇳 第一百零四章：唐僧与渡河

## ①

  悟空和徒伴们走在西天的路上。小路穿过一片花海，通向灵山。

  八戒抬头望着巨大的山峰，摇了摇头。

> "那山好爬吗？"

  悟空哈哈大笑。

> "可别指望我背你啊。"

## ②

  旅人们很快来到了一条水流湍急的大河边，波浪拍打着嶙峋的巨石，水花四溅。

> "这条河看起来很危险，"悟净说，"但我们必须过了河才能到灵山。"

  八戒叹了口气。

> "我们永远也过不去。还是回家算了。"

> "胡说，"悟空说，"看那边。"

## ③

  猴子朝上游指去。一根细木头横跨在河面上。急流从下面冲过，木头晃晃悠悠。唐僧看了看那根木头。

> "悟净，别开玩笑了。这么细一根木头怎么走得过去？而且看起来滑得很。"

> "但这是通往灵山的唯一道路，"悟空说，"你一定要相信它是安全的。看我走给你看。"

## ④

  猴子跳上木头，跑过了河又跑回来。

> "看到了吧？"他说，"安全得很。"

  八戒哼了一声。

> "你这么轻，没问题的。可我要是一脚踩上去，这根木头就断了。"

> "我相信悟空，"悟净说，"我们必须从这根木头上走过去。"

## ⑤

  他拉着唐僧朝木头走去。唐僧吓得浑身发抖。

> "我会掉进河里淹死的！"唐僧说。

> "我们直接飞过去吧，"八戒提议说，"我们带着师父和马一起飞。"

> "不行，"悟空坚持道。

## ⑥

> "唐僧必须自己走完这全程。我们是来保护他的，不是来背他的。如果师父不自己走完每一步，佛祖是不会让他拿到真经的。"

## ⑦

  就在此时，一位老者驾着一叶小舟出现了。

> "我可以载你们过河，"老者说。

> "谢谢您。"唐僧说着走到了河岸边。

> "我的同伴想让我从那根木头上走过去，可是——"

  唐僧顿住了。

> "你的船没有底啊。水直接从船底穿过来了。"

## ⑧

> "船是安全的，"老者说，"请上来吧。佛祖还在等着呢。"

  唐僧犹豫了一下，踏进了船里。他惊讶地发现自己并没有沉下去。他坐在船边，悟净牵着马上了船。八戒和悟空也跟着上船。小船轻快地滑过了河面。

## ⑨

  突然传来"扑通"一声。唐僧往河里一看，看见一具尸体顺流漂下。他尖叫起来。

> "有人淹死了！"

  悟空把手搭在唐僧肩上，微微笑了。

> "师父，那就是你。"

## ⑩

> "谁呀？"唐僧说，"我？"

> "没错，悟空说得对！"八戒大叫道，"那就是你。师父，您再仔细看看，"悟净笑着说。

## ⑪

  唐僧再仔细一看那具尸体。尸体身上穿的正和他一样的僧袍。

> "真的是我，"唐僧轻声说，"可这是怎么回事？"

> "师父，您已经脱去了凡胎肉体，"悟空说，"您现在是仙体了。和我们一样了。"

  八戒乐开了花。

## ⑫

  小船很快到了对岸，旅人们踏出了小船。小舟和老者都不见了踪影。

  唐僧惊奇地大笑了起来。

> "我感觉身轻如燕，"他说，"我觉得自己能一口气跑上这座山。"

> "那太好了，"八戒说，"说不定您能背我上去。"

  大家哈哈大笑，继续向山上走去。
