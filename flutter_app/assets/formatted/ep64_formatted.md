# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixty-Four: Magnificent Silk Vests

## ①

> "I hope that monkey finds a lot of food," said Bajie. "I'm hungry."

  The Tang Monk shivered and hugged himself.

> "I'm so cold," he said.

  He looked up at the monastery again and then back at his companions.

> "Do you two believe there are demons up there?"

  Wujing stroked his chin.

> "I'm not sure. Wukong believed it though."

## ②

> "Kai," Bajie snorted. "Wukong is too careful. He's always worried that we'll bump into a demon."

  His stomach grumbled.

> "Oh, I'm starving."

  Wujing looked at the monastery again.

> "That place looks perfectly safe to me."

## ③

  Bajie nodded.

> "It looks warm too. Maybe the monks there will take us in."

> "It would be nice to get out of this cold," said the Tang Monk.

> "That settles it," said the pig. "Who cares what that monkey thinks? What does he know? Let's go up there, get warm, and eat some food."

  The Tang Monk climbed back onto the horse and the three travelers headed toward the monastery.

## ④

  When they got there, no people were around.

> "This place is deserted," said Wujing.

  Bajie knocked on the door but no one answered.

> "Hello!" the pig called. "Is anybody in there?"

  Again, there was no answer.

## ⑤

  Bajie looked at his companions.

> "Maybe the monks didn't hear me."

  His eyes lit up.

> "Maybe they're all in the kitchen cooking food. With noodles boiling and vegetables sizzling, a kitchen can be quite noisy."

  The pig's stomach growled again.

> "I'll go in and find out."

  He opened the door and stepped inside. The monastery was dark and silent.

## ⑥

> "I don't hear anyone cooking," Bajie said to himself. "Maybe there isn't anyone here."

  He walked down a long hallway past several dark rooms. He called again. Then he saw a room that was lit up. He went inside.

  Bright sunlight was streaming through a window and onto a table. On the table were three silk vests.

## ⑦

> "Those vests look magnificent," said the pig.

  He shivered.

> "They also look warm."

  He looked around. He grabbed the vests and hurried back outside.

> "Nobody's in there," he said to the monk and Wujing. "When I found these vests."

  Wujing grabbed a vest.

> "Great. I'm freezing."

## ⑧

  Bajie handed a vest to the Tang Monk.

> "Here you go, Master."

  The monk frowned.

> "I won't wear that. You stole it."

> "I didn't steal it," said Bajie. "I borrowed it."

  The monk glared at him.

> "You must return those vests right away."

## ⑨

> "We can return the vests before we leave," offered Wujing. "Right, Bajie."

> "Oh, I'm sure," said the pig. "We can do that."

> "You two are terrible," said the Tang Monk. "We'll never get the true scriptures from Buddha if you steal."

## ⑩

  Bajie shrugged.

> "Master, you don't have to wear one of the vests. But I'm cold."

  He put his arm through the armhole. Wujing did the same. Bajie put his other arm through. Wujing began buttoning his vest.

  Suddenly they both cried out.

> "It's tight," cried Bajie. "I can't breathe."

> "I can't breathe," cried Wujing.

  They both fell to the ground trying desperately to take the vests off.

## ⑪

> "This vest won't come off," cried the pig.

> "Who stole my vests?" a voice shouted.

  A demon with one big horn rushed over. He looked at Bajie and Wujing and then turned to the Tang Monk.

> "Who are you?" demanded the demon.

## ⑫

> "I'm from the Tang Empire," said the monk, shaking with fear. "I'm traveling to the west for..."

  The demon clapped his hands together.

> "You're the Tang Monk!"

# 🇨🇳 第六十四章：华美丝袍


## ①

> "希望那只猴子能找到很多吃的，"八戒说，"我都饿了。"

  唐僧打了个寒战，抱紧了自己。

> "我好冷啊，"他说。

  他又抬头看了看山上的寺院，然后把目光转回同伴身上。

> "你们俩真的觉得上面有妖怪吗？"

  悟净摸了摸下巴。

> "不好说，不过悟空是这么认为的。"

## ②

> "切，"八戒哼了一声，"悟空也太小心了，他总是担心我们会碰到妖怪。"

  他的肚子咕噜噜叫了起来。

> "哎呀，我快饿扁了。"

  悟净又看了看那座寺院。

> "我看那里挺安全的。"

## ③

  八戒点了点头。

> "看着也挺暖和的，说不定那里的僧人会收留我们。"

> "能离开这冰天雪地就好了，"唐僧说。

> "就这么定了，"八戒说，"管那只猴子怎么想呢？他懂什么？咱们上去暖和暖和，吃点东西。"

  唐僧翻上马背，三个行者朝寺院走去。

## ④

  到了那里才发现，一个人也没有。

> "这地方空荡荡的，"悟净说。

  八戒敲了敲门，没人回应。

> "有人吗？"八戒喊道，"里面有人吗？"

  还是没有回答。

## ⑤

  八戒看了看同伴们。

> "可能是和尚们没听见。"

  他眼睛一亮。

> "说不定他们都在厨房做饭呢。面条在锅里滚着，蔬菜在锅里滋滋响，厨房里应该很热闹。"

  八戒的肚子又咕噜噜叫了。

> "我进去看看。"

  他推开门走了进去。寺院里又黑又安静。

## ⑥

> "我好像没听到有人在做饭，"八戒自言自语道，"也许这里根本没人。"

  他沿着一条长廊走着，经过了好几间黑漆漆的房间。他又喊了一声，然后看到一间亮着灯的房间。他走了进去。

  明亮的阳光透过窗户照在一张桌子上。桌子上放着三件丝绸背心。

## ⑦

> "这些背心看起来真漂亮，"八戒说。

  他冷得发抖。

> "看起来也很暖和。"

  他四下看了看，抓起背心就跑出门外。

> "里面没有人，"他对唐僧和悟净说，"我找到了这些背心。"

  悟净抓起一件背心。

> "太好了，我快冻僵了。"

## ⑧

  八戒递给唐僧一件背心。

> "师父，给您。"

  唐僧皱了皱眉头。

> "我不穿，这是你偷来的。"

> "我没有偷，"八戒说，"我只是借来用用。"

  唐僧瞪着他。

> "你必须马上把背心还回去。"

## ⑨

> "我们走之前再还回去就是了，"悟净提议道，"对吧，八戒？"

> "当然啦，"八戒说，"我们肯定会还的。"

> "你们俩太不像话了，"唐僧说，"如果偷东西，我们永远也取不到佛祖的真经。"

## ⑩

  八戒耸了耸肩。

> "师父，您可以不穿背心，可是我好冷啊。"

  他把胳膊伸进了袖口。悟净也照做了。八戒把另一只胳膊也伸了进去。悟净开始扣背心的扣子。

  突然两个人都叫了起来。

> "好紧啊，"八戒叫道，"我喘不过气了。"

> "我也透不过气了，"悟净也叫道。

  两个人都倒在地上，拼命想脱掉背心。

## ⑪

> "这件背心脱不下来了，"八戒叫道。

> "谁偷了我的背心？"一个声音大吼道。

  一个长着一根大角的妖怪冲了过来。他看了看八戒和悟净，然后转向唐僧。

> "你们是什么人？"妖怪质问道。

## ⑫

> "我来自大唐，"唐僧浑身发抖说，"我前往西天去……"

  妖怪拍了一下手。

> "你就是唐僧！"
