
# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixty-Nine: The Stabbing Pain

## ①

  That woman was a demon! cried Wukong, and she kidnapped the Tang monk! He leaped into the air, and Bajie and Wujing followed.

## ②

  In the distance, the monkey saw dark clouds speeding toward a mountain.

> "That must be her," said Wukong.

> "Quickly!" said Wujing. "We must catch her!"

## ③

  They raced after the cloud, which disappeared when it reached the mountain. They soon arrived and found a cave with its door closed. Wukong pounded on the cave door.

> "Open up!" he yelled.

## ④

  The door flew open, and the woman came out.

> "Who are you?" demanded the woman. "Why are you pounding on my door?"

> "Release our master!" shouted Wukong, ignoring her questions.

  The woman laughed.

> "Don't tell me what to do."

  The monkey attacked. The woman spun around fast, blocking his iron bar and striking back with fury.

> "You shouldn't have come here," she shouted as she fought. "Even the Jade Emperor is afraid of me."

## ⑤

  Bajie tried to hit her with his rake, but missed. The woman was now spinning faster and faster.

> "She's faster than a tornado!" cried the pig.

  Wukong leaped at the woman. Suddenly he felt a stabbing pain.

> "Ah ha ha!" cried the monkey, grabbing his head. "It hurts! It hurts!"

  He ran down the mountain. Bajie and Wujing stared after Wukong in shock. The woman went back into her cave.

## ⑥

> "What happened to Wukong?" asked the pig. "Nothing hurts him except for the tight headband spell."

> "I don't know," said Wujing, scratching his head.

## ⑦

  They went down the mountain and found the monkey crouching by a stream. He was still holding his head.

> "Are you alright?" asked Wujing.

> "That woman stabbed me in the head," moaned Wukong. "The pain is horrible."

  Bajie snorted.

> "You're always bragging about how tough you are. Now you're crying about getting stabbed in the head. Pathetic!"

  Shouted the monkey angrily.

## ⑧

  Wujing moved closer.

> "Let me see where she stabbed you."

  Wukong lowered his hands. There was no injury.

> "Ha ha ha!" Bajie laughed. "Gosh, he's fine! He just wants us to feel bad for him."

## ⑨

  The monkey glared at the pig.

> "I'm telling you, it hurts!"

  He took a deep breath.

> "The pain is going away now, though. Let's go back up there and fight her again."

## ⑩

  The three companions returned to the cave.

> "Wukong, you stand back," said Bajie, laughing. "I don't want you getting hurt again."

  Wukong clenched his teeth. The pig hit the door with his rake. Smash! The door exploded. The woman ran outside.

> "You smashed my door!" she shouted.

> "Release our master!" shouted the pig.

  The woman spun around faster and faster.

## ⑪

  Bajie and Wujing tried to strike her with their weapons, but the woman was too fast. Wukong raised his iron bar, waiting for a good moment to strike. Suddenly Bajie cried out.

## ⑫

  He covered his nose and ran back down the mountain. The woman spun toward Wukong and Wujing, and they turned and ran back down as well. Bajie was huddled on the ground, tears streaming from his eyes.

> "It hurt so badly!"

  Wukong laughed.

> "No laughing, Bajie!" cried Bajie. "She stabbed my nose!"

> "You deserved it," said the monkey. "You didn't believe me when I got hurt."

## ⑬

> "Stop arguing," said Wujing. "We still have to save the Tang monk."

> "You're right," said Wukong. "Let's go back up there."

  A voice called out.

> "Wait."

  It was Guanyin. She was walking towards the group.

> "Beware of that scorpion spirit."

# 🇨🇳 第六十九章：刺骨之痛

## ①

  "那女人是个妖怪！"悟空大叫道，"她把唐僧掳走了！"

  他纵身一跃跳到空中，八戒和悟净紧随其后。

## ②

  远远地，悟空看见一团乌云正向一座山疾飞而去。

> "一定是她，"悟空说。

> "快！"悟净喊道，"我们得追上她！"

## ③

  三人追着那团云，云到了山前便消失了。他们很快赶到山前，发现了一个洞穴，洞门紧闭。悟空用力捶打洞门。

> "开门！"他大喊道。

## ④

  门猛地打开，那女人走了出来。

> "你们是谁？"那女人厉声问道，"为什么砸我的门？"

> "放了我们师父！"悟空不理会她的问题，大声喝道。

  那女人哈哈大笑。

> "轮不到你来命令我。"

  猴子挥棒攻击。那女人迅速转身，挡开铁棒反打回来，怒不可遏。

> "你们不该来这里！"她一边打一边喊。"就连玉帝都怕我！"

## ⑤

  八戒举起钉耙打她，却扑了个空。那女人旋转得越来越快。

> "她比龙卷风还快！"八戒惊叫道。

  悟空纵身扑向那女人，突然感到一阵刺骨的疼痛。

> "哈哈哈！"悟空抱着头叫道，"好疼！好疼！"

  他转身跑下了山。八戒和悟净望着悟空的背影，愣住了。那女人退回了洞中。

## ⑥

> "悟空怎么了？"八戒问道，"除了紧箍咒，没有什么能伤害他啊。"

> "我也不知道。"悟净挠了挠头说。

## ⑦

  他们下了山，发现悟空蹲在一条小溪边，双手还抱着头。

> "你没事吧？"悟净问。

> "那女人用兵器刺了我的头，"悟空痛苦地说，"太疼了，疼得厉害！"

  八戒哼了一声。

> "你老是吹嘘自己有多厉害。现在被刺了头倒哭起来了，真没用！"

  悟空怒吼了一声。

## ⑧

  悟净走近了些。

> "让我看看她刺了你哪里。"

  悟空放下双手，头上并没有任何伤口。

> "哈哈哈！"八戒大笑起来，"他好好的！只是想让我们同情他。"

## ⑨

  悟空瞪了八戒一眼。

> "我告诉你们，真的很疼！"

  他深吸了一口气。

> "不过现在疼得不那么厉害了。我们回去再跟她打一场。"

## ⑩

  三人回到了洞穴前。

> "悟空，你站远点，"八戒得意地说，"我可不想你又受伤。"

  悟空咬紧牙关。八戒用钉耙猛砸洞门，"轰"的一声，门被炸开了。那女人跑了出来。

> "你们砸了我的门！"她大喊道。

> "放了我们师父！"八戒喝道。

  那女人又飞快地旋转起来。

## ⑪

  八戒和悟净举起兵器进攻，但那女人实在太快了。悟空高举金箍棒，等待出击的好时机。突然，八戒惨叫了一声。

## ⑫

  他捂着鼻子往山下跑。那女人转身冲向悟空和悟净，两人也转身往山下逃去。八戒蜷缩在地上，眼泪从脸上流下来。

> "疼死了！"

  悟空大笑起来。

> "不许笑！"八戒哭喊道，"她刺了我的鼻子！"

> "活该，"悟空说，"谁叫你刚才不信我。"

## ⑬

> "别吵了，"悟净说，"我们还得去救唐僧呢。"

> "你说得对，"悟空说，"我们再去一次。"

  一个声音从远处传来。

> "等等。"

  是观音菩萨，她正朝他们走来。

> "当心那个蝎子精，她很危险。"
