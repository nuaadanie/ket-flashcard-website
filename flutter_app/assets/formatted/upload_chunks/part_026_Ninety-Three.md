
# Journey to the West · Chapter Ninety-Three: The Elephant Demon

## ①

  The lion demon rubbed his aching mouth.

> "Let's leave the Tang monk alone. Wukong is dangerous."

  The raptor nodded. The elephant demon waved his hand.

> "I'm not afraid of that silly monkey. I have a perfect plan to defeat him and capture the Tang monk."

## ②

  The lion shook his head.

> "The last time you had a plan, I wound up with a broken tooth."

  The elephant snorted.

> "Just leave everything to me."

  Wukong returned to his companions. He heard Bajie and Wujing arguing.

## ③

> "We can't go home!" shouted Wujing. "We're on an important journey."

> "But Wukong got eaten by the lion demon," said Bajie. "Without Wukong, we'll never get to the Western Paradise."

> "Here I am!" said the monkey.

  Everyone spun around.

> "You're alive!" cried the Tang monk.

## ④

> "Yes, I am," said Wukong. "I beat up that lion's insides and broke one of his teeth. The demons are scared of me now. They promised to let us cross their mountain safely."

> "That's wonderful," said the Tang monk.

  Wujing helped the monkey climb onto the horse. The other travelers started walking. They hadn't gone far when they saw a large figure ahead blocking the road.

## ⑤

> "That's the elephant demon," said Wukong with a frown.

> "What's he doing here?"

> "We want the Tang monk!" shouted the elephant.

  Wukong turned to Bajie.

## ⑥

> "I've been dealing with these demons all day. It's your turn to fight them."

  Bajie sighed.

> "Okay."

  Waving his rake, the pig charged toward the elephant.

> "You'll never harm my master!"

  The elephant blocked the rake, spun around and struck back. Bajie jumped out of the way.

## ⑦

  The elephant jerked his head and his trunk shot out. Before Bajie could duck, it wrapped tightly around his body. The elephant ran off with Bajie.

> "The monkey turned to his companions. You two wait here, while I go save the pig."

  Wukong returned to the demon's cave and turned into a fly. He found Bajie deep inside the cave. The pig was tied up, floating in a tub of salt water. Wukong changed back into himself and untied his companion.

## ⑧

> "I'm done," said Bajie. "Those demons were planning to pickle me."

> "You're welcome, Bajie," said Wukong. "Now let's go."

  The elephant's trunk wrapped tightly around Wukong's chest. Then the elephant stepped out of a dark corner.

## ⑨

> "I knew you'd come to save your friend," said the elephant. "Now you can't protect the Tang monk. My trunk is very strong."

  Wukong's arms were still free. He pulled out his iron bar, which instantly grew. With all his strength, he slammed it down on the elephant's trunk.

## ⑩

> "Ow, ow, ow!" the elephant cried.

  He released Wukong and fell backwards. His trunk had a horrible purple bruise. Wukong moved toward the elephant, his iron bar raised high. The elephant's eyes went wide.

## ⑪

> "Please don't hurt me anymore. We'll let the Tang monk cross the mountain."

  The monkey glared at the elephant for a moment. Then he put the iron bar away.

> "You'd better not trick us again."

  Wukong left the cave and returned to their companions. The group continued over the mountain.

## ⑫

  As the travelers came around a bend, they saw the lion and the elephant. The two demons were blocking the road. Wukong strode toward them.

> "What are you doing? You promised to --"

  Wukong spun around. The raptor had grabbed the monkey's companions with its talons. With a powerful flap of its wings, the giant bird flew away.

# 🇨🇳 第九十三章：大象精

## ①

  狮子精揉着疼痛的嘴巴。

> "我们别动唐僧了，悟空太危险。"

  猛禽精点了点头。大象精挥了挥手。

> "我才不怕那只傻猴子呢。我有完美的计划能打败他、抓住唐僧。"

## ②

  狮子摇了摇头。

> "上次你有计划，害我丢了一颗牙。"

  大象哼了一声。

> "这事就交给我吧。"

  悟空回到了同伴们身边，听到八戒和悟净在争吵。

## ③

> "我们不能回家！"悟净大喊，"我们在完成一项重要的使命。"

> "可是悟空被狮子精吃了，"八戒说，"没有悟空，我们永远到不了西天。"

> "我在这儿呢！"猴子说。

  所有人都转过身来。

> "你还活着！"唐僧大叫。

## ④

> "是的，我还活着，"悟空说，"我把狮子肚子里面打了个稀巴烂，还打掉他一颗牙。妖怪们现在怕了我。他们答应让我们安全过这座山。"

> "太好了，"唐僧说。

  悟净帮猴子上马。其他旅人继续前行。走了不久，他们看见前面路上有个庞大的身影挡着路。

## ⑤

> "那是大象精，"悟空皱着眉说。

> "他在这里干什么？"

> "我们要唐僧！"大象大喊。

  悟空转向八戒。

## ⑥

> "我跟这些妖怪斗了一整天了，这回该你上了。"

  八戒叹了口气。

> "好吧。"

  他挥舞着钉耙朝大象冲去。

> "你休想伤害我师父！"

  大象挡住了钉耙，转身反击。八戒跳开躲过。

## ⑦

  大象猛地一甩头，鼻子伸了出去。八戒还没来得及躲避，象鼻就把他卷住了。大象抓起八戒就跑。

  猴子转向同伴们。

> "你们两个在这里等我，我去救猪。"

  悟空回到妖怪洞，变成了一只苍蝇。他在洞的深处找到了八戒。猪被绑着，泡在一缸盐水里。悟空变回原形，解开了同伴的绳子。

## ⑧

> "可算出来了，"八戒说，"那些妖怪想腌了我。"

> "不客气，八戒，"悟空说，"我们走吧。"

  大象的鼻子紧紧缠住了悟空的胸口。大象从暗处走了出来。

## ⑨

> "我就知道你会来救你的朋友，"大象说，"现在你可保护不了唐僧了。我的鼻子很厉害。"

  悟空的双臂还能动。他掏出金箍棒，金箍棒瞬间变大。他用尽全力朝大象鼻子打去。

## ⑩

> "哎呀呀！"大象惨叫。

  他放开了悟空，向后倒去。他的鼻子肿了一大块紫青色。悟空逼近大象，金箍棒高高举起。大象瞪大了眼睛。

## ⑪

> "求你别再打我了！我们让唐僧过山。"

  猴子狠狠瞪了大象一眼，然后把金箍棒收了回去。

> "你们最好别再耍花招。"

  悟空走出洞府，回到了同伴身边。队伍继续翻山。

## ⑫

  拐过一个弯道，他们看见狮子和大象。两个妖怪又挡住了路。悟空大步走向他们。

> "你们干什么？你们明明答应过——"

  悟空猛地转身。猛禽精用爪子把他两个同伴抓走了。巨鸟用力一扇翅膀，飞上了天空。
