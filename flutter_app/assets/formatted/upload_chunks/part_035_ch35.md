
# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred and Two: The Prisoners Are Released

## ①

  Lady Kou ran into the courthouse early the next morning. With tears in her eyes, she told the judge that she had lied the day before.

> "The Tang monk and his companions are innocent," Lady Kou sniffed. "Please release them."

  The judge nodded.

> "I was going to release the prisoners this morning."

  He explained how the wandering spirit had visited him the night before.

## ②

  Lady Kou gasped.

> "My husband visited me last night!"

> "We must release the prisoners at once."

  They hurried to the Tang monk's cell. A guard swung open the cell door.

> "I'm glad to be out of there," said Wujing.

> "Me too," said Bajie with a yawn. "The beds were very uncomfortable."

## ③

  Lady Kou bowed to the Tang monk.

> "I framed you for killing my husband. It was a terrible thing to do. Please forgive me."

  The Tang monk put his hand on Wukong's shoulder.

> "Don't yell, Wukong."

  He looked at Lady Kou.

> "We forgive you. The Squire was a kind man. His death is a terrible tragedy."

## ④

  Wukong rubbed his chin.

> "I can probably bring him back to life."

  Lady Kou and the judge were shocked.

> "Can you really do that?" asked Lady Kou.

## ⑤

  The Tang monk smiled.

> "Yes, he can. Wukong brought the King of the Black Rooster Kingdom back to life."

  He turned to the monkey.

> "The sage Laozi helped you that time. Will you ask him for help again?"

> "No," said Wukong. "Laozi doesn't really like me. I'll go to the Land of Darkness and talk to the judge of the dead instead."

## ⑥

  When Wukong entered the palace of darkness, the judge of the dead frowned.

> "Why are you here, Wukong? You already made yourself immortal by crossing out your name in the directory."

  Wukong laughed.

> "I wondered if you would remember that. I'm looking for Squire Kou's name."

> "Squire Kou!" The judge opened the directory and flipped through some pages.

## ⑦

> "Ah, yes. He died two days ago. You know — he works for the Bodhisattva Ksitigarbha in the Jade Palace. It's here in the Land of Darkness."

  Wukong went to the Jade Palace.

> "Greetings, Sun Wukong," said the Bodhisattva.

> "Greetings, Bodhisattva." The monkey bowed.

## ⑧

> "Two nights ago, a friend of mine — Squire Kou — was killed up there on Earth. I would like to bring him back."

  The Bodhisattva raised an eyebrow.

> "You want him to return to life?"

> "Yes," said Wukong. "I'm helping the Tang monk reach the Western Paradise to fetch the true scriptures. The Squire was very kind to us."

## ⑨

  The Bodhisattva smiled.

> "Very well. The Squire may return to Earth until it's time for him to come back here."

  The Bodhisattva brought Wukong to an office. Squire Kou was sitting behind a large desk smiling as he studied some documents. His eyes sparked with light when he saw Wukong.

## ⑩

> "It's good to see you, Wukong," said the Squire. "What are you doing here?"

> "I have good news," said the monkey. "Your life isn't over yet. I'm taking you back to Earth."

  The Squire looked confused. He glanced at the Bodhisattva. The Bodhisattva nodded.

> "You may return to Earth. When your life is over, you will return to your job here."

## ⑪

  The Squire came out from behind his desk and bowed.

> "Thank you, Bodhisattva. I am happy here, but I would love to see my wife and friends again."

  Wukong moved closer to the Squire.

> "You'll be home soon, Squire."

  The monkey recited a spell and the Squire vanished.

# 🇨🇳 第一百零二章：释囚

## ①

  第二天一早，寇夫人跑到公堂上，含着泪水告诉县官她昨天撒了谎。

> "唐僧和他的徒弟们是清白的，"寇夫人抽噎着，"请您放了他们。"

  县官点了点头。

> "我本来就打算今天一早释放他们。"

  他告诉寇夫人，昨晚一位游荡的鬼魂曾拜访过他。

## ②

  寇夫人大惊。

> "我丈夫昨晚来托梦给我了！"

> "我们必须立刻释放囚犯。"

  他们急忙赶到唐僧的牢房前。一个狱卒打开牢门。

> "终于能出来了，"悟净说。

> "是啊，"八戒打了个哈欠，"床太不舒服了。"

## ③

  寇夫人向唐僧鞠躬。

> "我诬告您杀了我丈夫。这是我做的一件大恶事。请您原谅我。"

  唐僧把手放在悟空肩上。

> "悟空，别冲动。"

  他看着寇夫人。

> "我们原谅你。员外是个好心人。他的死是一场大悲剧。"

## ④

  悟空摸了摸下巴。

> "我大概能让他起死回生。"

  寇夫人和县官都大吃一惊。

> "你真的能做到吗？"寇夫人问道。

## ⑤

  唐僧微笑了。

> "能做到的。悟空曾把乌鸡国的国王救活过。"

  他转向猴子。

> "那次是太上老君帮了你。这次你再去请他帮忙好吗？"

> "不必了，"悟空说，"太上老君不太喜欢我。我还是去地府跟阎王谈吧。"

## ⑥

  悟空来到阴司的幽冥殿，阎王皱起了眉头。

> "悟空，你来做什么？你不是早就从生死簿上勾了名字，成了不死之身吗？"

  悟空笑了。

> "我猜你还记得那件事。我来查一下寇员外的名字。"

> "寇员外！"阎王打开生死簿，翻了几页。

## ⑦

> "啊，找到了。他两天前死的。他现在在地藏菩萨那里做文书，就在这幽冥界。"

  悟空去了地藏菩萨的玉宇宫。

> "悟空，欢迎，"地藏菩萨说。

> "菩萨好。"猴子行了礼。

## ⑧

> "两天前，我的一个好朋友寇员外在阳间被害。我想请菩萨让他还阳。"

  地藏菩萨挑了挑眉毛。

> "你要他回阳间？"

> "是的，"悟空说，"我正在帮唐僧去西天取经。寇员外对我们非常好。"

## ⑨

  地藏菩萨微笑了。

> "好吧。寇员外可以返回阳间，等到阳寿尽了再回这里来。"

  地藏菩萨带悟空来到一间公房。寇员外正坐在一张大桌前，微笑着审阅文书。他看到悟空，眼中闪出欣喜的光。

## ⑩

> "悟空，见到你真好，"员外说，"你怎么来了？"

> "有个好消息，"猴子说，"你的阳寿还没尽呢，我来带你回阳间。"

  员外一脸困惑，他看了看地藏菩萨。菩萨点了点头。

> "你可以回阳间了。等到阳寿尽了，你再回到这里来上班。"

## ⑪

  员外从桌后走出来，行了一个大礼。

> "感谢菩萨。我在这里很安稳，但我实在想念妻子和亲友们。"

  悟空靠近员外。

> "员外，你马上就能回家了。"

  猴子念了一道咒语，员外便消失了。
