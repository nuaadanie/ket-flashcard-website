
# Journey to the West · Chapter Ninety-Nine: The Monster's Cave

## ①

  "Who's down there now?" the monster yelled from inside the cave.

  Bajie fell to his knees and cried, tears streaming from his eyes.

> "I can't believe you actually believed that monster!" Wukong frowned at his companions.

  He sighed.

> "Wait here," said the monkey.

## ②

  The monkey turned into a moth and flew into the cave. He soon found the Tang monk tied up in a dark room. Wukong turned back into himself.

> "Wukong!" cried the Tang monk.

> "Shhh!" The monkey untied the monk. "We must be quiet!"

## ③

  Wukong recited a spell. A moment later, the sound of snoring drifted into the room.

> "My spell worked on the monsters and demons," said the monkey. "Let's go!"

  Wukong and the monk came out of the cave. Bajie and Wujing were thrilled.

> "Master! We thought you'd been captured!" said Bajie.

## ④

> "I knew he was fine," said Wukong. "Now I'm going to make sure these villains never bother anyone again."

  He pulled out his iron bar and willed it until it was the size of a very tall tree.

  *CRUSH!* With one swing, Wukong destroyed the entire cave. Rocks exploded and fell on top of one another. Wukong looked at the rubble and smiled.

## ⑤

  Weeks went by and the travelers arrived in a small town. Bajie's stomach growled loudly.

> "I guess we should look for food," laughed Wukong.

  They saw an old monk walking down a street.

> "Kind monk," said the Tang monk, "do you know where we can beg for some food? We are quite hungry."

## ⑥

  The old monk nodded.

> "Go to Squire Kou's house. The squire is about to feed ten thousand Buddhist monks. He will surely give you some food."

  He gave the Tang monk directions to the house.

> "Thank you," said the Tang monk.

## ⑦

  The travelers arrived at the Squire's estate and a servant let them in. The Squire's house was very large and fancy. Squire Kou came outside.

> "Greetings," he said with a smile. "Please come inside."

  The travelers sat with the Squire in a large room. A servant brought tea and snacks.

> "Thank you for inviting us in, Squire," said the Tang monk. "We heard that you made a vow to feed ten thousand monks."

## ⑧

> "Yes," said the Squire. "I've made plenty of money during my life, but I wanted to do good deeds as well. I hope to be reborn one day as a bodhisattva or even a Buddha."

> "Wow, Squire," said Bajie. "Today's your lucky day. I can easily eat as much food as ten thousand monks. So if you feed me, you'll definitely become a Buddha."

## ⑨

  Everybody laughed.

> "If it is my lucky day," said the Squire, "last night, I checked to see how many monks I've fed. So far, I've fed nine thousand nine hundred ninety-nine monks. I need to feed only four more in order to fulfill my vow."

  A smile spread across his face.

> "And there are four of you."

## ⑩

  That evening, Squire Kou held a feast. Everybody in the town was invited. The travelers sat with the Squire and his wife, Lady Kou. Bajie's eyes darted at all of the wonderful food on the table.

  Noodles, buns, mushrooms — he didn't know what to choose!

  As they ate, Squire Kou leaned toward the Tang monk.

> "So, where are you and your companions going?"

## ⑪

> "We're traveling to the Western Paradise," said the Tang monk. "We're going to fetch the true scriptures from Buddha."

  The Squire's eyes lit up.

> "You are special guests. I can't let you leave."

# 🇨🇳 第九十九章：魔怪之洞

## ①

  "里面是谁？"怪物在洞穴里大叫道。

  八戒跪在地上大哭起来，眼泪从眼眶中流出来。

> "我真不敢相信你们居然相信了那个怪物！"悟空对同伴们皱起了眉头。

  他叹了口气。

> "在这儿等着，"猴子说。

## ②

  猴子变成一只飞蛾飞进了洞穴。他很快发现唐僧被绑在一个黑暗的房间里。悟空现出了原形。

> "悟空！"唐僧叫道。

> "嘘——"猴子给唐僧松了绑，"我们一定要安静！"

## ③

  悟空念了一道咒语。过了一会儿，鼾声传进了房间。

> "我的法术让妖怪和妖魔都睡着了，"猴子说，"我们走吧！"

  悟空和唐僧走出了洞穴。八戒和悟净激动不已。

> "师父！我们还以为您被抓了！"八戒说。

## ④

> "我早就知道他没事的，"悟空说，"现在我要确保这些坏蛋再也不会骚扰任何人了。"

  他掏出铁棒一挥，铁棒变得像一棵非常高大的树一样粗。

  *轰隆！*悟空一棒扫平了整座洞穴。岩石爆裂塌方，互相压在一起。悟空看着废墟，微微笑了。

## ⑤

  几周过去了，一行旅人来到了一个小镇。八戒的肚子咕噜噜地大声叫了起来。

> "我想我们该去找点吃的了，"悟空笑着說。

  他们看到一位老和尚正沿街走来。

> "善良的师父，"唐僧说，"您知道我们去哪里可以讨到一些食物吗？我们非常饿。"

## ⑥

  老和尚点了点头。

> "去寇员外家吧。员外正要款待一万个僧人。他一定会给你们食物的。"

  他为唐僧指了去员外家的路。

> "谢谢您，"唐僧说。

## ⑦

  一行旅人来到了寇员外的府邸，一个仆人让他们进去了。员外的宅院十分宽敞华丽。寇员外走了出来。

> "欢迎欢迎，"他笑着说，"请进来坐。"

  旅人和员外一起坐在大厅里。一个仆人端上了茶和点心。

> "感谢员外邀请我们，"唐僧说，"听说您发愿要款待一万个僧人。"

## ⑧

> "是的，"员外说，"我这辈子赚了不少钱，但我也想做善事。我希望有一天能转世做菩萨，甚至成佛。"

> "哎呀，员外，"八戒说，"今天是您的幸运日啊。我一顿就能吃下一万个僧人的饭量。您请了我，保准您能成佛。"

## ⑨

  大家都笑了。

> "如果今天真是我的幸运日，"员外说，"昨晚我查了一下已经款待了多少僧人。到现在为止，我已经款待了九千九百九十九个僧人。我只要再请四个就能圆愿了。"

  他脸上露出了笑容。

> "而你们正好是四个。"

## ⑩

  那天晚上，寇员外设宴席款待，镇上的人都来赴宴。旅人和员外及员外的妻子寇夫人一起用餐。八戒的眼睛盯着桌上所有佳肴，馋得不得了。

  面条、包子、蘑菇——他都不知道该选什么好！

  吃着吃着，寇员外凑近唐僧。

> "请问你们要去哪里？"

## ⑪

> "我们要去西天，"唐僧说，"我们要去佛祖那里取真经。"

  员外的双眼明亮了起来。

> "你们是我的贵客，我可不能让你们走。"
