
# Journey to the West · Chapter Eighty-Seven: The Priest Plan

## ①

  Bajie cried out.

> "Help! Spider webs have covered me so I couldn't move at all!"

  Wukong quickly grew tall. He swung his iron bar furiously at the young women.

> "Oh no!" cried one woman. "The monkey is a powerful spirit!"

> "We must leave!" cried the second.

> "We must leave!" cried the third.

  Poof! The women vanished.

## ②

  Wukong ran into the house. He saw a large form wrapped in spider webs in a corner.

> "Master!" cried the monkey. He quickly pulled off the webs.

> "Thank you, Wukong," said the monk breathlessly. "Those women were planning to eat me."

> "Those women are gone now," said Wukong. Then he smirked. "Bajie scared them away. The pig was a real hero."

## ③

> "Wonderful," said the Tang Monk as they went outside. "Where is Bajie now?"

> "He's right there." Wukong pointed and laughed. "Bajie, the brave hero."

  Bajie was on the ground, still struggling to get out of the webs. He pretended to laugh.

> "Wukong, very funny."

## ④

  The road left the forest and headed up a mountain. The travelers arrived at a Taoist abbey.

> "Maybe we can rest here," said Wujing.

  The group entered the abbey. An old priest was inside mixing a potion.

> "Visitors!" said the priest. "Come in and make yourselves at home."

> "Thank you," said the Tang Monk.

## ⑤

  The priest led them to a sitting area.

> "You're Buddhists," the priest observed as everyone sat down. "Where are you going?"

  The Tang Monk opened his mouth to answer, but Wukong spoke first.

> "We're on our way to visit friends," said the monkey.

> "That's nice," said the priest. "Would you like some tea? I have plenty of food too."

## ⑥

> "I hope there's a meal for us," said Bajie. "That would be wonderful."

  The priest stood up and left the room.

> "Wukong, why did you lie to the priest?" asked the Tang Monk.

> "Just being careful," said the monkey. "We shouldn't tell everyone we meet that you're the Tang Monk."

  Wujing nodded.

> "Wukong is right, Master. You almost got eaten just this morning."

## ⑦

  The priest was in the kitchen boiling water for tea. He was looking for rice when he realized he wasn't alone. The three young women stood nearby, and they looked angry.

> "Hello, my dear friends," said the priest. "What's wrong? You look upset."

> "We had the Tang Monk this morning," said one woman, "but we had to flee our house."

> "A monkey and a pig attacked us," said the third.

## ⑧

> "I have visitors right now," the priest whispered. "There's a monk with three companions. One is a monkey and another is a pig."

> "Are you serious?" whispered the first woman.

> "It must be them," whispered the second woman.

> "They must not leave," whispered the third.

## ⑨

  An evil smile flashed across the priest's face.

> "We'll eat the Tang Monk, and we'll all live forever."

  The first woman now looked worried.

> "But how will we defeat the monk's companions? That monkey is powerful."

  The priest went to a cabinet and pulled out a small bottle.

> "I'll add this poison to their tea."

## ⑩

  The priest returned to the travelers.

> "Sorry I took so long," he said, smiling. "The meal will be ready soon. Here's your tea."

> "Thank you," said the Tang Monk.

  Wukong looked at the priest and suddenly felt suspicious. There was something evil behind the man's smile. The monkey glanced at his tea. It didn't look quite right.

## ⑪

> "Master!" cried the monkey. "Don't drink the--"

  But it was too late. The monk, Wujing, and Bajie were already sipping their tea.

# 🇨🇳 第八十七章：僧侣计划

## ①

  八戒大叫。

> "救命！蜘蛛网把我全缠住了，动弹不得！"

  悟空迅速长高，挥起铁棒朝年轻女子打去。

> "不好！"一个女子叫道，"这猴子法力高强！"

> "我们快走！"第二个叫道。

> "快走快走！"第三个叫道。

  噗！三个女子消失了。

## ②

  悟空跑进屋子。他看到角落里有一个被蛛网裹着的大身影。

> "师父！"猴子叫道，迅速扯掉蛛网。

> "谢谢你，悟空，"唐僧气喘吁吁地说，"那些女子打算吃掉我。"

> "她们已经跑了，"悟空说，然后他坏笑了一下，"八戒把她们吓跑的。这头猪还真是个大英雄。"

## ③

> "太好了，"唐僧说着走了出来，"八戒呢？"

> "就在那儿。"悟空指着笑道，"八戒，勇敢的英雄。"

  八戒正躺在地上，还在拼命挣脱蛛网。他假装笑着。

> "悟空，真有意思。"

## ④

  道路离开森林，通向一座山。一行人来到一座道观前。

> "也许我们能在这里休息一下，"悟净说。

  一行人走进道观。一个老道士正在里面调药。

> "贵客来了！"道士说，"快请进，别客气。"

> "谢谢，"唐僧说。

## ⑤

  道士领他们到一处坐席。

> "你们是佛门弟子，"道士看着众人坐下说，"你们要去哪里？"

  唐僧正要回答，悟空抢先开口。

> "我们去拜访朋友，"猴子说。

> "好啊，"道士说，"想喝点茶吗？我还有很多食物。"

## ⑥

> "希望有饭给我们吃，"八戒说，"那太好了。"

  道士站起身离开了房间。

> "悟空，你为什么骗道士？"唐僧问。

> "小心为上，"猴子说，"我们不能见人就告诉人家你是唐僧。"

  悟净点了点头。

> "悟空说得对，师父。今天早上您差点就被吃了。"

## ⑦

  道士在厨房煮水泡茶。找米时，他发现屋里多了一个人。那三个年轻女子站在旁边，满脸怒气。

> "你好，亲爱的朋友们，"道士说，"怎么了？你们看起来很生气。"

> "我们今天遇到了唐僧，"一个女子说，"但我们不得不逃出家门。"

> "一个猴子和一个猪攻击了我们。"第三个女子说。

## ⑧

> "我现在有客人，"道士低声说，"一个和尚带着三个徒弟，其中一个是猴子，一个是猪。"

> "你是认真的？"第一个女子低声说。

> "一定是他们，"第二个女子低声说。

> "不能让他们跑了，"第三个女子低声说。

## ⑨

  道士脸上闪过一丝邪恶的笑容。

> "我们吃掉唐僧，就能长生不老。"

  第一个女子面露担忧。

> "但是怎么对付他的同伴呢？那个猴子很厉害。"

  道士走到柜子前，拿出一个小瓶子。

> "我往茶里加点毒药。"

## ⑩

  道士回到旅行者们身边。

> "抱歉让你们久等了，"他笑着说，"饭马上就好。先喝点茶吧。"

> "谢谢，"唐僧说。

  悟空看了看道士，突然起了疑心。那个人笑容背后藏着邪恶。猴子看了看茶水，觉得不太对劲。

## ⑪

> "师父！"悟空大叫，"别喝那——"

  但已经太迟了。唐僧、悟净和八戒已经在喝茶了。
