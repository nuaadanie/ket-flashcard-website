
# Journey to the West · Chapter Eighty-Six: The Three Young Women

## ①

  Wukong and his companions were walking through a forest.

> "I'm glad the road goes through the shady forest," said the Tang Monk. "The sun is very hot this morning."

  Wujing nodded.

> "It's nice to walk in the shade."

  Wukong looked around suspiciously. He knew that forests were often filled with dangers.

## ②

  Suddenly, Bajie fell flat on his head. He climbed back to his feet and rubbed his nose.

> "I'm okay," he said. "I tripped on a rock."

  The travelers soon came to a wall running along the road. Behind the wall, they could see the roof of a house. Up ahead was a gate.

> "I wonder who lives here?" said Wujing.

## ③

> "Wukong," he said, "I hear laughter."

  The monkey peeked over the wall. Three young women were laughing and kicking a ball around.

> "I see three young women," said Wukong.

> "Let's ask them for some food," said Wujing.

> "That's a good idea," said Bajie. "I'll go talk to them."

## ④

  The pig headed toward the gate.

> "Bajie, wait here," said the Tang Monk. He looked at his companions. "I will speak to the women. You three might frighten them."

> "Right, man," Bajie echoed.

> "Yes," said the Tang Monk. "After all, Bajie, you're a large talking pig. Wujing, you're big and purple. And Wukong, you're scary sometimes."

  The monkey shrugged. Wujing nodded.

## ⑤

> "Fine," sighed Bajie.

  The Tang Monk opened the gate and went through. He approached the women.

> "Bajie," said one woman, "we have a visitor."

> "He's a monk," said another woman.

> "What can we do for you?" asked the third.

## ⑥

> "Hello," said the Tang Monk. "I'm wondering if you could spare some food for my companions and me."

  The three women smiled.

> "Of course we can spare some food," said the first woman.

> "We had plenty," said the second woman.

> "Please come inside and rest," said the third.

## ⑦

  The three young women let the Tang Monk into their house. As soon as the monk entered, he became scared. The interior of the house was dark and dreary. Dirt covered everything and cobwebs hung from the walls.

> "Tell us, monk," said one woman. "Why are you walking through our forest today?"

  The Tang Monk looked around nervously.

## ⑧

> "I, um... I'm going to the Western Paradise."

> "You're the Tang Monk!" shrieked all three women.

  The Tang Monk took a step back.

> "You've... heard of me?"

> "Of course!" said one woman.

## ⑨

  All three women reached behind their heads. They began to pull out long strands of spider silk. Slowly they moved toward the Tang Monk.

  What's taking Master so long? asked Wukong.

  The monk's companions were waiting outside in the road.

> "He's probably inside that house, enjoying a nice meal," said Bajie. "Meanwhile, we're out here starving!"

## ⑩

> "The Tang Monk wouldn't do that, Bajie," said Wujing.

  Wukong looked worried.

> "We'd better check on Master."

> "If there's food, I want to be there," said Bajie.

> "I'll go with you!"

  The two of them went to the gate. When they saw the house, they both gasped.

## ⑪

> "The house is covered in spider webs!" cried the pig.

  Wukong kicked in the gate and they rushed toward the house. The monkey banged on the door.

> "Open up!"

  The door flew open. The three women leapt out, shooting sticky webs at Wukong and Bajie.

## ⑫

  The pig waved his rake. Webs wrapped around it, and soon the weapon was stuck to his head. Bajie turned to run, but more webs flew toward him. They wrapped around his legs and he fell to the ground.

> "Help!" cried Bajie. "Spider webs have covered me so we couldn't move at all."

  Wukong quickly grew tall. He swung his iron bar furiously at the young women.

## ⑬

> "Oh no!" cried one woman. "The monkey is a powerful spirit!"

> "We must leave," cried the third.

  Poof! The women vanished.

  Wukong ran into the house. He saw a large form wrapped in spider webs in a corner.

> "Master!" cried the monkey. He quickly pulled off the webs.

## ⑭

> "Thank you, Wukong," said the monk breathlessly. "Those women were planning to eat me."

> "Those women are gone now," said Wukong. Then he smirked. "Bajie scared them away. The pig was a real hero."

> "Wonderful," said the Tang Monk as they went outside. "Where is Bajie now?"

> "He's right there," Wukong pointed and laughed. "Bajie, the brave hero."

## ⑮

  Bajie was on the ground, still struggling to get out of the webs. He pretended to laugh.

> "Wukong, very funny."

  The road left the forest and headed up a mountain. The travelers arrived at a Taoist abbey.

> "Maybe we can rest here," said Wujing.

## ⑯

  The group entered the abbey. An old priest was inside mixing a potion.

> "Visitors!" said the priest. "Come in and make yourselves at home."

> "Thank you," said the Tang Monk.

  The priest led them to a sitting area.

> "You're Buddhists," the priest observed as everyone sat down. "Where are you going?"

## ⑰

  The Tang Monk opened his mouth to answer, but Wukong spoke first.

> "We're on our way to visit friends," said the monkey.

> "That's nice," said the priest. "Would you like some tea? I have plenty of food too."

> "I hope there's a meal for us," said Bajie. "That would be wonderful."

  The priest stood up and left the room.

## ⑱

> "Wukong, why did you lie to the priest?" asked the Tang Monk.

> "Just being careful," said the monkey. "We shouldn't tell everyone we meet that you're the Tang Monk."

  Wujing nodded.

> "Wukong is right, Master. You almost got eaten just this morning."

# 🇨🇳 第八十六章：三个年轻女子

## ①

  悟空和同伴们正在森林中行走。

> "走在这阴凉的路上真舒服，"唐僧说，"今天的太阳太晒了。"

  悟净点了点头。

> "在树荫下走路确实好。"

  悟空警惕地环顾四周。他知道森林里常常充满危险。

## ②

  突然，八戒一头栽倒在地。他爬起来揉了揉鼻子。

> "我没事，"他说，"被石头绊了一下。"

  一行人来到路边的一堵墙前。墙后面能看到房屋的屋顶。前面有一扇大门。

> "不知道谁住在这里？"悟净说。

## ③

> "悟空，"他说，"我听到笑声。"

  猴子从墙上探出头去。三个年轻女子正在笑着踢一个球。

> "我看到三个年轻女子，"悟空说。

> "向她们讨点吃的吧，"悟净说。

> "好主意，"八戒说，"我去跟她们说。"

## ④

  八戒朝大门走去。

> "八戒，等等，"唐僧说，他看了看同伴们，"我去跟她们说话，你们三个会吓到她们。"

> "有道理，"八戒附和道。

> "是啊，"唐僧说，"八戒，你是一头大猪，太吓人。悟净，你长得又高又紫。悟空，你有时候也挺吓人的。"

  猴子耸了耸肩。悟净点了点头。

## ⑤

> "行吧，"八戒叹了口气。

  唐僧推开大门走了进去。他走近那三个女子。

> "哎，"一个女子说，"来了个客人。"

> "是个和尚，"另一个女子说。

> "有什么能帮您的？"第三个女子问。

## ⑥

> "你好，"唐僧说，"请问能给我和同伴们一些吃的吗？"

  三个女子微笑着。

> "当然可以，"第一个女子说。

> "我们家食物充足，"第二个女子说。

> "请进屋休息吧，"第三个女子说。

## ⑦

  三个年轻女子把唐僧领进了屋。唐僧一进去就害怕了。屋里又暗又潮，到处是土，墙上挂满了蜘蛛网。

> "请问和尚，"一个女子问，"你为什么走过我们的森林？"

  唐僧紧张地四处张望。

## ⑧

> "我……我要去西天。"

> "你就是唐僧！"三个女子一齐惊叫。

  唐僧向后退了一步。

> "你们……听说过我？"

> "当然！"一个女子说。

## ⑨

  三个女子同时把手伸到脑后。她们开始拉出长长的蜘蛛丝，慢慢朝唐僧逼近。

  师父怎么这么久？悟空心想。

  同伴们正在路上等着。

> "他大概在里面吃好吃的呢，"八戒说，"我们可在这里饿肚子！"

## ⑩

> "师父不会这样的，八戒，"悟净说。

  悟空面露担忧。

> "我们去看看师父吧。"

> "有吃的我也要去，"八戒说。

> "我也跟你去！"

  两人走到大门前。看到房子的时候，他们都倒吸一口凉气。

## ⑪

> "房子全被蜘蛛网盖住了！"猪大叫。

  悟空一脚踢开大门，两人冲进屋里。猴子猛敲房门。

> "开门！"

  大门飞开，三个女子跳出来朝悟空和八戒喷射黏糊糊的蛛网。

## ⑫

  八戒挥舞着钉耙。蛛网缠上了钉耙，很快兵器就黏在了他头上。八戒转身想跑，但更多的蛛网朝他飞来，缠住了他的腿，他摔在地上。

> "救命！"八戒大叫，"我被蛛网缠住了，动弹不得！"

  悟空迅速长高，挥起铁棒朝三个女子打去。

## ⑬

> "不好！"一个女子叫道，"这猴子法力高强！"

> "我们快走！"第三个叫道。

  噗！三个女子消失了。

  悟空跑进屋子。他看到角落里有一个被蛛网裹着的大身影。

> "师父！"猴子叫道，他迅速扯掉蛛网。

## ⑭

> "谢谢你，悟空，"唐僧气喘吁吁地说，"那些女子打算吃掉我。"

> "她们已经跑了，"悟空说，然后他坏笑了一下，"八戒把她们吓跑的。八戒可是个大英雄。"

> "太好了，"唐僧说着走了出来，"八戒呢？"

> "就在那儿，"悟空指着笑，"八戒，勇敢的英雄。"

## ⑮

  八戒正躺在地上，还在拼命挣脱蛛网。他也跟着笑起来。

> "悟空，你真幽默。"

  道路离开森林，通向一座山。一行人来到一座道观前。

> "也许我们能在这里休息一下，"悟净说。

## ⑯

  一行人走进道观。一个老道士正在里面调药。

> "贵客来了！"道士说，"快请进，别客气。"

> "谢谢，"唐僧说。

  道士领他们到一处坐席。

> "你们是佛门弟子，"道士看着众人坐下，"你们要去哪里？"

## ⑰

  唐僧正要回答，悟空抢先开口。

> "我们去拜访朋友，"猴子说。

> "好啊，"道士说，"想喝点茶吗？我还有很多食物。"

> "希望有饭给我们吃，"八戒说，"那太好了。"

  道士站起身，离开了房间。

## ⑱

> "悟空，你为什么骗道士？"唐僧问。

> "小心为上，"猴子说，"我们不能见人就告诉人家你是唐僧。"

  悟净点了点头。

> "悟空说得对，师父。今天早上您差点就被吃了。"
