# 西游记 · 第69-108章

# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixty-Nine: The Stabbing Pain

## ①

  That woman was a demon! cried Wukong, and she kidnapped the Tang monk! He leaped into the air, and Bajie and Wujing followed.

## ②

  In the distance, the monkey saw dark clouds speeding toward a mountain.

> "That must be her," said Wukong.

> "Quickly!" said Wujing. "We must catch her!"

## ③

  They raced after the cloud, which disappeared when it reached the mountain. They soon arrived and found a cave with its door closed. Wukong pounded on the cave door.

> "Open up!" he yelled.

## ④

  The door flew open, and the woman came out.

> "Who are you?" demanded the woman. "Why are you pounding on my door?"

> "Release our master!" shouted Wukong, ignoring her questions.

  The woman laughed.

> "Don't tell me what to do."

  The monkey attacked. The woman spun around fast, blocking his iron bar and striking back with fury.

> "You shouldn't have come here," she shouted as she fought. "Even the Jade Emperor is afraid of me."

## ⑤

  Bajie tried to hit her with his rake, but missed. The woman was now spinning faster and faster.

> "She's faster than a tornado!" cried the pig.

  Wukong leaped at the woman. Suddenly he felt a stabbing pain.

> "Ah ha ha!" cried the monkey, grabbing his head. "It hurts! It hurts!"

  He ran down the mountain. Bajie and Wujing stared after Wukong in shock. The woman went back into her cave.

## ⑥

> "What happened to Wukong?" asked the pig. "Nothing hurts him except for the tight headband spell."

> "I don't know," said Wujing, scratching his head.

## ⑦

  They went down the mountain and found the monkey crouching by a stream. He was still holding his head.

> "Are you alright?" asked Wujing.

> "That woman stabbed me in the head," moaned Wukong. "The pain is horrible."

  Bajie snorted.

> "You're always bragging about how tough you are. Now you're crying about getting stabbed in the head. Pathetic!"

  Shouted the monkey angrily.

## ⑧

  Wujing moved closer.

> "Let me see where she stabbed you."

  Wukong lowered his hands. There was no injury.

> "Ha ha ha!" Bajie laughed. "Gosh, he's fine! He just wants us to feel bad for him."

## ⑨

  The monkey glared at the pig.

> "I'm telling you, it hurts!"

  He took a deep breath.

> "The pain is going away now, though. Let's go back up there and fight her again."

## ⑩

  The three companions returned to the cave.

> "Wukong, you stand back," said Bajie, laughing. "I don't want you getting hurt again."

  Wukong clenched his teeth. The pig hit the door with his rake. Smash! The door exploded. The woman ran outside.

> "You smashed my door!" she shouted.

> "Release our master!" shouted the pig.

  The woman spun around faster and faster.

## ⑪

  Bajie and Wujing tried to strike her with their weapons, but the woman was too fast. Wukong raised his iron bar, waiting for a good moment to strike. Suddenly Bajie cried out.

## ⑫

  He covered his nose and ran back down the mountain. The woman spun toward Wukong and Wujing, and they turned and ran back down as well. Bajie was huddled on the ground, tears streaming from his eyes.

> "It hurt so badly!"

  Wukong laughed.

> "No laughing, Bajie!" cried Bajie. "She stabbed my nose!"

> "You deserved it," said the monkey. "You didn't believe me when I got hurt."

## ⑬

> "Stop arguing," said Wujing. "We still have to save the Tang monk."

> "You're right," said Wukong. "Let's go back up there."

  A voice called out.

> "Wait."

  It was Guanyin. She was walking towards the group.

> "Beware of that scorpion spirit."

# 🇨🇳 第六十九章：刺骨之痛

## ①

  "那女人是个妖怪！"悟空大叫道，"她把唐僧掳走了！"

  他纵身一跃跳到空中，八戒和悟净紧随其后。

## ②

  远远地，悟空看见一团乌云正向一座山疾飞而去。

> "一定是她，"悟空说。

> "快！"悟净喊道，"我们得追上她！"

## ③

  三人追着那团云，云到了山前便消失了。他们很快赶到山前，发现了一个洞穴，洞门紧闭。悟空用力捶打洞门。

> "开门！"他大喊道。

## ④

  门猛地打开，那女人走了出来。

> "你们是谁？"那女人厉声问道，"为什么砸我的门？"

> "放了我们师父！"悟空不理会她的问题，大声喝道。

  那女人哈哈大笑。

> "轮不到你来命令我。"

  猴子挥棒攻击。那女人迅速转身，挡开铁棒反打回来，怒不可遏。

> "你们不该来这里！"她一边打一边喊。"就连玉帝都怕我！"

## ⑤

  八戒举起钉耙打她，却扑了个空。那女人旋转得越来越快。

> "她比龙卷风还快！"八戒惊叫道。

  悟空纵身扑向那女人，突然感到一阵刺骨的疼痛。

> "哈哈哈！"悟空抱着头叫道，"好疼！好疼！"

  他转身跑下了山。八戒和悟净望着悟空的背影，愣住了。那女人退回了洞中。

## ⑥

> "悟空怎么了？"八戒问道，"除了紧箍咒，没有什么能伤害他啊。"

> "我也不知道。"悟净挠了挠头说。

## ⑦

  他们下了山，发现悟空蹲在一条小溪边，双手还抱着头。

> "你没事吧？"悟净问。

> "那女人用兵器刺了我的头，"悟空痛苦地说，"太疼了，疼得厉害！"

  八戒哼了一声。

> "你老是吹嘘自己有多厉害。现在被刺了头倒哭起来了，真没用！"

  悟空怒吼了一声。

## ⑧

  悟净走近了些。

> "让我看看她刺了你哪里。"

  悟空放下双手，头上并没有任何伤口。

> "哈哈哈！"八戒大笑起来，"他好好的！只是想让我们同情他。"

## ⑨

  悟空瞪了八戒一眼。

> "我告诉你们，真的很疼！"

  他深吸了一口气。

> "不过现在疼得不那么厉害了。我们回去再跟她打一场。"

## ⑩

  三人回到了洞穴前。

> "悟空，你站远点，"八戒得意地说，"我可不想你又受伤。"

  悟空咬紧牙关。八戒用钉耙猛砸洞门，"轰"的一声，门被炸开了。那女人跑了出来。

> "你们砸了我的门！"她大喊道。

> "放了我们师父！"八戒喝道。

  那女人又飞快地旋转起来。

## ⑪

  八戒和悟净举起兵器进攻，但那女人实在太快了。悟空高举金箍棒，等待出击的好时机。突然，八戒惨叫了一声。

## ⑫

  他捂着鼻子往山下跑。那女人转身冲向悟空和悟净，两人也转身往山下逃去。八戒蜷缩在地上，眼泪从脸上流下来。

> "疼死了！"

  悟空大笑起来。

> "不许笑！"八戒哭喊道，"她刺了我的鼻子！"

> "活该，"悟空说，"谁叫你刚才不信我。"

## ⑬

> "别吵了，"悟净说，"我们还得去救唐僧呢。"

> "你说得对，"悟空说，"我们再去一次。"

  一个声音从远处传来。

> "等等。"

  是观音菩萨，她正朝他们走来。

> "当心那个蝎子精，她很危险。"

# Journey to the West · Chapter Seventy: The Star Lord

## ①

  Guanyin shook her head.

> "You cannot go back to that cave," said Guanyin.

> "But we must go back," said Wukong. "That woman kidnapped the Tang monk."

> "That woman is a scorpion spirit. She is very dangerous."

## ②

> "She sure is!" cried Bajie. "What did she stab us with, anyway?"

> "It was her stinger that stabbed you," said Guanyin.

  Wukong thought about the pain he'd felt.

> "I won't go near her."

## ③

> "Even Buddha is afraid of her," said Guanyin. "He was teaching his students one day and saw that scorpion creeping toward him. He tried to move it away, but it stung him. The pain was horrible. He told his guards to capture the scorpion, but it escaped to Earth."

## ④

  Wukong paced anxiously.

> "What are we going to do, then?"

> "We must save the Tang monk. Go to Heaven and ask the Star Lord for help," said Guanyin.

  Guanyin disappeared in a flash of light.

## ⑤

  A little while later, Wukong stood at the southern gate of Heaven.

> "Greetings, Wukong," said the guard.

> "I must speak with the Star Lord," said the monkey.

  The guard told Wukong the way to the Star Lord's palace. A few minutes later, the monkey knocked on the palace door.

## ⑥

> "Wukong," said the Star Lord. "What can I do for you?"

> "I need your help," said Wukong. "I'm traveling to the Western Paradise with the Tang monk, but he was captured by a demon. Guanyin said you can help me."

  The Star Lord looked confused.

> "I don't know why you need my help. I've heard you're quite strong. What kind of demon is it?"

## ⑦

> "It's a scorpion spirit," said the monkey.

> "Ah," said the Star Lord. "I understand now. Lead the way. We must hurry."

## ⑧

  Wukong led the Star Lord out of Heaven and down to Earth. They landed on the mountain where Bajie was still rubbing his nose.

> "The Star Lord is here to help us," said Wukong.

  The Star Lord looked at Bajie's nose.

> "I can heal that for you."

  Bajie walked over to the Star Lord, who then blew on the pig's nose. The injury healed instantly.

## ⑨

> "Thank you," said the pig, smiling.

> "I was stabbed too," said Wukong. "But the stinger didn't leave a mark on me." He grinned at Bajie. "I'm much more powerful than the pig."

  Bajie frowned.

## ⑩

> "I have special magic that I'll use to defeat the Scorpion," said the Star Lord. "You just need to get her to come outside."

  Everyone went up to the cave. Bajie and Wujing hid behind some rocks. Wukong stood in front of the cave entrance and shouted.

> "Come out, demon! Release my master!"

  Then he ran as fast as he could away from the cave.

## ⑪

  The woman rushed out of the cave, spinning wildly. Suddenly the Star Lord turned into an enormous rooster. The rooster crowed, and the sound echoed through the mountains. The woman screamed and held her hands over her ears. The rooster crowed again, and the woman disappeared in a puff of smoke. In her place was a dead scorpion.

## ⑫

  Wukong ran into the cave. He found the Tang monk sitting at a table. The monk's eyes lit up.

> "Wukong! Thank you for saving me!"

  The monkey smiled.

> "As long as I'm protecting you, no demon will ever harm you."

## ⑬

  The Tang monk shook his head.

> "That woman didn't want to harm me. She wanted to marry me."

  The monk sighed.

> "But I told her that I'm a Buddhist monk and I will never marry. My only goal is to reach the Western Paradise."

# 🇨🇳 第七十章：昴日星官

## ①

  观音摇了摇头。

> "你们不能再回那个洞去了，"观音说。

> "可是我们必须回去，"悟空说，"那女人掳走了唐僧。"

> "那女人是蝎子精，非常危险。"

## ②

> "她确实危险！"八戒叫道，"她到底用什么刺我们的？"

> "是她的尾刺刺了你们，"观音说。

  悟空想起那阵疼痛，心有余悸。

> "我再也不靠近她了。"

## ③

> "连佛祖都怕她，"观音说，"有一天佛祖正在讲经，看见这只蝎子悄悄向他爬来。他想把她赶走，却被她的尾刺蛰了一下。那疼痛难以忍受。他叫护法金刚把她捉住，但她却逃到了人间。"

## ④

  悟空焦急地来回踱步。

> "那我们怎么办？我们得救唐僧啊。"

> "去天上请昴日星官帮忙。"观音说。

  观音说完便在一道金光中消失了。

## ⑤

  过了一会儿，悟空来到了南天门外。

> "大圣，你好。"守卫说。

> "我要见昴日星官。"悟空说。

  守卫给悟空指了去昴日星官宫殿的路。几分钟后，悟空敲响了宫殿的门。

## ⑥

> "悟空，"昴日星官说，"你有什么事？"

> "我需要你的帮助，"悟空说，"我和唐僧正在去西天取经的路上，但他被一个妖怪抓走了。观音菩萨说你能帮我。"

  昴日星官一脸困惑。

> "我不明白你为什么需要我的帮助。听说你很厉害，是什么妖怪？"

## ⑦

> "是一只蝎子精。"悟空说。

> "噢，我明白了，"昴日星官说，"带路吧，我们得赶快。"

## ⑧

  悟空领着昴日星官离开天庭来到人间。他们降落在山上，八戒正在那里揉鼻子。

> "昴日星官来帮我们了。"悟空说。

  昴日星官看了看八戒的鼻子。

> "我来帮你治好。"

  八戒走到昴日星官面前，星官朝他的鼻子吹了一口气，伤口瞬间就愈合了。

## ⑨

> "谢谢！"八戒笑着说。

> "我也被刺了，"悟空说，"不过尾刺没在我身上留下痕迹。"他朝八戒咧嘴一笑，"我可比这猪强多了。"

  八戒皱了皱眉。

## ⑩

> "我有一套特别的法术可以降服蝎子精，"昴日星官说，"你们只需要把她引出洞来。"

  大家一起上了山。八戒和悟净躲在石头后面，悟空站在洞口大喊。

> "蝎子精！快出来见我师父！"

  然后他飞快逃离洞口。

## ⑪

  那女人冲出洞来，疯狂地旋转着。突然之间，昴日星官变成了一只巨大的公鸡。公鸡高声啼鸣，声音在山间回荡。那女人惨叫一声，双手捂住耳朵。公鸡又啼叫了一声，那女人在"砰"的一声中化作了一团青烟。原地留下一只死了的蝎子。

## ⑫

  悟空跑进洞里，发现唐僧正坐在桌旁。唐僧眼睛一亮。

> "悟空，谢谢你救我！"

  悟空笑道。

> "只要有我保护你，没有任何妖怪能伤害你。"

## ⑬

  唐僧摇了摇头。

> "那女人并非想害我，她想跟我成亲。"

  唐僧叹了口气。

> "但我告诉她我是出家人，绝不会娶亲。我唯一的目标就是到达西天取经。"

# Journey to the West · Chapter Seventy-One: The Unexpected Danger

## ①

> "I'm hungry," Bajie moaned. His stomach rumbled loudly. "Can we stop walking?"

> "We'll stop for lunch soon," said the Tang monk. "The weather is nice and the road is smooth. I want to keep moving. I'm eager to reach the Western Paradise."

  Wukong laughed.

> "Master, I don't know why you're still going there. You should have married that scorpion spirit instead."

## ②

  The Tang monk frowned.

> "That's not funny, Wukong."

> "I agree with Wukong," said Bajie. "I wouldn't mind marrying her."

  Wukong raised an eyebrow.

> "I doubt she would have married you."

> "Why not?" Bajie puffed out his chest. "I'm very handsome."

  Everybody laughed, including Bajie.

## ③

  They had continued for another hour when the pig complained again.

> "My legs are tired," he said.

> "We'll stop at the next village," said the Tang monk.

  Bajie sighed.

> "I hope we reach the next village soon."

> "I know how we can get there soon," said Wukong with a laugh. The monkey smacked the horse with his stick, and the horse charged forward.

## ④

> "Wukong!" cried the Tang monk, hanging on tightly.

  When the horse finally stopped running, the monk was far ahead of his companions. A group of nasty-looking men stood in front of him.

> "Give us all your money!" said a man with an axe.

  The Tang monk shook with fear. Wukong was already running over.

## ⑤

> "Don't you dare harm my master!" shouted the monkey.

> "Oh, you!" said a man. "Who are you?"

> "My name is Sun Wukong. Give us all your money."

> "I don't have much money," said the monkey. "You'd better give yours to me."

  The man's face turned red.

## ⑥

> "I'm not going to give you money!"

> "Let's make a bet then," said Wukong. He pulled out his iron bar, which quickly grew to the height of a person. The men looked nervous. The monkey stuck the bar into the ground. "If you can pick this up, we'll give you all of our money."

## ⑦

> "Careful," said the man with an axe. "I'm sure I can pick that bar up."

> "Go ahead and try," said Wukong.

  The man put down his axe. He grasped the bar with his hands and tried to lift it.

> "It's heavier than it looks," said the man. He tried to lift the bar again. Sweat poured down his red face. But the bar didn't budge.

## ⑧

> "Now it's my turn," said Wukong. He lifted the bar over his head and twirled it around. His eyes flashed with anger. He got ready to strike the man.

> "Wukong, don't!" shouted the Tang monk.

  The man took a step back. Suddenly the bar dropped from Wukong's hands.

## ⑨

  Crying out, the monkey held his head. He fell to his knees as the Tang monk quietly recited the tight headband spell. The robbers glanced at one another and then ran away.

  When the monk stopped reciting the spell, Wukong was furious.

> "Why did you do that?" he cried. "They were going to rob you!"

## ⑩

> "You must not hurt people," commanded the monk.

> "I destroy demons all the time," said the monkey.

> "It's fine to hurt demons. But you must not hurt people," the monk said. "You're still evil, Wukong. I can't bring you to the Western Paradise. You must leave."

> "But you need me, Master!" Wukong insisted.

## ⑪

> "I refuse. Please go," said the Tang monk. "Or I'll recite the tight headband spell until your head explodes."

  Wukong could see that the monk wasn't going to change his mind. With tears in his eyes, the monkey leaped into the air and was gone.

# 🇨🇳 第七十一章：意外之险

## ①

> "我饿了，"八戒嚷嚷着，肚子咕咕直叫，"我们能歇会儿吗？"

> "很快就停下来吃午饭，"唐僧说，"天气这么好，道路也平坦，我想继续赶路。我急着要到西天去。"

  悟空听了说：

> "师父，我真不明白你为什么还非要去西天。你当初还不如娶了那个蝎子精呢。"

## ②

  唐僧皱起眉头。

> "悟空，这一点也不好笑。"

> "我同意悟空的话，"八戒说，"我倒是很乐意娶她呢。"

  悟空挑了挑眉毛。

> "恐怕人家还未必看得上你呢。"

> "为什么？"八戒挺起胸膛，"我可英俊得很。"

  大家都笑了，八戒也跟着笑了起来。

## ③

  他们又走了一个时辰，八戒又抱怨起来。

> "我的腿好酸。"

> "我们在下一个村子歇脚。"唐僧说。

  八戒叹了口气。

> "但愿快点到下一个村子。"

> "我知道怎么才能快点到，"悟空笑着说，他拍了白龙马一巴掌，马猛然向前飞奔。

## ④

> "悟空！"唐僧惊叫着死死抓住缰绳。

  等马终于停下来时，唐僧已经远远走到了同伴们的前面。一群凶神恶煞的汉子挡在他面前。

> "把身上的钱都交出来！"一个拿斧头的人说。

  唐僧吓得浑身发抖。悟空已经飞奔过来。

## ⑤

> "你们竟敢伤害我师父！"悟空大喊道。

> "哼，你又是谁？"一个人说。

> "我叫孙悟空。把你们的钱都交出来。"

> "我没多少钱，"悟空说，"你们最好把你们的钱给我。"

  那人涨红了脸。

## ⑥

> "我才不会给你钱！"

> "那我们来打个赌，"悟空说。他掏出金箍棒，迎风一晃，变得有一人高。那些汉子面露惧色。悟空将金箍棒立在地上："如果你们谁能举起这根棒子，我们就把所有钱给你们。"

## ⑦

> "小心，"拿斧头的人说，"我肯定能举起这根棒。"

> "那就来试试吧。"悟空说。

  那人放下斧头，双手抓住金箍棒使劲往上抬。

> "比看起来重多了，"那人说，他又试着抬了一次，汗水从涨红的脸上淌下来，可金箍棒纹丝不动。

## ⑧

> "现在轮到我了，"悟空说着把金箍棒举过头顶，舞了起来，眼中闪出怒火，准备打那人。

> "悟空，住手！"唐僧大喊道。

  那人连连后退。忽然悟空手一松，金箍棒从手中掉落。

## ⑨

  他双手抱头，扑通跪倒在地——原来唐僧正在默念紧箍咒。那些强盗互相看了一眼，转身逃跑了。

  唐僧停下咒语，悟空愤怒地质问。

> "你为什么要这样做？他们可是要抢你啊！"

## ⑩

> "你绝不能伤害人。"唐僧命令道。

> "我打的都是妖怪。"悟空说。

> "打妖怪可以，但你绝不能伤害人，"唐僧说，"悟空，你还是太凶恶了。我不能带你去西天了，你必须离开。"

> "可是师父，你需要我呀！"悟空坚持道。

> "到了，"唐僧说，"那是一个神奇的地方。我们现在回东土，我要把真经带回去。"

> "那太好了，"乌龟说，"你帮我问佛祖我没有？我什么时候才能再投胎做人？"

## ⑩

  唐僧咬了咬嘴唇。他已经忘了帮乌龟问了。

> "您怎么不回答？"乌龟说。

> "对不起，"唐僧说，"我……"

## ⑪

  乌龟突然往水下一沉，把背上的所有人都翻了下水。悟空被冷水一激，立刻睁开了眼睛。他纵身飞上了空中。八戒、悟净和白龙马都快速飞过了河。但是唐僧在湍急的河水中拼命挣扎。

> "救命！"唐僧叫道。

## ⑫

  悟空施法将唐僧和经包从河中捞起。众人上了岸，猴子把包袱倒了个底朝天。水哗啦啦流了出来，紧跟着涌出的是经卷。全都被泡透了。

> "真经！"唐僧叫了起来，"全都泡湿了！"

# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred and Seven: Nothing Is Perfect

## ①

  The travelers laid the silk scrolls on some rocks.

> "The sun will dry them out," said Wukong.

  The Tang monk moaned.

> "What if they're ruined?"

  He sat down and put his head in his hand.

> "I wish I'd remembered to ask Buddha the turtle's question."

## ②

  Bajie snorted.

> "Buddha has already forgiven me, and I forgot to turn the turtle's question. That turtle had no right to dump us in the water."

> "Dumping us in the water was rude," Wujing agreed.

  Bajie sighed.

> "The journey back east has been just as difficult as the journey west."

## ③

> "Hello!" came quite a voice.

  The travelers turned and saw a man hurrying toward them.

> "Chen!" said the Tang monk.

  He grasped the man's hands.

> "It's good to see you again."

> "It's good to see all of you," Chen smiled. "Did you reach the Western Paradise?"

## ④

> "Yes," said the Tang monk. "It was magnificent. The land is filled with beauty."

  Chen noticed the scrolls lying on the rocks and his eyes went wide.

> "Are those the true scriptures?" he whispered.

> "Yes," said the monk. "But as you can see, they got wet. A giant turtle dumped us in the river," added Bajie with a sigh.

## ⑤

> "Please come with me," said Chen. "You can dry the true scriptures by my fire. Everyone in the village would be excited to see the travelers who met Buddha."

  Wujing started gathering the wet scriptures.

> "I'll help you," said Bajie.

  The pig grabbed a scroll and pulled — but it was stuck to a rock.

## ⑥

  *Riiip!* The corner of the scroll tore off. The Tang monk gasped.

> "Oh no!"

  Bajie stared at the torn scroll. Wukong smiled.

> "Don't worry, Master."

  The monkey gently picked the rest of the scroll off the rock.

## ⑦

> "This is all I find... But it's ripped!" cried Bajie. He hung his head.

> "Tell me something, Master," said Wukong. "Are you perfect?"

  The monk looked at Wukong, confused.

> "Of course not."

> "Am I perfect?" asked the monkey.

  The Tang monk shook his head.

> "How about Bajie?"

## ⑧

  Wukong smirked.

> "We all know he's not perfect."

  The pig snorted indignantly.

> "Nothing on earth or in heaven is perfect," said Wukong. "So why should the true scriptures be any different? They'll teach the people of the East how to be good and avoid suffering. They will also teach the people that nothing needs to be perfect."

## ⑨

  The travelers finished gathering the scrolls and followed Chen back to the village. People came out of their homes to see the travelers.

> "Welcome back!" called a woman.

> "It's wonderful to see you all again," shouted an old man.

  Soon a crowd was following the Tang monk and his companions to Chen's house. That evening the villagers held a big feast.

## ⑩

  Later that night, Wukong and the Tang monk sat talking.

> "We must leave soon," said the monk. "The villagers will want to see the true scriptures. They'll become jealous that we have them."

> "I agree," said Wukong. He remembered the priest who tried to kill the Tang monk and steal the robe.

## ⑪

> "Let's sneak away now while the villagers are asleep," said Wukong.

  Wukong woke up Bajie and Wujing. The group sneaked out of Chen's house and headed east. It was dark, but the light from the full moon lit up the road.

## ⑫

  Suddenly the guard from the Thunderclap Monastery appeared.

> "And there he goes," said the monk with relief. "I will receive my awards at last."

  The guard recited a summon. Once again the travelers flew toward the east.

# 🇨🇳 第一百零七章：无一是完美

## ①

  旅人们把丝绸经卷铺在几块石头上。

> "太阳会把它们晒干的，"悟空说。

  唐僧唉声叹气。

> "万一毁了怎么办？"

  他坐下来，双手抱头。

> "我当时怎么就忘了帮乌龟问佛祖那句话呢。"

## ②

  八戒哼了一声。

> "佛祖已经宽恕我了，我忘了帮乌龟问那个问题。那只乌龟也没资格把我们翻到水里。"

> "把我们翻进水里也太粗鲁了，"悟净赞同地说。

  八戒叹了口气。

> "这东归之路跟西行一样艰难。"

## ③

> "大家好啊！"传来一声洪亮的招呼。

  旅人们转身，看见一个人朝他们快步走来。

> "是陈澄！"唐僧说。

  他握住那人的手。

> "又见面了，真好。"

> "看到你们真好啊，"陈澄微笑着，"你们到了西天吗？"

## ④

> "到了，"唐僧说，"那里太壮观了。大地充满美丽。"

  陈澄注意到岩石上铺着的经卷，眼珠子都瞪大了。

> "那些就是真经吗？"他轻声问道。

> "是的，"唐僧说，"但你看，全泡湿了。一只大乌龟把我们翻到了河里，"八戒叹着气说。

## ⑤

> "请到我家吧，"陈澄说，"你们可以在我家炉火边烘干经卷。全村人都想见见过佛祖的取经人呢。"

> "我来帮你，"八戒说。

  八戒抓住一卷用力一拉——可是经卷粘在了石头上。

> "师父，不用担心。"

## ⑦

  唐僧一脸困惑地看着悟空。

> "当然不完美。"

  唐僧摇了摇头。

> "那八戒呢？"

> "咱们都知道他可不完美。"

## ⑫

  突然，雷音寺的护法出现了。

> "走吧，"唐僧松了一口气，"我最终可以得到封号了。"

  护法念了一道咒语。旅人们再次飞向了东土。

# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred and Eight: Rewards

## ①

  The travelers flew through the sky over the Tang Empire. Tears of happiness rolled down the Tang monk's cheeks.

> "I see the Emperor's palace," said the monk.

  "I will wait here while you all deliver the true scriptures," said the guard.

  The Tang monk and his companions went down to earth and entered the palace.

## ②

  The Emperor's eyes lit up.

> "My brother!"

  He embraced the monk.

> "For many years I wondered if I'd ever see you again. Did you get the true scriptures?"

> "Yes," said the monk. "But please forgive me. The journey took much longer than I expected."

> "There's no need to apologize," said the Emperor.

## ③

  Then he noticed the monk's companions.

> "Who are they?"

  Sun Wukong stepped forward.

> "I am Sun Wukong, the Monkey King. I once defeated Heaven's entire army."

> "And I am Zhu Bajie," said the pig.

> "My name is Sha Wujing," said the purple spirit.

## ④

> "They protected me during the journey," the monk explained. "I couldn't have reached the Western Paradise without them."

  The Emperor bowed to Wukong and the others.

> "You don't need a bow," the monkey laughed. "We were happy to help."

  "Well, Wujing and I were," Bajie complained with a snort. "There were a lot of battles that night."

## ⑤

  The pig snorted. The Emperor asked the monk many questions about the journey. He listened in amazement to the monk's stories.

> "I will build a magnificent pagoda," the Emperor declared. "The true scriptures will be kept safe inside it."

  He put his hand on the monk's shoulder.

> "You will always be remembered as the brave monk who traveled to the West. Because of you, our people won't have to suffer anymore."

## ⑥

  After the feast was eaten, the Emperor asked the monk to read from the scriptures. The Tang monk smiled.

> "I would be happy to read from them."

## ⑦

  The monk sat on a platform where the scriptures had been placed in baskets. He unrolled a scroll. Suddenly, light filled the room. The Tang monk and his companions rose into the sky.

> "We should return to the Thunderclap Monastery," said the guard. "Buddha is waiting for you."

> "Yes, let's go," said the monk.

## ⑧

  The travelers were soon bowing before Buddha again. Other Buddhas and bodhisattvas stood behind him. Buddha addressed the horse first.

> "You carried the Tang monk on the journey and earned forgiveness for your crimes. You will now be a golden dragon in heaven."

  Light flashed as a large golden dragon soared into the sky.

## ⑨

> "Sha Wujing," said Buddha. "You too have earned forgiveness for your past crimes. You will now be a golden deity."

  Wujing's body turned gold. The spirit bowed.

> "Thank you, Buddha."

> "You have earned forgiveness, Bajie," said Buddha. "You will be an altar cleaner, an important deity who looks after Buddhist altars."

> "Thank you, Buddha," Bajie said.

## ⑩

  Buddha next turned to the Tang monk.

> "Because of you, the people in the East will never have to suffer again. You made a journey that no other person could make. You are now a Buddha."

  Light glowed around the monk, who bowed before Buddha.

> "Thank you, Buddha," said the monk.

## ⑪

  Finally Buddha turned to the monkey.

> "And you, Wukong," He began. "Your crimes long ago were terrible. But since then you have used your tremendous powers to do good deeds. You too are now a Buddha."

  Wukong bowed.

> "Thank you, Buddha."

## ⑫

  *Hey!* The monkey suddenly noticed that his head felt different. The headband was gone. Buddha smiled.

> "You don't need it anymore."

  Filled with joy, Wukong and the Tang monk joined the other Buddhas and bodhisattvas in the West. They have been in the Western Paradise ever since.

# 🇨🇳 第一百零八章：封号

## ①

  旅人们飞越大唐帝国的上空，唐僧激动得热泪盈眶。

> "我看到皇帝的宫殿了，"唐僧说。

> "我在这里等着，你们去送真经吧，"护法说。

  唐僧和徒伴们降落到地面，走进了皇宫。

## ②

  皇帝的双眼亮了起来。

> "御弟！"

  他拥抱了唐僧。

> "这么多年了，我一直在想还能不能再见到你。你取到真经了吗？"

> "取到了，"唐僧说，"但请陛下原谅我。这趟旅程比我预想的长了很多。"

> "不必道歉，"皇帝说。

## ③

  然后他注意到了唐僧身边的三位徒伴。

> "他们是谁？"

> "我是孙悟空，齐天大圣。我当年曾打败天庭千军万马。"

> "我是猪八戒。"八戒说。

> "我叫沙悟净。"悟净说。

## ④

> "这一路上他们保护了我，"唐僧解释道，"没有他们，我到不了西天。"

  皇帝向悟空等人鞠躬。

> "陛下不必行此大礼，"猴子笑道，"能帮上忙，我们很高兴。"

> "哼，悟净和我是很高兴。"八戒嘟囔了一声，"可那些打打杀杀的差事，我可吃了不少苦。"

## ⑤

  八戒哼了一声。皇帝问了唐僧许多关于旅途的问题，听他讲述一路上的故事，惊叹不已。

> "我要修建一座宏伟的宝塔，"皇帝宣布道，"真经将安放在塔中保存。"

  他把手搭在唐僧肩上。

> "你将永远被人们铭记，因为你是一位前往西方取经的英勇僧人。因为有您，我们的百姓再也不必受苦了。"

## ⑥

  盛宴之后，皇帝请唐僧诵读经书。唐僧微笑。

> "我愿意为大家诵读。"

## ⑦

  唐僧坐在讲台上，面前是经卷筐。他展开了一卷经书。突然，光芒充满了整间大殿。唐僧和他的徒伴们升上了天空。

> "我们应该回雷音寺了，"护法说，"佛祖在等着你们受封。"

> "好，走吧，"唐僧说。

## ⑧

  唐僧师徒又跪拜在了佛祖面前。佛祖身后站着众佛和菩萨。佛祖先对白龙马说道：

> "你一路上驮着唐僧取经，为你的罪过赎了罪。如今你将为天庭的一条金龙。"

  金光一闪，一条巨大的金龙飞上了天空。

## ⑨

> "沙悟净，"佛祖说，"你也为过去的罪过赎了罪。你将为天庭的南无八宝金身罗汉菩萨。"

  悟净的全身变得金光闪闪。他行了一个大礼。

> "感谢佛祖。"

> "猪八戒，你也赎了罪，"佛祖说，"你将为净坛使者，负责打扫和守护佛前供品。"

> "感谢佛祖。"八戒说。

## ⑩

  佛祖接下来转向唐僧。

> "因为有您，东土百姓再也不必受苦了。您走了一段无人能及的旅程。您现在是旃檀功德佛。"

> "感谢佛祖。"唐僧说。

> "悟空，"佛祖说，"你当年的罪行极为严重。但从此之后你用你莫大的法力做了无数善事。你现在是斗战胜佛。"

  悟空行了一个大礼。

> "感谢佛祖。"

> "你不再需要它了。"

  满怀着喜悦，悟空和唐僧与其他佛和菩萨一起留在了西天。从此以后，他们便一直在西天极乐世界。
