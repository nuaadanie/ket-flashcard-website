# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred and Five: The Thunderclap Monastery

## ①

  The travelers walked up the Spirit Mountain. The mountain slopes were covered in pine trees, bamboo, and flowers. Birds sang and small animals ran by.

  The Tang monk stopped walking and turned to his companions.

> "I want to thank you all," said the monk. "You have helped me to reach the Western Paradise. You saved me from demons countless times. I couldn't have gotten here without you."

## ②

  Wukong laughed.

> "You don't need to thank us, Master. We all help each other. You helped me to be good. Now I will earn forgiveness for the evil things I did long ago."

  Wujing nodded.

> "And I will earn forgiveness for breaking that glass vase in heaven."

> "I will earn forgiveness for causing trouble in heaven too," added Bajie. He waved a hand toward the horse. "And remember, that horse is really a dragon."

## ③

> "He accidentally sent rain to his father's palace. He too will earn forgiveness."

  Wujing smiled.

> "See, Master? We are all thankful."

## ④

  They continued up the mountain and were soon surrounded by clouds.

  Bright light shone from the Thunderclap Monastery above. The travelers arrived at a northern gate where two guards stood. Beyond the gate, the Thunderclap Monastery towers and archway sparkled with bright light.

> "Greetings," said the monk to the guards. "I am the Tang monk, and I am here to receive the true scriptures from Buddha."

> "You may enter," said one of the guards.

## ⑤

  The Tang monk and his companions reached a large hall. Buddha sat at the opposite end of the hall. Other Buddhas and bodhisattvas stood behind him. Guanyin was standing next to Buddha and smiling. The travelers bowed.

> "Congratulations," said Buddha. "You have all traveled far and faced many challenges. You have done well."

  Wukong's heart swelled. He wiped tears from his eye.

## ⑥

> "Thank you," said the Tang monk. "It was an honor to make the journey."

  Servants brought a meal to the travelers. When the travelers were done eating, a guard led them to a large room filled with magical light and mist. Wooden chests lined the walls.

## ⑦

> "Marvelous," the Tang monk murmured.

> "The true scriptures are written on these scrolls," said the guard.

  He picked out three scrolls and handed them to the Tang monk. Speechless, the monk gazed at the scrolls. Then he handed them to Wujing and Bajie. The two spirits carefully wrapped the scrolls and placed them in a bag.

## ⑧

  Once again the travelers bowed before Buddha.

> "Take the true scriptures back to the Tang Empire," said Buddha.

> "Another long journey," Bajie murmured.

> "Stop complaining, Bajie," whispered Wukong.

> "Don't tell me what to do," Bajie whispered back.

> "Both of you stop arguing," whispered Wujing.

## ⑨

  Buddha was still talking.

> "The scriptures will teach the people there to be good and to treat each other kindly. But take good care of the scriptures. They are extremely valuable. Once you have delivered them, you shall come back here to receive your awards."

## ⑩

> "Where are you, Buddha?" said Wukong. "We will protect the scriptures with our lives."

  A guard offered to carry the travelers to the Tang Empire.

> "Thank you," said the Tang monk.

> "I'll hold on tight," said Bajie.

  The guard recited a spell and then flew out of the monastery with the travelers.

## ⑪

  Buddha turned to Guanyin.

> "The Tang monk and his companions have done well."

  Guanyin nodded.

> "Wukong especially has learned a lot during the journey. He isn't violent anymore. I believe he is now a good Buddhist."

## ⑫

  Just then a messenger ran into the hall.

> "Bodhisattva!" he cried. "We have a problem."

> "Our problem?" Buddha asked.

# 🇨🇳 第一百零五章：雷音寺

## ①

  旅人们沿着灵山向上攀登。山坡上长满了松树、翠竹和鲜花。鸟儿歌唱，小动物从身边跑过。

  唐僧停下脚步，转向同伴们。

> "我想谢谢你们各位，"唐僧说，"是你们帮我到达了西天。无数次你们从妖怪手中救了我。没有你们，我走不到这里。"

## ②

  悟空笑了。

> "师父，您不必谢我们。我们互相帮助。您帮我向善。如今我要为我早年的恶行赎罪了。"

  悟净点了点头。

> "我也要为打碎天庭琉璃盏的事赎罪。"

> "我也要为在天庭惹的祸赎罪，"八戒补充道。他朝马的方向挥了挥手，"别忘了，那匹马可是一条真龙。"

## ③

> "他不小心把玉帝宫殿的雨水下错了地方。他也该赎罪。"

  悟净微笑了。

> "师父您看吧，我们都很感恩。"

## ④

  他们继续向上攀登，很快被云雾环绕。

  上方雷音寺闪耀着明亮的光芒。旅人来到北门，两位护法站在那里守卫。门内，雷音寺的塔楼和牌坊闪闪发光。

> "两位好，"唐僧向护法说，"我是唐僧，来佛祖这里求取真经。"

> "请进吧，"一位护法说。

## ⑤

  唐僧和同伴们来到了一座大殿。佛祖端坐在大殿尽头，身后站着众佛和菩萨。观音站在佛祖身旁，面带微笑。旅人们鞠躬行礼。

> "恭喜你们，"佛祖说，"你们远道而来，历经千难万险。你们做得很好。"

  悟空心中感慨万千，他抹去了眼中的泪水。

## ⑥

> "感谢佛祖，"唐僧说，"能踏上这段旅程是我的荣幸。"

  侍从给旅人们端来了饭菜。吃完饭后，一位护法把他们领进了一间充满灵光和仙雾的大厅。墙壁上排列着许多木箱。

## ⑦

> "太壮观了，"唐僧喃喃道。

> "真经就是写在这些经卷上的，"护法说。

  他挑出三部经卷，递给了唐僧。唐僧说不出话来，凝望着经卷。然后他接过经卷交给了悟净和八戒。两位仙人小心翼翼地包好经卷，放进了包袱。

## ⑧

  旅人们再次在佛祖面前行礼。

> "把真经带回大唐吧，"佛祖说。

> "又是长途跋涉，"八戒嘟囔着。

> "别抱怨了，八戒，"悟空低声说。

> "你少管我，"八戒回嘴。

> "你们两个别吵了，"悟净低声说。

## ⑨

  佛祖继续讲话。

> "经卷将教导那里的人民向善、彼此友善相处。但你们要好好保护经卷，它们极其珍贵。送到经卷之后，你们再回到这里来接受封号。"

## ⑩

> "佛祖您放一百个心，"悟空说，"我们用性命来保护经卷。"

  一位护法主动提出护送旅人回大唐。

> "谢谢您，"唐僧说。

> "我可抓牢了，"八戒说。

  护法念了一道咒语，便带着旅人们飞出了寺院。

## ⑪

  佛祖转向观音。

> "唐僧师徒做得很好。"

  观音点了点头。

> "特别是悟空一路学到了很多。他不再暴躁了。我相信他现在是个好佛徒了。"

## ⑫

  就在此时，一个信使跑进了大殿。

> "菩萨大人！"他喊道，"出了个问题。"

> "什么问题？"佛祖问道。
