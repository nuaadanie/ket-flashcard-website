# Journey to the West · Chapter One Hundred: A Terrible Robbery

## ①

  For over a month, the travelers stayed at Squire Kou's house. Every time they tried to leave, the Squire begged them to stay longer. Finally, one morning during breakfast, the Tang monk insisted on leaving.

> "We'll never fetch the true scriptures if we stay here."

## ②

> "Please, don't leave!" said Squire Kou. "You are very important monks. Having you as guests is a good deed."

> "I'd like to stay here," said Bajie. He took a bite of a peach. "No — Master's right," said Bajie. "We must continue our journey."

## ③

  Lady Kou grew angry.

> "You monks are ungrateful. We have fed you and taken care of you. Yet you can't wait to leave."

> "We are very grateful," said the Tang monk. "But we cannot stay any longer."

## ④

  After breakfast, Wujing got the horse from Squire Kou's stable. Bajie gathered the bags. The Tang monk bade Squire and Lady Kou farewell, and the travelers headed west.

> "They are ungrateful," muttered Lady Kou.

## ⑤

  The next morning, Lady Kou ran into the town's courthouse. Tears filled her eyes.

> "I must speak with the judge," she cried.

  The judge rushed to her.

> "Lady Kou, what's wrong?"

## ⑥

> "Robbers broke into our house last night," said Lady Kou breathlessly. "They stole many valuable items and two of our horses. Squire Kou tried to stop them, but the robbers — they killed my husband."

  Lady Kou burst into tears. The judge gasped.

> "Oh no! Did you see the robbers?"

## ⑦

  Lady Kou sniffed.

> "Yes. And I know who they are."

> "Who are they?" asked the judge.

  Lady Kou gritted her teeth.

> "The Tang monk and his companions."

## ⑧

  The judge turned to a soldier.

> "Gather some soldiers and arrest the Tang monk and his companions."

> "I'm glad we're headed west again," said the Tang monk as the travelers walked. "I'm eager to reach the Western Paradise."

  Wujing nodded.

> "We were delayed at Squire Kou's house for too long."

  Bajie snorted.

> "We should have stayed longer."

## ⑨

  Suddenly a group of men stepped onto the road. They carried swords and axes. Their horses carried several large bundles.

> "Give us your money!" shouted one of them.

  Wujing pulled out his iron bar.

> "Wujing," said the Tang monk. "Remember, you must not harm people."

> "I'm just going to talk to them, Master," Wujing smiled.

## ⑩

  He turned to the man who had shouted.

> "We have no money."

> "No money, huh?" said the man. "Then we're going to beat up all of you."

  Wujing laughed.

> "You don't realize who I am. I'm Sha Wujing, who served the Curtain-Lifting General in heaven."

> "We don't care who you are," said the man. "We're dangerous robbers. Last night we robbed a very wealthy squire."

## ⑪

  The Tang monk raised his eyebrows.

> "The squire..."

  Wukong quickly recited a spell, which froze the robbers. Wujing rushed to the horses and opened one of the bundles. Inside were dishes, jewels, and other valuable items.

## ⑫

> "These items belong to Squire Kou," Wujing gasped. "So do the horses."

  The Tang monk bit his lip.

> "Squire Kou is a good man. We must return his belongings."

  Everyone agreed. Wukong recited another spell and the robbers became unfrozen. The monkey grew large, and fire burned in his eyes.

## ⑬

> "Get out of here!" he thundered. "And never steal again!"

  The robbers screamed and ran away. The travelers headed back toward town with the Squire's horses. Soon they saw a group of soldiers on horseback.

> "Those soldiers are riding toward us," said Wujing.

> "I wonder what they want," said the Tang monk.

## ⑭

  One of the soldiers pointed a sword at the travelers.

> "Stop, thieves! You're all under arrest."

# 🇨🇳 第一百章：可怕的劫案

## ①

  一行人在寇员外家住了一个多月。每次他们想走，员外都苦苦挽留。一天早上吃早饭的时候，唐僧坚决说要走。

> "再待下去，我们就永远取不到真经了。"

## ②

> "求求你们，别走！"寇员外说，"你们是不凡的僧人。留你们做客也是做善事。"

> "我倒想留在这里，"八戒咬了一口桃子。"不过——师父说得对，"八戒说，"我们得继续赶路了。"

## ③

  寇夫人恼了起来。

> "你们这些僧人真是忘恩负义。我们管你们吃管你们住，你们却急着想走。"

> "我们非常感激，"唐僧说，"但我们不能再待下去了。"

## ④

  吃完早饭，悟净到马厩牵马。八戒收拾包裹。唐僧辞别了寇员外和寇夫人，一行往西出发。

> "他们真是忘恩负义，"寇夫人嘟囔着。

## ⑤

  第二天一早，寇夫人跑到了镇上的公堂。她眼中含着泪。

> "我要见大人！"她哭喊着。

  县官急忙迎出来。

> "寇夫人，怎么了？"

## ⑥

> "昨晚有强盗闯进了我们家，"寇夫人气喘吁吁地说，"他们偷了许多贵重财物和两匹马。寇员外想拦他们，可那些强盗——他们把我丈夫杀了。"

  寇夫人放声大哭。县官倒抽一口冷气。

> "天哪！你看清那些强盗了吗？"

## ⑦

  寇夫人擦了擦眼泪。

> "看清楚了，我知道他们是谁。"

> "他们是谁？"县官问道。

  寇夫人咬了咬牙。

> "就是那个唐僧和他的徒弟们。"

## ⑧

  县官转身对一个士兵下令。

> "带人立刻去抓捕唐僧和他的徒弟们！"

> "这次我们又往西走了，真高兴，"唐僧一行人走在路上说，"我迫不及待想到西天了。"

  悟净点了点头。

> "我们在寇员外家耽误太久了。"

  八戒哼了一声。

> "真该再多住几天。"

## ⑨

  突然，一伙人拦住了去路。他们手持刀斧，马背上驮着几个大包裹。

> "把钱交出来！"其中一个人大叫着。

  悟净抽出了降妖宝杖。

> "悟净，"唐僧说，"记住，万万不可伤人。"

> "我只是和他们说说话，师父，"悟净微微一笑。

## ⑩

  他转向那个大叫的人。

> "我们没有钱。"

> "没钱，是吧？"那汉子说，"那我们就要把你们统统打一顿。"

  悟净哈哈大笑。

> "你们不知道我的厉害。我曾在天庭做卷帘大将。"

> "不管你是谁，"那汉子说，"我们是厉害的强盗。昨晚我们抢了一个非常有钱的员外。"

## ⑪

  唐僧挑了挑眉毛。

> "员外家……"

  悟空立刻念了一道定身法，把强盗们定住了。悟净冲到马旁，打开一个包裹，里面是器皿、珠宝等各种贵重物品。

## ⑫

> "这些东西都是寇员外家的，"悟净惊呼道，"马也是他的。"

  唐僧咬了咬嘴唇。

> "寇员外是个好人。我们必须把东西还给他。"

  大家一致同意。悟空又念了一道解咒，强盗们恢复了自由。猴子现出法天象地，眼中冒火。

## ⑬

> "快滚！"他喝斥道，"今后再不可做强盗！"

  强盗们惨叫一声，落荒而逃。一行旅人牵着员外的马返回镇上。不久他们看见一队骑马的士兵。

> "那些士兵正朝我们过来，"悟净说。

> "不知他们要做什么，"唐僧说。

## ⑭

  一个士兵用剑指着旅人们。

> "站住，盗贼！你们全被捕了！"
