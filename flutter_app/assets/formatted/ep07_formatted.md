# Journey to the West · Chapter Seven: The Land of Darkness

## ①

  Wukong touched the iron pillar and felt it move.
  > "Hmm," he thought, "this piece of iron is truly magical. I bet it will do whatever I wish."
  > "Shrink!" commanded Wukong.
  The pillar shrank. Wukong was now just a little taller than before. He lifted it and swung it around.
  > "This is a perfect weapon for me," he said.
  > "I can't believe he can lift it," exclaimed the Dragon Queen.
  Wukong made the bar even smaller. It became as tiny as a needle. He placed it inside his ear.
  > "I've got what I want," said Wukong. "Goodbye."
  He turned to leave.
  > "He was very rude," said the Dragon Queen.
  The Dragon King clenched his fists.
  > "I'm going to report him to the Jade Emperor."

## ②

  Back at the Mountain of Flowers and Fruit, the others were amazed by Wukong's new weapon.
  > "No one will ever attack us again!" declared Wukong.
  The monkeys and apes cheered. They held a great feast that lasted until late at night. Wukong went to bed happily. But as he fell asleep, two spirits seized him.
  > "What are you doing?" demanded Wukong. "Let go of me!"
  He kicked and struggled, but the spirits dragged him out of the cave. They walked toward a dark and eerie city. A sign above the gate read: **Land of Darkness**.

## ③

  > "The Land of Darkness?" cried Wukong. "That's where people go when they die. I'm supposed to live forever!"
  He pulled his arms free.
  > "No way!" he roared. The iron bar quickly grew.
  Whack! He knocked out the first spirit. Crack! He knocked out the second.
  > "Who can stop me now?" he shouted.
  He marched toward the Palace of Darkness, swinging his iron bar. Demons and ghosts fled. A tall spirit stepped out of the palace.

## ④

  > "What's going on out here?" asked the spirit.
  > "Who are you?" demanded Wukong.
  > "I am the Judge of the Dead," replied the spirit. "Why are you causing all this trouble?"
  > "I want answers," said Wukong. "I was brought here. Tell me now!"
  The Judge raised his hands.
  > "If you were brought here, it means your time on earth is over."

## ⑤

  > "No way!" yelled Wukong. "I'm going to live forever!"
  The Judge looked nervous.
  > "Let's check the directory," he said. "The directory contains the name and age of every creature on earth. It states when each creature will die."

## ⑥

  Inside the palace, the Judge opened a large book.
  > "What is your name?" he asked.
  Wukong told him. The Judge flipped through the pages.
  > "Here it is," said the Judge. "It says: Sun Wukong, born from a rock 300 years ago. Died today."
  Wukong shook his head.
  > "No. I'm going to live forever."
  > "The directory isn't wrong," said the Judge. "You'll have to stay here now."

## ⑦

  Wukong raised his iron bar and glared at the Judge.
  > "Give me that book."
  The Judge hesitated.
  > "I can't give it to you."
  > "Now," said Wukong.
  The Judge handed the book to Wukong. Wukong pulled two of his hairs and blew on them. They turned into a brush and a jar of ink. He drew a line through his name.
  > "You can't do that!" cried the Judge.
  > "Yes, I can," said Wukong.
  Next, Wukong found the names of all the other monkeys and apes. He crossed out their names as well.
  > "Now I really will live forever," said Wukong. "And so will all of my subjects!"

# 🇨🇳 第七章：地府销籍

## ①

  悟空伸手摸了摸铁柱，感觉到它在移动。
  > "嗯，"他想，"这件铁器真是神奇。我敢打赌它会随我心意。"
  > "变小！"悟空命令道。
  铁柱立刻缩小。悟空的身高只比原来高一点。他举起铁柱，挥舞了两下。
  > "这对我来说简直是完美的武器，"他说。
  > "我简直不敢相信他竟然能举起来，"龙后惊叹道。
  悟空让铁棒变得更小。悟空小得如同一枚针。他将铁棒塞进耳朵里。
  > "我要的东西到手了，"悟空道，"告辞。"
  他转身离开。
  > "他太无礼了，"龙后说道。
  龙王握紧拳头。
  > "我要去禀告玉皇大帝。"

## ②

  回到花果山，众人对悟空的新武器惊讶不已。
  > "以后再也没谁敢攻击我们了！"悟空宣称。
  猴子、猿猴们齐声欢呼。他们举办了盛宴，一直热闹到深夜。悟空快活地上床睡觉。但刚入睡，两个小鬼抓住了他。
  > "你们干什么？"悟空喝问，"放开我！"
  他拳打脚踢，却仍被小鬼拖出洞府。他们走向一座阴暗诡异的城市，城门上挂着一块牌子：**地府**。

## ③

  > "地府？"悟空惊呼，"人死后才去的地方。我可是要永生不死的！"
  他猛地挣脱双臂。
  > "休想！"他吼道。铁棒迅速变长。
  砰！第一鬼被打晕。咔嚓！第二鬼也被打翻。
  > "现在谁能挡住我？"他喝道。
  他朝地府宫殿走去，一路挥舞铁棒。妖魔鬼怪四散奔逃。一个高大的鬼差从宫殿里走出来。

## ④

  > "外面乱哄哄的怎么回事？"鬼差问道。
  > "你是谁？"悟空喝问。
  > "我是阎王，"鬼差答，"你在此地胡闹什么？"
  > "我来问个明白，"悟空道，"我被带来这里，立刻告诉我！"
  阎王举起双手。
  > "如果你被带来这里，那就表示你在阳间的寿命已尽。"

## ⑤

  > "没门！"悟空吼道，"我要永生不死！"
  阎王有些慌张。
  > "我们查一下生死簿，"他说，"生死簿上记载着阳间每一个生灵的姓名和寿数，何时命终都有记录。"

## ⑥

  宫殿内，阎王翻开一本大书。
  > "你叫什么名字？"他问道。
  悟空告知。阎王翻阅书页。
  > "找到了，"阎王说，"上面写着：孙悟空，石头所生，三百年前出生，今日身亡。"
  悟空摇头。
  > "不，我要永生不死。"
  > "生死簿不会错，"阎王道，"你现在必须留在这里。"

## ⑦

  悟空举起铁棒，怒视阎王。
  > "把书给我。"
  阎王犹豫不决。
  > "我不能给你。"
  > "现在就给！"悟空喝道。
  阎王无奈，只得将书递上。悟空拔下两根毫毛，吹了一口气，毛发变成笔和墨汁。他用力划去自己的名字。
  > "你不能这样做！"阎王惊呼。
  > "我当然能，"悟空答道。
  接着，悟空又找到所有猴子、猿猴的名字，一一划去。
  > "这下我真的永生不死了，"悟空道，"我的所有子民也都能永生！"
