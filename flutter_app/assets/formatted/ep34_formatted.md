# Journey to the West · Chapter Thirty-Four: Tricked by the Princess

## ①

  The Tang Monk was tied up in a room. He heard his companions yelling at the monster outside.

> "Hand over the Tang Monk now!" shouted Bajie.

  The Tang Monk heard the sound of weapons clashing.

> "I hope they rescue me," said the monk.

  A young woman entered the room.

> "Who are you?" asked the Tang Monk.

> "I'm a prisoner here," said the woman. "That monster kidnapped me thirteen years ago and won't let me leave. Will you help me?"

## ②

> "I wish I could help you," said the Tang Monk. He showed the young woman his ropes.

> "I can help you get free," said the young woman. "If I do, will you deliver a letter to my parents?"

> "Of course," said the monk. "Who are your parents?"

> "They're the king and queen of the Precious Image Kingdom," said the young woman. "My name is Princess Hundred Flowers."

## ③

  Bajie and Wu Jing were still fighting the monster when the princess ran outside.

> "Monster!" she cried. "I must ask you a favor."

  Right away, the monster stopped fighting and turned to the princess.

> "What can I do for you, my sweet?" asked the monster.

> "There's a monk inside," said the princess. "It makes me so sad to see him tied up. Won't you let him go?"

  The monster hesitated.

> "That monk isn't an ordinary monk. He's the..."

## ④

  Princess Hundred Flowers pretended to cry. The monster held up his hands.

> "Okay, okay, sweetie. You know I only want you to be happy. The monk may go."

  The monster followed the princess back inside. Bajie and Wu Jing looked at each other in confusion.

> "The monster is going to free him," mumbled Bajie.

  A moment later, the Tang Monk came out.

> "There's something we must do," he said quietly to his companions.

## ⑤

  Later that day, the travelers reached the palace of the Precious Image Kingdom. The queen was reading the letter from her daughter. Tears were in her eyes.

> "Our poor daughter," said the queen. "She's been a prisoner all this time."

  The king stood up, his eyes blazing with anger. He called to his general, who was standing nearby.

> "Gather your troops," said the king. "I want my daughter back. And I want that monster destroyed."

## ⑥

> "Forgive me, Your Majesty," said the general. "But we are just men. How can we defeat such a powerful monster?"

> "My two companions are powerful spirits, Your Majesty," the Tang Monk spoke up. "I'm sure they can rescue your daughter."

  Soon, Bajie and Wu Jing were back at the pagoda. The pig swung his rake. *Smash!* The doors broke.

> "Get out here, you rotten monster!" he shouted.

  The monster ran outside.

> "You brutes!" he yelled. "I let the monk go free. Why did you smash my door down?"

> "We're here for the princess. The king of the Precious Image Kingdom sent us."

> "You can't take the princess away," said the monster. "We are in love."

## ⑦

  Bajie and Wu Jing attacked. The monster blew off their weapons. They fought for over an hour. Finally, tired from fighting, Bajie turned and ran.

> "Bajie! Where are you going?" cried the Tang Monk.

  The monster knocked out Wu Jing and tied him up. He dragged Wu Jing into the pagoda and locked him in a room.

> "The king knows that the princess is here," said the monster to himself. "But how?"

  He tapped his chin and thought.

> "The princess must have asked that monk to go to the king." His eyes became dark and angry. "She tricked me!"

# 🇨🇳 第三十四章：公主的诡计


## ①

  唐僧被绑在一个房间里。他听到同伴们在外面冲妖怪大喊。

> "马上把唐僧交出来！"八戒喊道。

  唐僧听到兵器碰撞的声音。

> "希望他们能把我救出去，"唐僧说。

  这时，一个年轻女子走了进来。

> "你是谁？"唐僧问道。

> "我也是被关在这里的囚犯，"女子说，"那个妖怪十三年前把我劫走，一直不让我离开。你愿意帮我吗？"

## ②

> "我真想帮你，"唐僧说着，把身上的绳子给她看。

> "我可以帮你解开绳子，"年轻女子说，"你能帮我给父母送一封信吗？"

> "当然可以，"唐僧说，"你父母是谁？"

> "他们是宝象国的国王和王后，"年轻女子说，"我叫百花公主。"

## ③

  八戒和悟净还在跟妖怪战斗，百花公主跑了出去。

> "妖怪！"她叫道，"我有事求你。"

  妖怪立刻停下手来，转向公主。

> "有什么事，我的心上人？"妖怪问道。

> "里面有个和尚，"公主说，"看到他绑着，我心里难受。你能放了他吗？"

  妖怪犹豫了一下。

> "那个和尚可不是普通人。他就是......"

## ④

  百花公主假装哭了起来。妖怪举起双手。

> "好好好，别哭了。你知道我只要你开心就行了。放那个和尚走吧。"

  妖怪跟着公主回到屋里。八戒和悟净面面相觑，一脸不解。

> "妖怪要放他了，"八戒低声嘟囔。

  不一会儿，唐僧从里面走了出来。

> "我们还有件事要做，"他小声对同伴们说。

## ⑤

  那天晚些时候，一行人来到宝象国宫殿。王后正在读女儿托唐僧捎来的信，泪水盈满了眼眶。

> "我可怜的女儿，"王后说，"这么多年，她一直被关着。"

  国王猛地站起来，眼中燃起怒火。他叫来身边的将军。

> "集合军队，"国王说，"我要接回女儿，还要消灭那个妖怪。"

## ⑥

> "请陛下恕罪，"将军说，"我们只是凡人，怎么能对付那么强大的妖怪？"

> "我的两个同伴法力高强，"唐僧开口说，"他们一定能救出公主。"

  没过多久，八戒和悟净就回到了那座塔。八戒挥起钉耙，用力一砸，把门劈开了。

> "滚出来，你这个可恶的妖怪！"他大声喊道。

  妖怪冲了出来。

> "你们这些粗人！"他大吼，"我都把和尚放了，你们为什么要砸烂我的门？"

> "我们是来要公主的。宝象国国王派我们来的。"

> "你们休想带走公主，"妖怪说，"我们两情相悦。"

## ⑦

  八戒和悟净发起进攻。妖怪打飞了他们的武器。双方战斗了一个多小时。最后，八戒疲于战斗，转身就跑。

> "八戒！你往哪里跑？"唐僧叫道。

  妖怪打昏了悟净，把他绑了起来，拖进塔里锁进了一个房间。

> "国王知道公主在这里了，"妖怪自言自语，"可他怎么知道的？"

  他托着下巴想了起来。

> "一定是公主让那个和尚去给国王报信的。"他的眼中闪过一丝怒意，"她骗了我！"
