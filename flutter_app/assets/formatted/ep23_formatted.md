# Journey to the West · Chapter Twenty-Three: Great Protectors

## ①

  The creature had a pig's mouth and ears, but he ran on two legs like a person.

  He swung the rake again. Wukong blocked the rake with his iron rod. Clang! Then he swung the rod at the pig's feet.

  "Oh!" cried the pig. He fell face down in the dirt. He jumped back up. "You'll wish you hadn't done that!"

  Wukong leaped out of the way as the pig's rake slammed down, mashing a boulder. The two fought, kicking up dust and rocks. The Tang monk hid behind his horse.

## ②

  "Surrender now!" Wukong swung his iron rod again. "I am Sun Wukong! 500 years ago I caused lots of trouble in heaven. The Jade Emperor's entire army couldn't stop me."

  "I've heard of you," said the pig. "But you don't seem so powerful to me." He lunged at the monkey again.

  Wukong jumped out of the way.

  "I thought you were being punished," said the pig. He swung his rake again. "Weren't you trapped under a mountain?"

## ③

  "I'm free now," said Wukong. "I'm going to the west with the Tang monk to —"

  "The Tang monk!" cried the pig. He dropped his rake. "I'm supposed to go with him too."

  "You are?" asked Wukong.

  "I was banished to earth as a punishment," said the pig. "My name is Zhu Bajie. I caused trouble in heaven too. Guanyin said I would be forgiven if I help protect the Tang monk."

## ④

  Wukong rubbed his chin. "Okay," he said. "You can carry the bags."

  Zhu Bajie joined the group and the three of them continued west. As they traveled, the pig kept complaining.

  "These bags are so heavy. Do we really need to bring them?"

  "I'm not complaining," said Wukong. "Going to the western paradise is supposed to be difficult." "Wow."

  "I'm hungry too," the pig moaned. "When are we going to eat lunch?"

## ⑤

  The Tang monk frowned. "We just ate breakfast. How can you be hungry again?"

  They went around a bend and saw a large tiger.

  Bajie and Wukong quickly pulled out their weapons.

  "Who are you?" yelled Bajie. "What are you doing here?"

## ⑥

  "My master, the Great King Yellow Wind, is hosting a feast," said the tiger. He looked at the Tang monk. "I'm gathering people to eat."

  The Tang monk's face turned pale and nearly fell off his horse.

  "Don't you dare harm my master," Bajie warned. "He is the Tang monk. He is going to the west to get the true scriptures."

  The tiger's eyes opened wide. "I heard that eating the Tang monk will make someone live forever."

  The tiger showed his claws and then he lunged at Bajie. The pig hit him with the rake. The tiger tumbled over. Wukong ran toward the tiger with his iron rod. The tiger dodged the rod and then almost got hit by the rake again. He turned and fled.

## ⑦

  Wukong and Bajie ran after the tiger. For a moment they lost him. Then they spotted a flash of orange behind some bushes.

  "There he is," whispered Bajie.

  The monkey and pig sneaked up to the bushes. But the tiger wasn't there. Instead, a tiger's skin lay over a rock.

  Wukong laughed. "He was so scared he ran right out of his skin." He slapped Bajie on the back.

  "We are great protectors." The two friends walked back to the road, laughing.

## ⑧

  But suddenly their laughter stopped.

  "Oh no!" cried Wukong. "Where is the Tang monk?"

# 🇨🇳 第二十三章：护法


## ①

  那个怪物长着猪嘴和猪耳朵，但却像人一样用两条腿奔跑。

  他又挥起钉耙砸来。悟空用铁棒挡住。铛！然后他挥棒打向猪的脚。

  "哎哟！"猪痛叫一声，脸朝下摔倒在泥地里。他又跳了起来，"你会后悔的！"

  猪的钉耙重重劈下，把一块大石头砸成了碎块。悟空闪身躲开。两人打得尘土飞扬。唐僧躲到马的后面。

## ②

  "现在就投降！"悟空再次挥起铁棒，"我是孙悟空！五百年前我大闹天宫，玉皇大帝的十万天兵天将都奈何不了我。"

  "我听说过你的大名，"猪说，"但在我看来你也没什么了不起。"他又朝悟空扑了过来。

  悟空跳开躲避。

  "我还以为你在受罚呢，"猪说着一耙挥来，"你不是被压在山下吗？"

## ③

  "我现在自由了，"悟空说，"我和唐僧一起去西天——"

  "唐僧！"猪叫了一声，放下了钉耙，"我也应该跟他一起去。"

  "是吗？"悟空问。

  "我是被贬下凡间作为惩罚，"猪说，"我叫猪八戒。我也在天宫闯了祸。观音说只要我保护唐僧西行，就能得到宽恕。"

## ④

  悟空摸着下巴想了想，"好吧，"他说，"你就负责背行李。"

  猪八戒加入了队伍，三人继续向西行进。一路上，猪八戒不停地抱怨。

  "这行李太重了。我们真的需要带这么多东西吗？"

  "我可没有抱怨，"悟空说，"去西天的旅途本来就该艰苦。"

  "我还饿了呢，"猪八戒呻吟道，"什么时候可以吃午饭？"

## ⑤

  唐僧皱起眉头，"我们才刚吃过早饭，你怎么又饿了？"

  他们转过一个弯，看见了一只大老虎。

  八戒和悟空立刻掏出武器。

  "你是谁？"八戒喝道，"在这里干什么？"

## ⑥

  "我家大王黄风大王正在摆宴，"老虎说。他打量了一下唐僧，"我正在找人回来吃肉。"

  唐僧脸色苍白，差点从马上摔下来。

  "你胆敢伤害我师父，"八戒警告道，"他就是唐僧，要去西天取真经的。"

  老虎瞪大眼睛，"我听说吃了唐僧肉可以长生不老。"

  老虎亮出爪子，朝八戒扑去。八戒一钉耙打中了他，老虎翻倒在地。悟空举铁棒奔来，老虎躲过铁棒，又差点被钉耙打中，转身就逃。

## ⑦

  悟空和八戒追赶老虎。一时看不见他的踪影，随后他们发现灌木丛后闪过一片橙色。

  "在那里，"八戒低声说。

  悟空和八戒悄悄靠近灌木丛，可是老虎不在那里，只有一张虎皮盖在一块石头上。

  悟空笑了，"他吓得连皮都脱了。"他拍了一下八戒的背。

  "我们真是了不起的保护者。"两位朋友笑着走回路上。

## ⑧

  然而，笑声突然停住了。

  "不好了！"悟空喊道，"唐僧在哪里？"
