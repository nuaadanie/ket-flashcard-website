# Journey to the West · Chapter Forty-Three: A Visit from Guanyin

## ①

  Golden Horn stared at Wukong.

> "I doubt you have a gourd like mine."

> "I do," said Wukong. He held up the real gourd.

  The demon looked carefully at the gourd.

> "That looks like my gourd. But my gourd is magical."

> "My gourd is magical too," said Wukong.

  The demon rubbed his chin.

> "What can your gourd do?"

> "It can hold a demon," said Wukong. "All I have to do is say a demon's name. The demon will get sucked in and then turn into liquid."

  Golden Horn raised his eyebrows.

> "My gourd can do that too."

> "Let's see whose gourd works better. I'll go first," said Wukong. "I forgot your name."

> "Kong Sun Wukong!" said the demon.

> "Oh yes, now I remember," said Wukong.

  Golden Horn held up his gourd.

> "Kong Sun Wukong!"

  Nothing happened. Golden Horn looked at his gourd.

> "Why isn't my gourd working?"

## ②

> "Let me try mine," said Wukong. "Golden Horn!"

  Instantly the demon was sucked into the gourd. Wukong laughed.

> "Golden Horn will soon be turned into liquid."

  He closed the gourd and ran into the cave. Several demons were inside. The monkey waved his gourd at them.

> "Bajie! Wujing! Get out of here before I clobber you all!" he shouted.

  The demons screamed and ran out of the cave.

  After a quick search of the cave, Wukong freed his companions.

> "We thought you were trapped under a mountain," said the Tang monk.

> "I was freed a couple of hours ago," said Wukong. He untied the monk.

> "A couple of hours ago?" asked Bajie. "What were you doing for all that time? We were scared to death in here!"

  Wukong shrugged.

> "I couldn't free you right away. I had to get rid of the demon first."

  The monkey untied Bajie and Wujing.

## ③

  They all went outside.

> "It's still early in the afternoon," said Wujing. "Let's start walking now."

> "Yes," said the Tang monk. "I want to be as far from this mountain as I can by nighttime."

  The travelers hurried west along the road. They hadn't gone far when an old man approached them.

> "Give me back my gourd!" said the old man.

  Wukong stepped forward.

> "What gourd? I don't have a gourd."

> "I know you have my gourd!" said the man.

> "I don't know what you're talking about," said the monkey.

  There was a flash of light. Then Laozi the sage stood before the travelers. He looked angry.

> "Give me my gourd, Wukong!" bellowed the sage.

## ④

  Wukong smiled.

> "Aha! Laozi! I was just joking around. I knew it was you. Here's your gourd."

  Wukong took the gourd out of his robe and handed it to the sage.

> "Why did that demon have your gourd?" asked the monkey.

> "Golden Horn works for me," said Laozi. "I sent him here to cause trouble for you. I wanted to make sure you are all serious about reaching the western paradise. You fought well, Wukong."

  Wukong scratched his head.

> "Golden Horn is probably all liquified by now."

> "No," said Laozi. "The gourd won't harm him. Goodbye."

  The sage vanished. The group continued west.

## ⑤

  As night fell, they arrived at a monastery. After a small dinner with the monastery's monks, everyone went to bed.

  The Tang monk was almost asleep when he heard a moaning sound from outside. The monk sat up, trembling with fear.

  Slowly his door creaked open. A man stood in the doorway, dripping wet. He looked at the monk and held out his hands.

> "Please help me."

# 🇨🇳 第四十三章：观音来访


## ①

  金角大王盯着悟空。

> "我才不信你也有像我这样的葫芦。"

> "我有，"悟空掏出真葫芦举了起来。

  妖怪仔细端详着葫芦。

> "那看起来跟我的葫芦很像。但是我的葫芦是有法力的。"

> "我的葫芦也有法力，"悟空说。

  妖怪摸着下巴。

> "你的葫芦能干什么？"

> "它能装妖怪，"悟空说，"只要我叫出妖怪的名字，他就会自动被吸进去，然后化成液体。"

  金角大王挑了挑眉。

> "我的葫芦也能做到。"

> "那就看看谁的葫芦更灵吧。我先来，"悟空说，"嗯，我忘了你的名字了。"

> "行者孙！"妖怪喊道。

> "哦对，我想起来了，"悟空说。

  金角大王举起他的葫芦。

> "行者孙！"

  什么也没发生。金角大王看了看自己的葫芦。

> "我的葫芦怎么不灵了？"

## ②

> "看我的，"悟空说，"金角大王！"

  眨眼间，妖怪被吸进了葫芦里。悟空大笑。

> "金角大王马上就化成液体了。"

  他盖好葫芦，冲进洞府。里面还有几个小妖。悟空举起葫芦朝他们一扬。

> "八戒！悟净！快跑！不然我统统收拾你们！"他大喊。

  小妖们尖叫着跑出了山洞。

  悟空在洞里快速搜索了一下，解救了同伴。

> "我们以为你被压在山下了呢，"唐僧说。

> "我几个小时前就已经脱身了，"悟空替师父松了绑。

> "几个小时前？"八戒问，"那这么久你在干什么？我们在这里怕得要死！"

  悟空耸了耸肩。

> "我没法马上救你们，得先把妖怪解决了才行。"

  悟空又替八戒和悟净松了绑。

## ③

  大家走到洞外。

> "现在还是下午，天色还早，"悟净说，"我们赶紧赶路吧。"

> "对，"唐僧说，"天黑之前，走得离这座山越远越好。"

  一行人沿路向西赶去。走了没多久，一个老人迎面走来。

> "把我的葫芦还给我！"老人说。

  悟空走上前。

> "什么葫芦？我哪有葫芦。"

> "我知道你拿了我的葫芦！"老人说。

> "我不知道你在说什么，"悟空说。

  突然一道强光闪过，太上老仙出现在旅行者面前，表情十分愤怒。

> "悟空，还我的葫芦！"老仙大声喝道。

## ④

  悟空笑了。

> "哈哈！太上老仙！我就是开个玩笑嘛。我早知道是您。葫芦给您。"

  悟空从袍子里掏出葫芦递给了老仙。

> "那个妖怪怎么会有您的葫芦呢？"悟空问。

> "金角大王是我的人，"太上老仙说，"是我派他来故意刁难你们的。我要看看你们是不是真心诚意要去西天取经。悟空，你打得不错。"

  悟空挠了挠头。

> "金角大王恐怕已经化成液体了吧。"

> "不会的，"太上老仙说，"那葫芦伤不了他。后会有期。"

  仙人消失了。一行人继续向西行。

## ⑤

  天黑的时候，他们来到一座寺院。和寺里的和尚简单吃过晚饭之后，大家都去睡了。

  唐僧快睡着的时候，忽然听到外面有呻吟声。他坐起身来，害怕得发抖。

  门吱呀一声慢慢地开了。一个浑身滴水的男人站在门口，他看着唐僧伸出了双手。

> "求求您，救救我吧。"
