# Journey to the West · Chapter Forty-Four: The King's Ghost

## ①

  The man walked into the Tang monk's room.

> "Who are you?" asked the Tang monk nervously.

> "I'm the King of the Black Rooster Kingdom," said the man. "My kingdom suffered a horrible drought five years ago. Crops wouldn't grow while my people were starving. One day a Taoist arrived in the kingdom. He said he had great powers and could pray for rain."

> "Did the Taoist's prayers bring rain?" asked the Tang monk.

  The king nodded.

> "It rained for days and our crops began to grow."

> "Wonderful!" said the Tang monk with a smile. But then his smile faded. "I'm confused. Why do you need my help?"

## ②

  The king took a deep breath.

> "After the Taoist's prayers brought the rain, we became close friends. One day we were walking when I noticed a strange light coming from my well. I went to the well and looked in. Suddenly I felt someone push me in. It was the Taoist. He had made himself look like me with magic so he could trick me. I fell deep into the water and he sealed the well."

  Tears came to the king's eyes.

> "My body is still down there. I'm just a ghost."

> "That's horrible!" said the Tang monk. "Your guards punished the Taoist, right?"

  The king shook his head.

> "The Taoist made himself look like me and now everyone thinks he's the real king. I've been in the land of the dead ever since. Tonight a spirit brought me to see you. He said you can help me."

## ③

> "How can I help you?" asked the monk. "I'm just a monk."

> "Yes, but you're traveling with Sun Wukong!" said the king. "Sun Wukong is very powerful. He can destroy the Taoist!"

  The Tang monk hesitated.

> "Wukong can't charge into the palace and attack the Taoist. People will think the monk is evil, attacking a king for no reason."

> "My son, the prince, can bring all of you into the palace," said the king. "Then Sun Wukong can fight the Taoist in private."

> "Where can we find the prince?" asked the monk.

> "He will be out hunting tomorrow," said the king. "Tell him everything I told you."

> "But he won't believe us," said the monk. "He thinks the Taoist is his father."

> "Show him this." The king held up a jade coin. "This is the one royal treasure I still have. When my son sees it, he'll know you're telling the truth."

## ④

  Suddenly the king looked at the hour.

> "The spirit is calling me. Let us go!"

  The king vanished. The Tang monk called to his companions, who rushed into the room. The monk told them everything that the king had said.

> "He showed me a jade coin," said the monk. He looked around. "I don't know where it is now, though. It was just a dream."

> "No, it wasn't a dream," said the monk, still looking around.

  He went outside and the others followed.

> "There it is!"

  A jade coin lay on the ground by the door.

## ⑤

  The next morning the prince went out hunting. He saw a white rabbit and shot an arrow. He missed and the rabbit ran. The prince chased the rabbit as it ran into a monastery.

  Inside the prince saw a monk, a pig, and a bearded spirit. Then the rabbit turned into a monkey.

> "What's going on here?" cried the prince.

  The monk stepped forward.

> "Don't be scared, Prince. We must tell you something."

# 🇨🇳 第四十四章：国王的鬼魂


## ①

  一个男人走进了唐僧的房间。

> "你是谁？"唐僧紧张地问。

> "我是乌鸡国的国王，"那男人说，"五年前，我的国家遭受了一场可怕的旱灾，庄稼长不出来，百姓们饿着肚子。有一天，一位道士来到了我国。他说自己法力高强，能祈来雨水。"

> "那道士祈来雨了吗？"唐僧问。

  国王点了点头。

> "连下了好几天雨，庄稼又开始生长了。"

> "太好了！"唐僧笑着说。但随后他的笑容消失了，"我不明白，您需要我帮什么忙？"

## ②

  国王深吸一口气。

> "那场雨下过之后，我们成了好朋友。有一天，我们一起散步，我发现井里闪出奇怪的光。我走到井旁往里看，突然有人推了我一把——正是那个道士。他用魔法变成了我的模样来骗我。我掉进了井水深处，他把井封住了。"

  国王眼眶里涌出了泪水。

> "我的身体还在井底，我现在只是一个鬼魂。"

> "太可怕了！"唐僧说，"您的卫兵一定惩罚了那个道士吧？"

  国王摇了摇头。

> "道士变成我的模样，如今所有的人都以为他是真正的国王。我一直困在阴间。今晚是一个鬼魂带我来看你的，他说你能帮上忙。"

## ③

> "可是我能帮什么忙呢？"唐僧说，"我不过一个出家人。"

> "但你身边有孙悟空啊！"国王说，"孙悟空法力高强，他能打败那个道士！"

  唐僧犹豫了。

> "悟空不能冲进王宫去打道士。否则百姓会以为我们和尚是坏人，无缘无故攻击国王。"

> "我儿子，王子殿下，可以把你们都带进王宫，"国王说，"到时候私下让孙悟空去对付那个道士。"

> "我们到哪里去找王子呢？"唐僧问。

> "他明天要出去打猎，"国王说，"你们把事情的经过都告诉他。"

> "可他不会相信我们的，"唐僧说，"他以为道士就是他父亲。"

> "把这个给他看。"国王掏出一枚玉币，"这是仅存的一件王室宝物。我儿子看到它，就知道你们说的是真话。"

## ④

  忽然国王看了看时辰。

> "鬼使在召唤我了。我该走了！"

  国王消失了。唐僧呼唤同伴们，他们赶紧跑进了房间。唐僧把国王的话全都告诉了他们。

> "给我看了一枚玉币，"唐僧说。他环顾四周，"不过现在不知道去哪儿了。也许只是个梦吧。"

> "不，不是梦，"唐僧说着，还在四处寻找。

  他走出门外，其他人跟了上去。

> "在那里！"

  一枚玉币躺在门边的地上。

## ⑤

  第二天一早，王子出去打猎。他看到一只白兔，射了一箭，但没有射中，兔子跑了。王子追了过去，兔子跑进了一座寺院。

  王子在寺院里看到一个和尚、一头猪和一个长着胡子的鬼魂。然后兔子变成了一只猴子。

> "这是怎么回事？"王子惊叫道。

  唐僧走上前来。

> "别怕，殿下。我们要告诉您一件事。"
