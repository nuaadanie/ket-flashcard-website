# Journey to the West · Chapter Forty-Six: 1,000 Pills

## ①

  The Tang monk glared at Wukong.

> "Bajie says you can bring the king back to life," said the monk. "Please do so now."

  Wukong shot a look at Bajie, who was trying not to laugh.

> "Master, use the tight headband spell," said the pig. "Don't cry, Wukong."

  Laozi the sage has pills that can make people live forever. Maybe he makes pills that can bring people back to life.

## ②

  Laozi the sage was busy working on a potion. Steam rose from the bubbling cauldron. Suddenly, Wukong burst into the room.

> "Hello, Laozi!"

  Laozi the sage groaned.

> "What are you doing here, Wukong? You're supposed to be helping the Tang monk reach the western paradise."

  Wukong walked around the room, looking at all the bottles and equipment.

> "I'm still helping the Tang monk," he said. "We're helping a king right now, though." He poked a blue bottle filled with liquid.

  Laozi looked up from his potion.

> "Don't touch anything. Just tell me why you're here."

> "I need pills that will bring someone back to life," said Wukong. "Give me one thousand of them."

## ③

  Laozi the sage stopped what he was doing and walked over to Wukong.

> "How dare you tell me to give you one thousand pills!" he said. "Get out of here right now!"

  The monkey laughed.

> "I'm kidding. I don't really need one thousand pills. Just give me one hundred."

  "Not a chance." Laozi squeezed his hands into fists.

> "I'm not giving you any pills. Now get out of here."

  Wukong scratched his head, pretending to think.

> "Now that I think about it, I don't need one hundred pills. Just give me ten."

> "You are rude and disrespectful."

> "You're rude and disrespectful. Get out of here."

> "I really just need one pill," said Wukong. "Give me that."

> "Get out of here!" shouted Laozi.

  Wukong sighed and then left.

## ④

  Laozi went back to his potion and stirred, but then he stopped.

> "That monkey would definitely come back and cause trouble," he said to himself. "I should just give him a pill."

  The sage opened a jar and took out a pill. He went outside and found Wukong waiting for him.

> "Hello again," said the monkey. "I was just trying to decide what sort of trouble I could cause for you."

  Laozi flinched, shoving the pill toward Wukong.

> "Take this and leave."

## ⑤

  Wukong returned to the monastery. Everyone watched as the monkey put the pill in the king's mouth.

  The pill sparkled around the king. And then his eyes popped open. The king sat up and looked at Wukong.

> "You saved me," the king said. "I'm alive again."

  He turned and saw the prince standing nearby.

> "My son."

> "Father," said the prince. He took his father's hands and bowed his head. "I've missed you."

> "It's time for you to return to your palace."

  The prince led everyone to the palace. They walked past guards and went into the throne room. The prince kneeled down before the Taoist.

> "Greetings, son," said the Taoist. "Who are these people?"

  The prince stood up.

> "This is the Tang monk. He is going to the western paradise to get scriptures from Buddha. These three are his companions."

  The Taoist looked at a person standing near them. The person was wearing a hood.

> "Who was that man wearing the hood?"

  The man pulled the hood back and showed his face. The Taoist gasped.

> "The King!"

  Wukong pulled out his iron bar and leapt toward the Taoist. The Taoist snapped his fingers and vanished.

# 🇨🇳 第四十六章：千粒仙丹


## ①

  唐僧瞪着悟空。

> "八戒说你能让国王复活，"唐僧说，"现在就让他活过来吧。"

  悟空瞥了八戒一眼，八戒拼命忍住笑。

> "师父，念紧箍咒，"八戒说。"别耍赖了，悟空。"

  太上老仙有长生不老的仙丹，说不定他也有能让人复活的仙丹。

## ②

  太上老仙正忙着炼药，丹炉里咕嘟咕嘟冒着热气。忽然，悟空闯了进来。

> "你好啊，老仙！"

  太上老仙叹了口气。

> "悟空，你来干什么？你不是应该护送唐僧去西天取经吗？"

  悟空在屋里走来走去，看着所有的瓶子和器具。

> "我还在保唐僧呢，"他说，"不过我们现在正在帮一位国王。"他戳了戳一只装满蓝色液体的瓶子。

  老仙从药炉前抬起头。

> "什么都别碰。快说是来干什么的。"

> "我需要能让人复活的仙丹，"悟空说，"给我一千颗。"

## ③

  太上老仙放下手里的活，走到悟空面前。

> "你竟敢让我给你一千颗仙丹！"他大声喝道，"马上给我滚出去！"

  悟空笑了。

> "开个玩笑嘛。我真不需要一千颗，给我一百颗就行了。"

> "没门儿。"老仙双手攥紧。

> "我一颗都不会给你，现在就滚！"

  悟空挠了挠头，假装想了想。

> "我想了一下，一百颗也多了。给我十颗吧。"

> "你这猴子又粗鲁又放肆。"

> "你才又粗鲁又放肆呢。快让我走。"

> "我真的只要一颗就行了，"悟空说，"给我一颗吧。"

> "滚出去！"老仙大喊道。

  悟空叹了口气，然后走了出去。

## ④

  老仙回去继续搅药，但接着又停了下来。

> "那猴子肯定会回来捣乱，"他自言自语道，"还不如给他一颗。"

  老仙打开一个罐子，取出一颗仙丹。他走到外面，发现悟空正在等他。

> "又见面了，"悟空说，"我正在琢磨能给你惹出什么麻烦来。"

  老仙吓了一跳，赶紧把仙丹塞给悟空。

> "拿着，快走吧。"

## ⑤

  悟空回到寺院。众目睽睽之下，悟空把仙丹放进国王嘴里。

  仙丹在国王体内发出金光，随后他的眼睛睁开了。国王坐起来看着悟空。

> "你救了我，"国王说，"我又活过来了。"

  他转过身，看见王子站在旁边。

> "我的儿子。"

> "父王，"王子说，他握住父亲的双手，低下了头，"我好想您。"

> "是时候回王宫了。"

  王子带着众人回到了王宫。他们穿过守卫，走进大殿。王子向道士跪下。

> "孩儿，拜见父王，"道士说，"这些人是谁？"

  王子站起来。

> "这是唐僧，他要去西天拜佛求经。这三位是他的徒弟。"

  道士看了看旁边站着的一个戴兜帽的人。

> "那个戴兜帽的人是谁？"

  那人掀开兜帽，露出了真面目。道士大惊失色。

> "是国王！"

  悟空抽出铁棒，朝道士扑去。道士打了个响指，凭空消失了。
