# Journey to the West · Chapter Thirty: The Frying Pan

## ①

  Wukong and his companions were tied up in a room in the abbey.

> "I will deal with you all in the morning," said the Great Immortal.

  He turned around and left the room. The Tang monk began to cry.

> "It's bad enough that there are dangers along the journey," he said. "But my own companions are causing trouble for me."

> "Calm down, Master," said Wukong. "We'll get out of this."

> "Master, this is all Wukong's fault," said Bajie. "He stole the ginseng fruit and he knocked down the tree."

> "Feeling the fruit was your idea!" shouted the monkey. "I can't help it if I was hungry," said Bajie.

> "You should have refused to steal it."

> "Let's stop arguing and figure out how to escape," said Bajie.

## ②

> "I have a plan," said Wukong. "But we must wait until the Great Immortal is asleep."

  Late that night, Wukong recited a spell. He shrank to the size of a pea and then slipped out from his ropes. He quickly untied his friends.

> "Let's go," he whispered.

  The group tiptoed through the dark and silent abbey. When they got outside, the sun was beginning to rise.

> "That was easy," said Bajie as they walked down the road.

> "I hope you all learned a lesson from this," said the Tang monk.

## ③

  Suddenly a voice boomed.

> "You can't escape from me!"

  The Great Immortal flew toward them. Wukong pulled out his iron bar and swung. The Great Immortal moved out of the way. Bajie struck with his rake and Wujing stabbed with his spear. But the Great Immortal was fast. He waved his arm and his sleeve swooshed toward the group. Once again, the travelers were swept up.

## ④

  Back at the abbey, the Great Immortal told his priests to start a fire.

> "Bring out a large frying pan," said the Great Immortal. "We're going to fry these criminals. Let's start with Sun Wukong."

  The priests carried an enormous frying pan into the room. They placed it over the fire.

> "Uh-oh," thought Wukong. "I can sit in that frying pan all day and not get hurt at all. But the Tang monk is mortal. He'll be burned to a crisp."

  He spotted a statue of a lion nearby. The monkey recited a quick spell and made the statue look exactly like himself. Then he disappeared.

## ⑤

> "Throw Sun Wukong into the frying pan!" said the Great Immortal.

  Thinking the statue was Wukong, the two priests tried to lift it.

> "This monkey is much heavier than he looks," said one priest, breathing heavily.

> "Yes," said the other priest, panting.

  They finally dragged the statue to the frying pan and dropped it in. *Crash!* The frying pan broke in half.

> "The monkey tricked us!" shouted the Great Immortal. "He made that statue look like himself."

> "That rotten monkey!" cried Bajie. "He escaped without us."

> "Do you really think he left us?" asked the Tang monk.

> "Look around," said Bajie. "He's gone. He probably returned to the Fruit and Flower Mountain. I'm sure he's playing in a field of flowers right now. Meanwhile, we're trapped here."

> "I don't think he would leave us," said Wujing.

## ⑥

  The Tang monk started to cry. The Great Immortal shook with anger.

> "It's bad enough that Sun Wukong wanted to escape. But did he really have to break my frying pan too?"

  He stamped his foot.

> "Bring out another frying pan! We'll fry his companions, starting with the Tang monk!"

# 🇨🇳 第三十章：油锅


## ①

  悟空和同伴们被绑在道观的一间屋子里。

> "明天早上再来收拾你们，"大仙说。

  他转身离开了屋子。唐僧哭了起来。

> "这一路上危险重重也就罢了，"他说，"可我的徒弟们还偏偏给我惹麻烦。"

> "师父别慌，"悟空说，"我们会有办法脱身的。"

> "师父，这全怪悟空，"八戒说，"他偷了人参果，还打倒了树。"

> "要吃果子可是你出的主意！"猴子吼道。

> "我饿了有什么办法，"八戒说。

> "那你本该拒绝偷东西的。"

> "别吵了，先想想怎么逃跑吧，"八戒说。

## ②

> "我有一个计划，"悟空说，"不过得等大仙睡着了再说。"

  深夜里，悟空念了个法诀。他的身体缩成豌豆那么大，从绳子缝里溜了出去。他快速给同伴们松了绑。

> "走吧，"他小声说。

  一行人蹑手蹑脚地穿过安静的道观。到了外面时，天刚蒙蒙亮。

> "挺容易的嘛，"八戒一边走路一边说。

> "但愿你们从这件事上吸取了教训，"唐僧说。

## ③

  突然一个声音轰然响起。

> "你们休想逃掉！"

  大仙朝他们飞了过来。悟空掏出铁棒抡了过去。大仙轻巧地躲开了。八戒挥起钉耙，悟净挺枪便刺。但大仙身手敏捷。他袖子一甩，朝众人卷了过来。旅人们又一次被卷进了袖子里。

## ④

  回到道观，大仙吩咐道士们生火。

> "去拿一口大铁锅来，"大仙说，"我们要把这些犯人下油锅。先从孙悟空开始。"

  道士们抬了一口巨大的铁锅进屋，架在火上。

> "糟糕，"悟空心想，"我坐在锅里一天都不会有事。可是唐僧是凡人，他会被炸成脆皮的。"

  他看到了附近的一头石狮子。猴子念了个法诀，让石狮子变得跟自己一模一样。然后他隐身不见了。

## ⑤

> "把孙悟空扔进油锅！"大仙叫道。

  两个道士以为石狮子是悟空，试着抬起来。

> "这猴子比看上去要重得多，"一个道士喘着粗气说。

> "是啊，"另一个道士也气喘吁吁。

  他们好不容易把石狮子拖到油锅跟前，丢了进去。砰！油锅碎成了两半。

> "这猴子骗了我们！"大仙吼道，"他用法术把石狮子变成自己的模样。"

> "这该死的猴子！"八戒叫道，"他丢下我们跑了。"

> "你真的觉得他丢下了我们？"唐僧问。

> "你瞧瞧四周，"八戒说，"他人都不见了。八成是回了花果山。这会儿正开心地在花丛里玩呢。可我们却被困在了这里。"

> "我觉得他不会丢下我们的，"悟净说。

## ⑥

  唐僧又哭了起来。大仙气得浑身发抖。

> "孙悟空想逃跑就已经够可恶了。可他非要把我的锅也砸烂吗？"

  他跺了跺脚。

> "再拿一口锅来！先炸他的同伴，从唐僧开始！"
