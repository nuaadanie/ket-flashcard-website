# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixty: Snow

## ①

  The demon eyed the smiling boy suspiciously.

> "Are you scared of me?" asked the demon.

> "No," said the boy. "Should I be?"

> "Well, yes," said the demon. "I plan to eat you. Doesn't that sound terrible?"

  The boy shrugged. "Not really."

  The demon hesitated for a moment and then moved closer. Suddenly the boy changed into Wukong. The monkey pulled out his iron bar and swung. The demon jumped back and Wukong missed.

## ②

> "Who are you?" demanded the demon.

> "I am Sun Wukong," said the monkey. "And you should be ashamed of yourself. What kind of monster eats children?"

  "Sun Wukong," said the demon. "I thought you were trapped under a mountain. What are you doing here?"

> "My punishment is over," said the monkey. "I'm traveling to the Western Paradise with the Tang Monk."

  The demon hesitated a little, then turned. Wukong lunged. The demon spun around and disappeared.

## ③

  The monkey returned to Chen's home and told everyone what had happened.

> "Because of you, I still have my son," said Chen. "Thank you."

> "You're welcome," said Wukong. "But I believe the demon now wants to capture the Tang Monk. So in the morning we must find a boat and leave."

  Meanwhile the demon was deep under the water.

  "I must capture the Tang Monk and eat him," he said to himself. "It won't be easy with that monkey around. I'll need some magic."

## ④

  Late that night, a cold wind blew. A snowflake drifted to the ground. Then another one fell, and another.

  Bajie couldn't sleep.

  "It's freezing in here," he said to himself. He looked at Wukong and Wujing. Both of them were sleeping soundly.

  "Those two look very comfortable. That blanket must be warmer than mine."

## ⑤

  In the morning, Wukong woke up shivering.

  "That was terrible. I'm so cold," he said to himself. He looked down at his body. "No wonder I was cold. What happened to my blanket?"

  Wujing woke up. "Why is it so cold? And where did my blanket go?"

  They looked at Bajie. The pig was tucked cozy under three blankets. Even his head was covered.

  Wukong gave him a kick. "Wake up, pig!"

## ⑥

  Bajie rolled over, giggling under the blanket.

  Wujing frowned.

  Wukong scratched his head. "Why is it so cold anyway? It's spring!" He looked out the window and gasped. "It snowed last night."

  They woke the Tang Monk, Chen, and everyone went outside. A thick blanket of snow covered the entire village.

> "It would be dangerous to cross the river in this weather," said Chen. "You should stay here until the snow melts."

## ⑦

  A villager ran over. "The river is frozen solid," he said.

  "That's wonderful news," the Tang Monk smiled. "Maybe we can walk across it."

  The travelers thanked Chen and said goodbye. Soon they were standing at the edge of the frozen river.

> "I wonder if it's safe to walk on," said Wujing.

  "I'll test it with my rake," said Bajie.

  The pig walked onto the ice and raised his rake. *Bam!* He brought the rake down hard, but the ice didn't crack.

> "It's solid," he said.

## ⑧

  The travelers stepped onto the ice and started walking. After a few hours, the river bank was no longer in sight.

  Suddenly there was a rumbling sound. And the ice shook.

> "What caused that?" asked the monk.

> "I don't know," said Wujing. "But we should be—"

  *Crack.*

# 🇨🇳 第六十章：大雪


## ①

  妖怪狐疑地打量着这个微笑的男孩。

> "你怕我吗？"妖怪问。

> "不怕，"男孩说。"我应该怕吗？"

> "当然应该，"妖怪说。"我打算吃了你。听着不可怕吗？"

  男孩耸了耸肩。"没什么可怕的。"

  妖怪犹豫了一下，然后凑近了些。突然男孩变成了悟空。悟空掏出金箍棒就抡了过去。妖怪跳开了，悟空扑了个空。

## ②

> "你是谁？"妖怪问道。

> "我是孙悟空，"悟空说。"你真该感到羞耻。什么妖怪连小孩子都吃？"

  "孙悟空，"妖怪说。"我以为你被压在山下呢。你来这里干什么？"

> "我的惩罚已经结束了，"悟空说。"我和唐僧正在去西天的路上。"

  妖怪犹豫了一下，然后转身要跑。悟空扑了过去。妖怪转了个身就不见了。

## ③

  悟空回到陈家，把经过告诉了大家。

> "多亏了你，我还留住了儿子，"陈澄说。"谢谢你。"

> "不客气，"悟空说。"但我相信那妖怪现在想来抓唐僧。所以明天一早我们得找条船赶紧离开。"

  与此同时，妖怪潜到了水底深处。

  "我必须抓住唐僧吃了他，"他自言自语。"有那只猴子在可不容易。我得用些法术。"

## ④

  深夜，一阵寒风吹来。一片雪花飘落到地上。然后又落了一片，又一片。

  八戒睡不着。

  "太冷了，"他自言自语。他看了看悟空和悟净，两人都睡得很香。

  "这两人看起来真舒服。他们的毯子一定比我的暖和。"

## ⑤

  第二天早上，悟空冻得发抖，醒了过来。

  "太冷了，"他自言自语。他低头看了看自己。"难怪我冷。我的毯子哪去了？"

  悟净也醒了。"怎么这么冷？我的毯子呢？"

  他们看了看八戒。这猪裹着三条毯子，舒舒服服地躺着，连头都盖住了。

  悟空踢了他一脚。"醒醒，猪八戒！"

## ⑥

  八戒翻了个身，在毯子底下偷笑。

  悟净皱了皱眉。

  悟空挠了挠头。"怎么这么冷？明明是春天！"他朝窗外一看，倒吸一口气。"昨天下雪了。"

  他们叫醒唐僧、陈澄和所有人。大家走到外面。厚厚的积雪覆盖了整个村庄。

> "这种天气过河太危险了，"陈澄说。"你们应该等雪化了再走。"

## ⑦

  一个村民跑了过来。"河已经冻得结结实实了，"他说。

  "太好了，"唐僧笑了。"也许我们可以走过去。"

  一行人谢过陈澄，告别出发。他们很快站在结冰的河边。

> "不知道走上去安不安全，"悟净说。

  "我用钉耙试试，"八戒说。

  八戒走上冰面，举起钉耙。"嘭！"他使劲砸了下去，冰面纹丝不动。

> "冰很结实，"他说。

## ⑧

  一行人踏上冰面开始行走。几个小时后，河岸已经从视野中消失了。

  突然传来一阵轰隆声，冰面晃动起来。

> "那是什么声音？"唐僧问。

> "不知道，"悟净说。"但我们应该——"

  *咔嚓。*
