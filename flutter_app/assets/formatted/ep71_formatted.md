# Journey to the West · Chapter Seventy-One: The Unexpected Danger

## ①

> "I'm hungry," Bajie moaned. His stomach rumbled loudly. "Can we stop walking?"

> "We'll stop for lunch soon," said the Tang monk. "The weather is nice and the road is smooth. I want to keep moving. I'm eager to reach the Western Paradise."

  Wukong laughed.

> "Master, I don't know why you're still going there. You should have married that scorpion spirit instead."

## ②

  The Tang monk frowned.

> "That's not funny, Wukong."

> "I agree with Wukong," said Bajie. "I wouldn't mind marrying her."

  Wukong raised an eyebrow.

> "I doubt she would have married you."

> "Why not?" Bajie puffed out his chest. "I'm very handsome."

  Everybody laughed, including Bajie.

## ③

  They had continued for another hour when the pig complained again.

> "My legs are tired," he said.

> "We'll stop at the next village," said the Tang monk.

  Bajie sighed.

> "I hope we reach the next village soon."

> "I know how we can get there soon," said Wukong with a laugh. The monkey smacked the horse with his stick, and the horse charged forward.

## ④

> "Wukong!" cried the Tang monk, hanging on tightly.

  When the horse finally stopped running, the monk was far ahead of his companions. A group of nasty-looking men stood in front of him.

> "Give us all your money!" said a man with an axe.

  The Tang monk shook with fear. Wukong was already running over.

## ⑤

> "Don't you dare harm my master!" shouted the monkey.

> "Oh, you!" said a man. "Who are you?"

> "My name is Sun Wukong. Give us all your money."

> "I don't have much money," said the monkey. "You'd better give yours to me."

  The man's face turned red.

## ⑥

> "I'm not going to give you money!"

> "Let's make a bet then," said Wukong. He pulled out his iron bar, which quickly grew to the height of a person. The men looked nervous. The monkey stuck the bar into the ground. "If you can pick this up, we'll give you all of our money."

## ⑦

> "Careful," said the man with an axe. "I'm sure I can pick that bar up."

> "Go ahead and try," said Wukong.

  The man put down his axe. He grasped the bar with his hands and tried to lift it.

> "It's heavier than it looks," said the man. He tried to lift the bar again. Sweat poured down his red face. But the bar didn't budge.

## ⑧

> "Now it's my turn," said Wukong. He lifted the bar over his head and twirled it around. His eyes flashed with anger. He got ready to strike the man.

> "Wukong, don't!" shouted the Tang monk.

  The man took a step back. Suddenly the bar dropped from Wukong's hands.

## ⑨

  Crying out, the monkey held his head. He fell to his knees as the Tang monk quietly recited the tight headband spell. The robbers glanced at one another and then ran away.

  When the monk stopped reciting the spell, Wukong was furious.

> "Why did you do that?" he cried. "They were going to rob you!"

## ⑩

> "You must not hurt people," commanded the monk.

> "I destroy demons all the time," said the monkey.

> "It's fine to hurt demons. But you must not hurt people," the monk said. "You're still evil, Wukong. I can't bring you to the Western Paradise. You must leave."

> "But you need me, Master!" Wukong insisted.

## ⑪

> "I refuse. Please go," said the Tang monk. "Or I'll recite the tight headband spell until your head explodes."

  Wukong could see that the monk wasn't going to change his mind. With tears in his eyes, the monkey leaped into the air and was gone.

# 🇨🇳 第七十一章：意外之险

## ①

> "我饿了，"八戒嚷嚷着，肚子咕咕直叫，"我们能歇会儿吗？"

> "很快就停下来吃午饭，"唐僧说，"天气这么好，道路也平坦，我想继续赶路。我急着要到西天去。"

  悟空听了说：

> "师父，我真不明白你为什么还非要去西天。你当初还不如娶了那个蝎子精呢。"

## ②

  唐僧皱起眉头。

> "悟空，这一点也不好笑。"

> "我同意悟空的话，"八戒说，"我倒是很乐意娶她呢。"

  悟空挑了挑眉毛。

> "恐怕人家还未必看得上你呢。"

> "为什么？"八戒挺起胸膛，"我可英俊得很。"

  大家都笑了，八戒也跟着笑了起来。

## ③

  他们又走了一个时辰，八戒又抱怨起来。

> "我的腿好酸。"

> "我们在下一个村子歇脚。"唐僧说。

  八戒叹了口气。

> "但愿快点到下一个村子。"

> "我知道怎么才能快点到，"悟空笑着说，他拍了白龙马一巴掌，马猛然向前飞奔。

## ④

> "悟空！"唐僧惊叫着死死抓住缰绳。

  等马终于停下来时，唐僧已经远远走到了同伴们的前面。一群凶神恶煞的汉子挡在他面前。

> "把身上的钱都交出来！"一个拿斧头的人说。

  唐僧吓得浑身发抖。悟空已经飞奔过来。

## ⑤

> "你们竟敢伤害我师父！"悟空大喊道。

> "哼，你又是谁？"一个人说。

> "我叫孙悟空。把你们的钱都交出来。"

> "我没多少钱，"悟空说，"你们最好把你们的钱给我。"

  那人涨红了脸。

## ⑥

> "我才不会给你钱！"

> "那我们来打个赌，"悟空说。他掏出金箍棒，迎风一晃，变得有一人高。那些汉子面露惧色。悟空将金箍棒立在地上："如果你们谁能举起这根棒子，我们就把所有钱给你们。"

## ⑦

> "小心，"拿斧头的人说，"我肯定能举起这根棒。"

> "那就来试试吧。"悟空说。

  那人放下斧头，双手抓住金箍棒使劲往上抬。

> "比看起来重多了，"那人说，他又试着抬了一次，汗水从涨红的脸上淌下来，可金箍棒纹丝不动。

## ⑧

> "现在轮到我了，"悟空说着把金箍棒举过头顶，舞了起来，眼中闪出怒火，准备打那人。

> "悟空，住手！"唐僧大喊道。

  那人连连后退。忽然悟空手一松，金箍棒从手中掉落。

## ⑨

  他双手抱头，扑通跪倒在地——原来唐僧正在默念紧箍咒。那些强盗互相看了一眼，转身逃跑了。

  唐僧停下咒语，悟空愤怒地质问。

> "你为什么要这样做？他们可是要抢你啊！"

## ⑩

> "你绝不能伤害人。"唐僧命令道。

> "我打的都是妖怪。"悟空说。

> "打妖怪可以，但你绝不能伤害人，"唐僧说，"悟空，你还是太凶恶了。我不能带你去西天了，你必须离开。"

> "可是师父，你需要我呀！"悟空坚持道。

## ⑪

> "不行，走吧，"唐僧说，"不然我就一直念紧箍咒，念到你脑袋炸开为止。"

  悟空看出唐僧心意已决，眼中含泪，纵身一跃，消失在空中。
