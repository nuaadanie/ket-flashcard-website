# Journey to the West · Chapter Thirty-Six: Tricked Again

## ①

> "Why won't Wukong help us?" the horse asked Bajie.

  The pig sighed.

> "He told us not to look for him if we needed help. Plus, I kept telling Master to use the Tight Headband Spell. The monkey is really mad at me."

> "That doesn't matter now," said the horse. "You must get Wukong to come back."

  Bajie hesitated for a moment, and then leaped onto a cloud. He soared through the sky and soon arrived at the Fruit and Flower Mountain.

## ②

  He found Wukong sitting beside a big waterfall. Other monkeys and apes were nearby. They held swords and spears. They stared at Bajie silently.

> "Hello, Wukong," said the pig. "This is a lovely mountain."

  Wukong scowled.

> "What are you doing here, Bajie?"

  The pig looked at the ground.

> "The Tang Monk would like you to come back," he mumbled.

## ③

> "Well, that's too bad," said Wukong, standing up. "He told me to leave. I'll never help him again."

> "Please come back," said Bajie. "We all miss you."

> "I don't miss you," said Wukong. "You told Master to use the Tight Headband Spell. I should take out my iron bar and clobber you."

  Bajie forced a laugh.

> "I was just joking, Wukong. I knew you were really just protecting Master."

> "It wasn't funny," said Wukong. "That spell really hurts."

## ④

  The pig began to cry.

> "Please come back. The Tang Monk is in danger. Big danger. A monster turned him into a tiger. And Wu Jing was captured."

  Wukong shrugged.

> "Just tell the monster that you're friends with me, the Monkey King. He'll be scared and leave all of you alone."

> "Oh, I did tell him about you," Bajie lied.

> "Really?" Wukong leaned closer. "What did he say?"

  Bajie bit his lip.

> "He laughed and said that you're a tiny little coward."

## ⑤

  Wukong's eyes blazed with fury.

> "He will regret saying that. Let's go, Bajie!"

  The monster sat in the dining hall, still disguised as a handsome young man. He was eating a meal with the king and the queen. A servant came in and handed a note to the young man.

> "I'm sorry, Your Majesty, but I must go."

  He ran out of the dining hall. From behind a pillar, the servant watched the man leave. Light sparkled, and the servant turned into Bajie. The pig giggled.

> "The monster is going to be so surprised."

## ⑥

  Meanwhile, the young man ran outside and turned back into a monster. He flew through the air and returned to his pagoda. He burst through the door in a panic. Then he saw Princess Hundred Flowers and relaxed.

> "Oh, my sweet!" he cried. "I was so scared. I thought I'd lost you forever."

> "Why did you think that?" asked the princess.

  The monster sighed.

> "I got a note. It said there was a monkey here and he was trying to destroy my pagoda."

## ⑦

> "Oh, the monkey was here earlier," said the princess.

  The monster looked around.

> "He didn't destroy the pagoda. What did he want?"

  The princess hesitated.

> "You are not going to be happy about this. But he freed the Purple Spirit you had captured."

  The monster looked angry.

> "How dare he!"

> "The monkey did something else too," said the princess.

> "What?" asked the monster.

## ⑧

  There was a flash of light. Suddenly, Wukong stood before the monster. The monkey smiled.

> "I freed the princess too."

# 🇨🇳 第三十六章：再遭欺骗


## ①

> "悟空为什么不帮我们呢？"马问八戒。

  八戒叹了口气。

> "他说过出了事别去找他。再说呢，我一直怂恿师父念紧箍咒。猴子可被我气坏了。"

> "现在顾不了那么多了，"马说，"你必须去把悟空请回来。"

  八戒犹豫了一会儿，随即纵身跃上云端。他飞越天空，很快就到了花果山。

## ②

  他发现悟空正坐在一道大瀑布旁边。周围还有许多猴子和猿猴。他们拿着刀枪棍棒，默默地盯着八戒。

> "你好啊，悟空，"八戒说，"这座山真漂亮。"

  悟空板起了脸。

> "你来干什么，八戒？"

  八戒低下了头。

> "师父想请你回去，"他小声嘟囔。

## ③

> "哼，那可不行，"悟空站起来说，"他叫我走人的。我再也不会帮他的忙了。"

> "求你回去吧，"八戒说，"我们都很想你。"

> "我才不想你们，"悟空说，"你让师父念紧箍咒整我。我真该掏出铁棒狠狠敲你一顿。"

  八戒勉强挤出一个笑容。

> "我开玩笑的啦，悟空。我知道你其实是在保护师父。"

> "这一点也不好笑，"悟空说，"那紧箍咒真的很疼的。"

## ④

  八戒哭了起来。

> "求你回去吧。师父有危险，是大难。一个妖怪把他变成了老虎。悟净也被抓了。"

  悟空耸了耸肩。

> "你就跟妖怪说你是我的朋友——齐天大圣。他吓都吓死了，还怎么对付你们？"

> "哦，我跟他说起过你，"八戒撒了个谎。

> "真的？"悟空凑过来，"他怎么说？"

  八戒咬了咬嘴唇。

> "他哈哈大笑，说你是只胆小如鼠的小猴子。"

## ⑤

  悟空眼中怒火熊熊燃烧。

> "他会后悔说这话的。走吧，八戒！"

  此时，妖怪正坐在宴会厅里，仍旧伪装成英俊小伙子的模样。他正和国王、王后一起用餐。一个仆人走进来递给年轻人一封信。

> "抱歉，陛下，我有事得先走了。"

  他跑出了宴会厅。柱子后面，刚才的那个"仆人"目送他离去，只见光芒一闪，仆人变回了八戒。八戒暗暗窃喜。

> "妖怪看到我要大吃一惊了。"

## ⑥

  另一边，年轻人跑出宫门，变回了妖怪的模样。他腾空而起，飞回了自己的塔。他冲进门，一脸慌张，但看到百花公主后松了一口气。

> "哦，我的心肝！"他叫道，"吓死我了。我还以为再也见不到你了。"

> "你怎么会这样想？"公主问道。

  妖怪叹了口气。

> "我收到一封信，说有一只猴子在这里，企图毁坏我的塔。"

## ⑦

> "哦，那只猴子确实来过，"公主说。

  妖怪环顾四周。

> "可塔没有被毁呀。他来干什么？"

  公主犹豫了一下。

> "你听了不会高兴的。他把你抓来的紫阳真人放走了。"

  妖怪顿时大怒。

> "他怎敢如此！"

> "猴子还干了另外一件事呢，"公主说。

> "什么？"妖怪问道。

## ⑧

  只听见一声闪光，悟空突然出现在妖怪面前。猴子微微一笑。

> "我还把公主也救走了。"
