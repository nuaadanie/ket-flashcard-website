# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixty-Two: The Goldfish in the Basket

## ①

  Wukong landed on Mount Potalaka. A young man was meditating in the garden.

> "Where's Guanyin?" asked the monkey.

> "She's making a basket," said the man. He glanced at the monkey. "It's good to see you again, Wukong."

  Wukong was puzzled. "How do you know my name? Don't you recognize me?"

  Wukong stared for a moment and then gasped.

> "You're Red Boy! You look like a kind spirit now."

> "Yes," said Red Boy. "I'm not an evil demon anymore. And I have you to thank for that. You brought Guanyin into my life. She is teaching me all about Buddhism and now I'm very happy."

> "You're welcome," said Wukong. "You and I have a lot in common. We were both evil, causing all sorts of problems. Now we're both Buddhists."

## ②

  Guanyin came over holding a basket.

> "Hello, Wukong," said the Bodhisattva. "I knew you would come here today. I've been working on this basket all morning."

  A look of confusion spread across the monkey's face. "That's a nice basket, Bodhisattva. But what is it for?"

> "This basket has magical powers," said Guanyin. "I will catch the demon with it. Let's go."

## ③

  Wukong returned to the river with Guanyin. The Bodhisattva used a rope to lower the basket into the river. She recited a spell and then waited. After a moment she pulled the basket out of the water. Wukong looked in the basket. A small goldfish was flopping around in it.

  The monkey laughed. "I guess you need to try again, Bodhisattva. That's not the demon."

> "This is my goldfish," said Guanyin. "It usually lives in my lotus pond. The pond is near my garden where I teach my students. After years of listening to my lessons, the fish became a spirit. It escaped to Earth a few days ago. I had been wondering where it was."

> "A few days ago," repeated Wukong. "That means it's been on Earth for a few years."

> "Correct," said Guanyin. "The Tang Monk is safe now. You may go free him and continue your journey to the Western Paradise."

## ④

  Guanyin flew off and Wukong swam down to the palace. Now that the goldfish was gone, so was its magic. The monkey smashed the rocks with his iron bar and went inside.

> "Master, I'm here!" he cried.

  He got the monk out of the box and carried him out of the river.

  By now all the villagers had heard that Wukong had rescued Chen's son. When the travelers returned, the villagers cheered.

> "Please stay in our village!" said a man.

> "Yes," said an old woman. "Stay for as long as you like!"

> "Thank you," said the Tang Monk. "But we can stay only one more night to rest. In the morning we must leave. We still have far to travel."

## ⑤

  The villagers held a feast for the travelers that evening. By the time everyone went to sleep, it was late.

  The next morning the travelers woke to the sound of chopping.

> "What's going on?" asked the Tang Monk.

  They went to the river and found a very busy scene. Several men were chopping down trees. Other people carried tools.

> "We're building a boat for you!" said a smiling man.

> "Thank you," said the monk.

  He and his companions sat down by the water to wait. Suddenly there was a rumble and waves splashed onto the shore. Everyone stared in fear as a large creature rose from the waves.

# 🇨🇳 第六十二章：篮中金鱼


## ①

  悟空来到了普陀山。一个年轻人正在花园里打坐。

> "观音菩萨在哪？"悟空问。

> "她在编竹篮，"年轻人说。他看了看悟空。"悟空，又见到你真好。"

  悟空很困惑。"你怎么知道我的名字？你不认识我了吗？"

  悟空仔细看了看，随即吃了一惊。

> "你是红孩儿！你现在看起来像个善神了。"

> "是的，"红孩儿说。"我不再是妖怪了。这得感谢你。是你把观音请来的。她教我佛法，我现在很快乐。"

> "不客气，"悟空说。"你和我有很多相似之处。我们以前都是恶的，惹了不少麻烦。现在我们都成了佛门弟子。"

## ②

  观音拿着一个竹篮走了过来。

> "你好，悟空，"菩萨说。"我就知道你今天会来。我整个上午都在编这个篮子。"

  悟空一脸困惑。"篮子挺好看，菩萨。但它是用来做什么的？"

> "这个竹篮有法力，"观音说。"我要用它来收服妖怪。走吧。"

## ③

  悟空和观音回到了河边。菩萨用绳子把竹篮放进了河里。她念了一段咒语，然后等着。过了片刻，她把竹篮拉出水面。悟空朝篮子里一看，一条小金鱼在里面扑腾。

  悟空笑了。"菩萨，我想你得重新试试。那可不是妖怪。"

> "这是我的金鱼，"观音说。"它平常住在我莲花池里。池子在我花园旁边，我在那教书。多年来它听我讲课，修成了精。几天前它逃到了人间。我还一直找不到它呢。"

> "几天前？"悟空重复道。"那它在人间已经有好几年了。"

> "没错，"观音说。"现在唐僧安全了。你们可以去救他出来，继续西行取经。"

## ④

  观音飞走了，悟空游到水底的宫殿。金鱼一走，魔法也消失了。悟空一棒砸碎了堵住门的石头，走了进去。

> "师父，我来救你了！"他喊道。

  他把唐僧从石匣里救了出来，扛着他游出了河。

  此时全村人都听说悟空救了陈家的儿子。一行人回到村里时，村民们欢呼起来。

> "请留在我们村里吧！"一个男人说。

> "对呀，"一位老奶奶说。"想待多久就待多久！"

> "谢谢你们，"唐僧说。"但我们只能再住一晚休息。明天一早必须出发，前路还很远。"

## ⑤

  当天晚上村民们为旅行者举办了宴席。等所有人都去睡觉时，已经很晚了。

  第二天早上，旅行者们被叮叮当当的声音吵醒了。

> "发生什么事了？"唐僧问。

  他们走到河边，发现了一幅忙碌的景象。几个男人在砍树，其他人在搬运工具。

> "我们在给你们造船呢！"一个笑着的男人说。

> "谢谢你们，"唐僧说。

  他和同伴们在水边坐下来等着。突然一阵轰隆声响起，波浪拍到了岸上。一个巨大的生物从水中升起，大家恐惧地盯着它看。
