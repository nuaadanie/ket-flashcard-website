# Journey to the West · Chapter Fifty-Six: The Tiger Immortal

## ①

  Wukong led the monks to his companions. The monks told the Tang Monk everything that they had told Wukong.

> "That's terrible," said the Tang Monk. "We must help you."

> "Yes!" the monkey agreed. "I'll deal with the Tiger Immortal in the morning."

  The monks knew of an empty building nearby. They led the travelers there and everyone went to sleep for the night.

## ②

  The next morning, Wukong woke up early. Someone was pounding on the door of the building. A voice boomed.

> "I am the Tiger Immortal!"

  The Tang Monk's eyes widened with fear. He and all the other monks trembled.

  Wukong, Wujing, and Bajie drew their weapons.

> "No worry, everyone," said Wukong with a smile. "I'll deal with this villain."

  The monkey went outside.

## ③

> "Who are you?" demanded the Tiger Immortal.

> "I am Sun Wukong," said the monkey. "And you should be ashamed of yourself. Just because you're a little powerful, you think you can treat the Buddhist monks badly."

> "A little powerful?" The Tiger Immortal looked offended. "I am very powerful."

  Wukong laughed.

> "You're not as powerful as I am. Did you know that the Jade Emperor is afraid of me? I defeated his entire army."

## ④

  The Tiger Immortal puffed out his chest.

> "I can summon rain," he said. "I doubt you can do that."

  Wukong snored.

> "I can summon rain. It's easy."

  "Well then, let's have a contest," said Wukong. "Whoever summons more rain is the winner."

  "If you win," said the Tiger Immortal, "I'll let you and all the monks go free. But if I win, you must stay here and be my slaves."

> "You're on," said Wukong. "I accept your challenge."

## ⑤

  The Tiger Immortal led everyone into town. In the center of town was a Daoist altar. A stone tablet, incense sticks, and papers with magic writing were on the altar. A crowd of people arrived to see what was going on.

> "I will go first," announced the Tiger Immortal.

  He walked toward the altar.

## ⑥

  The Tang Monk shook his head.

> "This is terrible," he muttered.

  Bajie glanced at him.

> "Don't worry, Master. We'll win this contest."

> "But what if he doesn't win?" asked the monk. "We'll all be stuck here, forced to work for the Tiger Immortal. If that happens, we'll never reach the Western Paradise. The true scriptures will never get to the Tang Empire."

  The monk buried his face in his hands.

> "Bajie is right, Master," said Wujing. "We have nothing to worry about."

  Wukong stood by them smiling. From the altar, the Tiger Immortal called out.

> "I will now pray to Heaven for rain!"

## ⑦

  The Tiger Immortal lit the incense and then sat down. He closed his eyes and began to recite a spell. Next, he lit one of the papers on fire. When that one went out, he lit another paper. Soon all of the papers were gone.

  The Tiger Immortal lifted the stone tablet and then slammed it down. There was a gasp from the crowd.

  Wukong looked up at the sky, which was becoming dark. Wind blew and dark clouds moved in. Thunder rumbled.

  The Tang Monk trembled.

> "Oh no, it's working. It's going to rain."

## ⑧

  Wukong chuckled.

> "You all stay here. I'll probably be right back."

  The monkey recited a quick spell and then left his body. Up in the sky, he found the Old Woman of the Wind fanning the clouds. The Dragon King of the Eastern Sea was approaching.

> "Stop what you're doing!" Wukong called out. "Don't make any rain."

# 🇨🇳 第五十六章：虎仙


## ①

  悟空带着和尚们来到同伴身边。那些和尚把告诉悟空的事又都对唐僧说了一遍。

> "太残忍了，"唐僧说，"我们一定要帮你们。"

> "没错！"猴子说，"明天一早我就去对付虎力大仙。"

  和尚们知道附近有一处空房子，就带行者们去了那里。大家都睡了一夜。

## ②

  第二天一早，悟空早早地就醒了，有人正在用力拍打房门。一个洪亮的声音传来。

> "我是虎力大仙！"

  唐僧吓得睁大了眼睛，他和其他和尚全都浑身发抖。

  悟空、悟净和八戒都拔出了兵器。

> "大家别担心，"悟空笑着说，"我来对付这个恶人。"

  猴子走到了外面。

## ③

> "你是谁？"虎力大仙问道。

> "我是孙悟空，"猴子说，"你应该为自己感到可耻。就因为你有点法力，就可以这样虐待和尚吗？"

> "有点法力？"虎力大仙面露怒色，"我可是法力高强的！"

  悟空笑了起来。

> "你可没有我厉害。你知道玉皇大帝都怕我吗？我打败过他的全部天兵天将。"

## ④

  虎力大仙挺起胸膛。

> "我能呼风唤雨。"

  悟空哼了一声。

> "我恐怕你未必做得到。"

> "我能呼风唤雨，"虎力大仙说，"这很简单。"

  悟空说："那好，我们来比一比。谁唤来的雨多谁就赢。"

  "你要是赢了，"虎力大仙说，"我就让所有和尚都自由。不过要是我赢了，你们就得留下来做我的奴才。"

  "一言为定，"悟空说，"我接受你的挑战。"

## ⑤

  虎力大仙带着所有人进了城。城中央有一座道教祭坛，祭坛上放着石碑、香和写有符咒的纸。一大群人赶来看热闹。

> "我先来！"虎力大仙宣布道。

  他走向祭坛。

## ⑥

  唐僧摇了摇头。

> "这可怎么办，"他嘟囔道。

  八戒看了看他。

> "师父别担心，我们会赢的。"

> "可他要是输了呢？"一个和尚问，"那我们就全都被困在这里，给虎力大仙当苦力。那样的话，我们就再也到不了西天，经书也永远送不回大唐。"

  和尚把脸埋在手里。

> "八戒说得对，师父，"悟净说，"我们不用担心的。"

  悟空站在一旁微笑着。祭坛上，虎力大仙大声喊道：

> "我现在就向天祈雨！"

## ⑦

  虎力大仙点了香，坐下来闭上眼睛，开始念咒。然后他点燃了一张符纸，烧完之后又点燃另一张。不一会儿，符纸全都烧完了。

  虎力大仙拿起石碑，用力拍了下去。人群中传来一阵惊呼。

  悟空抬头望向天空，天色渐渐暗了下来。风吹过来，乌云聚集。雷声隆隆。

  唐僧吓得发抖。

> "不好了，他真的在唤雨。"

## ⑧

  悟空微微一笑。

> "你们在这里等着，我一会儿就回来。"

  猴子念了一段咒语，灵魂出窍飞上天空。在天上他找到了风婆婆在扇风鼓云。东海龙王正朝这边飞来。

> "停下手来！"悟空大喊道，"一滴雨也不许下！"
