# Journey to the West · Chapter Fifty-Three: The Man in the Canoe

## ①

  From behind Guanyin, Wukong peered at Red Boy. The demon was trapped by the swords, all pointed at his face.

> "Get these swords away from me!" cried the demon.

> "You must become a Buddhist," said Guanyin. "Then I'll release you."

> "I promise!" cried Red Boy. "I'll do whatever you say!"

  Guanyin recited a spell and the swords vanished.

## ②

  Red Boy's eyes flashed with anger.

> "Bodhisattva, watch out!" cried Wukong.

  Red Boy charged toward Guanyin. Wukong jumped in front of him. The monkey was about to strike the demon when Guanyin held up five metal bands. She threw them at Red Boy. Instantly they clamped around his head, wrists, and ankles.

## ③

> "Evil demon!" said Guanyin. "How dare you try to attack me!"

  Unable to move, Red Boy glared at Guanyin. Wukong raised his iron bar to strike him.

> "No, Wukong," said Guanyin. "Don't hurt the demon. I will take him back to Mount Putuo Luojia to be my student."

> "Yes, Bodhisattva," said the monkey.

## ④

  Guanyin took the vase down to Earth. She recited a spell and all the water flowed back into the vase.

  She then led Red Boy towards the south. Wukong went into Red Boy's cave and freed the Tang Monk. Then he found Bajie and freed him. The three of them went down the mountain and found Wujing.

  The group continued west.

## ⑤

  They traveled for weeks and then months. One day they came to a wide river.

> "I can't believe we have to cross another one," muttered Bajie.

> "There are many rivers between here and the west," said Wukong. "We'll have to cross them all."

> "How will we cross this one?" asked the Tang Monk.

  A small canoe came into view. A man paddled it toward the travelers. As the man paddled, he stared at the Tang Monk.

## ⑥

> "Greetings!" called the man as he came closer. "Do you all need a ride across the river?"

> "Yes," said the Tang Monk. "We were just wondering how we would get across. We're going to the west to get scriptures from Buddha."

> "You must be the Tang Monk," said the man brightly. "I've heard you would pass through this area eventually."

> "Really?" asked the Tang Monk.

> "Yes," said the man with a smile. "You're famous!" He glanced down at his canoe. "I would be happy to take you all across. But as you can see, my canoe is not very big. I can take only two passengers at a time."

## ⑦

  As the man talked, Wukong watched him carefully. Something about the man didn't look quite right. The monkey was about to say something when Bajie stepped forward.

> "The Tang Monk and I will go first," said the pig.

  Wukong hesitated.

> "Master, I don't think this is a good idea."

  The Tang Monk frowned.

> "You never trust people, Wukong. This kind man is willing to help us cross the river."

  The monkey bit his lip as the monk and Bajie climbed into the canoe. They sat down and the man paddled away from shore.

## ⑧

  Wukong watched the canoe glide across the river. When it was about halfway across, Wukong noticed something.

> "Am I imagining things or is the canoe sinking?" asked the monkey.

  Wujing looked.

> "You're not imagining things," he said. "It is sinking."

  The two of them watched as the canoe slipped under the surface of the water. Wukong stamped his foot.

> "That man kidnapped Master and Bajie! I knew he looked suspicious."

# 🇨🇳 第五十三章：独木舟上的人


## ①

  悟空从观音身后窥看红孩儿。妖怪被宝剑困住，每一把剑都指向他的面部。

> "快把这些剑拿走！"妖怪哭喊道。

> "你必须皈依佛门，"观音说，"然后我就放了你。"

> "我发誓！"红孩儿大喊道，"我一定听你的话！"

  观音念了一段咒语，宝剑消失了。

## ②

  红孩儿眼中闪现怒火。

> "菩萨小心！"悟空大喊道。

  红孩儿向观音冲去。悟空跳到了他面前。猴子正要打那妖怪时，观音举起了五个金箍，扔向红孩儿。金箍立刻套在了他的头上、手腕上和脚踝上。

## ③

> "恶毒的妖怪！"观音说，"你竟敢袭击我！"

  红孩儿动弹不得，只能瞪着观音。悟空举起金箍棒要打他。

> "别，悟空，"观音说，"别伤着他。我带他回普陀洛迦山做我的弟子。"

> "遵命，菩萨，"猴子说。

## ④

  观音拿着净瓶来到地面。她念了一段咒语，所有的水都流回了瓶中。然后她带着红孩儿向南走去。悟空走进红孩儿的洞，救出了唐僧，接着又找到八戒，也救了他出来。三人下了山，找到了悟净。

  一行人继续向西前行。

## ⑤

  他们走了几个星期，又走了几个月。一天，他们来到一条宽阔的大河前。

> "真不敢相信又要过河，"八戒嘟囔道。

> "从这里到西天，要过的河多着呢，"悟空说，"全都得过。"

> "这条河怎么过啊？"唐僧问。

  一条小独木舟出现在视野中，一个人划着船向行者们驶来，一边划船一边盯着唐僧看。

## ⑥

> "你们好！"那人靠近了喊道，"你们需要渡河吗？"

> "需要，"唐僧说，"我们正发愁怎么过去呢。我们要去西天拜佛求经。"

> "你一定是唐僧吧，"那人高兴地说，"我听说你们早晚会路过这里。"

> "是吗？"唐僧问。

> "是啊，"那人笑着说，"您可有名了！"他低头看了看自己的小船，"我很乐意载你们过去。不过你们也看到了，我的船不大，一次只能带两个人。"

## ⑦

  那人说话的时候，悟空仔细地打量着他，总觉得这人有些地方不太对劲。猴子正要开口，八戒走上前来。

> "我和师父先过去，"八戒说。

  悟空犹豫了一下。

> "师父，我觉得这不太好吧。"

  唐僧皱起了眉头。

> "你总是不信任别人，悟空。这位好心人愿意帮我们过河。"

  猴子咬了咬嘴唇，看着唐僧和八戒上了船。那两人坐下后，那人划船离开了岸边。

## ⑧

  悟空看着小船在河上前行。大约走到河中央的时候，悟空发现了异常。

> "是我眼花了还是船在下沉？"猴子问。

  悟净看了看。

> "你没眼花，"他说，"船确实在下沉。"

  两人眼睁睁看着小船沉入了水底。悟空跺了跺脚。

> "那人绑架了师父和八戒！我就说他可疑！"
