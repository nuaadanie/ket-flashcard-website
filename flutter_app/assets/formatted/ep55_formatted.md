# Journey to the West · Chapter Fifty-Five: The Monks and the Carts

## ①

> "Everybody, wake up!" called Wukong.

  The travelers had camped for a night in the woods. The Tang Monk woke up and stretched. Wujing opened his eyes and yawned. Bajie moaned.

> "It's so early. Let me sleep just a few more hours."

> "Bajie, don't be lazy," said the Tang Monk. "It's time to get up. Please start making breakfast."

  The pig got to his feet.

> "All we do is work," he said. "Cook food, carry bags, walk, walk, walk."

## ②

> "We're on a noble mission," said Wujing. "Because of our difficult journey, the Tang Empire will have the true scriptures. You should be happy to do all this work."

  After a quick breakfast, the group started walking. They went through a valley and over some hills. Later that afternoon, they heard yelling.

> "It sounds like some people are in trouble," said the Tang Monk.

  Wujing nodded.

> "Yes. I wonder what's going on."

> "You all wait here," said Wukong. "I'll go and investigate."

## ③

  The monkey leaped into the air and looked around. He saw a group of Buddhist monks pushing a cart up a steep hill. The cart was loaded with big stones. The monks looked miserable. Their robes were dirty and ripped.

  Two Taoist priests stood nearby, dressed in fine clothes.

> "Work faster!" yelled one of the priests.

  The Buddhists looked frightened. They struggled to move the cart faster.

  *Why are those monks pushing that cart?* thought Wukong. *And why are they scared of the Taoists?*

## ④

  The monkey waited for the Taoists to leave. Then he made himself look like a Buddhist monk.

> "Hello," said Wukong, walking toward the group of monks. "Why are you pushing this cart?"

  The monks stopped pushing the cart and turned toward Wukong. They looked surprised.

> "You must be new here," one monk said. "You should leave before you're discovered."

> "Why?" asked Wukong.

## ⑤

> "Taoists are in charge here," said another monk.

  Wukong shrugged.

> "People from different religions can still get along."

> "Not in this kingdom," said the first monk. "Buddhists are treated horribly here. The Taoists make us do hard work."

> "Why do the Taoists treat you so badly?" asked Wukong.

> "Years ago this kingdom had a drought," said the monk. "Crops died and people starved. Buddhist monks prayed to Buddha for rain, but no rain came."

## ⑥

> "One day a powerful Taoist arrived," said the other monk. "He's called the Tiger Immortal. He recited prayers and right away rain fell. Since then, Taoists have been in charge. They destroyed all the Buddhist monasteries and statues."

> "That's terrible," said Wukong. "Those Taoists can't get away with this."

## ⑦

> "The Taoists won't treat us this way forever," said a third monk. "The spirit of the planet Venus visited us in a dream one night. He said that one day someone would come along to save us."

> "Really?" Wukong raised an eyebrow. "Who is going to save you?"

> "A fierce monkey spirit," said the monk. "His name is Sun Wukong. He is so powerful that Heaven's entire army couldn't defeat him."

## ⑧

  Wukong smiled.

> "I've heard of Sun Wukong. And it's true that he's very powerful. Did Venus tell you what he looks like?"

> "Oh yes."

> "So you recognize him if you see him?" asked Wukong, trying not to laugh.

> "Certainly," said the monk. "Why do you ask?"

  Light sparkled around Wukong. A moment later, he was a monkey again.

> "Sun Wukong!" cried the monks.

  They all fell to their knees and bowed.

# 🇨🇳 第五十五章：推车僧侣


## ①

> "大家快起来！"悟空喊道。

  旅行者们在林子里扎营过了一夜。唐僧醒了过来，伸了个懒腰。悟净睁开眼睛打了个大哈欠。八戒哼哼唧唧的。

> "天还早着呢，让我再睡一会儿。"

> "八戒，别偷懒了，"唐僧说，"该起床了，去做早饭吧。"

  八戒爬起身来。

> "我们整天就知道干活，"他说，"做饭、挑行李、走啊走啊。"

## ②

> "我们肩负着神圣的使命，"悟净说，"正是因为这段艰难的旅程，大唐才能得到真经。你应该乐于做这些事。"

  匆匆吃过早饭后，一行人又上路了。他们穿过山谷，翻过山丘。到了下午晚些时候，他们听到了叫喊声。

> "好像是有人遇到了麻烦，"唐僧说。

  悟净点了点头。

> "是啊，不知道出了什么事。"

> "你们都在这里等着，"悟空说，"我去看看。"

## ③

  猴子腾空而起，四下张望。他看到一群和尚正在推着一辆车上陡峭的山坡，车上装满了大石头，和尚们的样子很凄惨，衣服又脏又破。

  两个道士站在旁边，穿着华丽的衣裳。

> "快一点！"其中一个道士大声喊道。

  和尚们满脸恐惧，艰难地推着车前行。

  *这些和尚为什么要推车？*悟空想道，*他们为什么怕道士？*

## ④

  猴子等到道士走开了，就把自己变成了一个和尚的模样。

> "你们好，"悟空走到一群和尚面前说，"你们为什么推着这辆车？"

  和尚们停下手中的活，转身看着悟空，一脸惊讶。

> "你一定是新来的，"一个和尚说，"快走吧，免得被发现。"

> "为什么？"悟空问。

## ⑤

> "这里是道士说了算，"另一个和尚说。

  悟空耸了耸肩。

> "不同信仰的人还是可以和平相处的嘛。"

> "在这个国家可不行，"第一个和尚说，"和尚们在这里被虐待得惨极了。道士们逼我们做苦力。"

> "道士们为什么对你们这么坏？"悟空问。

> "几年前这里闹过大旱，"和尚说，"庄稼全死了，人们都挨饿。和尚们向佛祖祈雨，可是一滴雨也没下。"

## ⑥

> "后来来了一个法力高强的道士，"另一个和尚说，"他叫虎力大仙。他念了咒，立刻就下雨了。从那以后就是道士说了算。他们把和尚们的寺庙和佛像全都毁掉了。"

> "太可恶了，"悟空说，"那些道士不能这么横行霸道。"

## ⑦

> "道士们不会永远这样对我们了，"第三个和尚说，"太白金星托梦给我们，说总有一天会有人来救我们。"

> "真的？"悟空挑了挑眉毛，"谁来救你们？"

> "一个法力高强的猴精，"和尚说，"他叫孙悟空。他的本事大到天兵天将都打不过他。"

## ⑧

  悟空微微一笑。

> "我听说过孙悟空。他确实很厉害。太白金星跟你们说了他长什么模样吗？"

> "当然说了。"

> "所以你们见到他就认得出来？"悟空强忍着笑问。

> "那当然，"和尚说，"你问这个干什么？"

  金光一闪，悟空恢复了猴子的模样。

> "孙悟空！"和尚们惊呼一声。

  他们纷纷跪倒在地。
