# Journey to the West · Chapter Sixty-Three: A Question for Buddha

## ①

  A steep riverbank lay ahead. The Tang Monk jumped to a stop as the villagers hid behind trees. Wukong, Wujing, and Bajie pulled out their weapons.

  A large shell rose from the water.

> "It's a giant turtle," said Bajie.

## ②

  The turtle bowed its head toward Wukong.

> "A bad demon had taken over my house and the river. Thank you for getting rid of him."

  The turtle then looked at the villagers.

> "You don't need to make a boat. I can take the travelers across the river."

## ③

> "That's wonderful," said the Tang Monk.

> "I'm from the Tang Empire and I know who you are," interrupted the turtle. "You're traveling to the Western Paradise to get scriptures from Buddha. I knew you would cross my river eventually. Climb onto my shell."

  Wukong eyed the turtle suspiciously.

> "How do we know we can trust you?"

## ④

> "Demons all along the way are waiting for my master to pass by," added Bajie. "They all want to eat him."

> "I promise I won't hurt the Tang Monk," said the turtle. "I want to help him reach the Western Paradise."

  After a little more hesitation, the travelers stepped onto the turtle's shell. The turtle began to paddle.

## ⑤

  The villagers came out from behind the trees and waved.

> "Goodbye," called a woman. "Please visit us on your way home," shouted someone else.

> "We will, I promise," called the monk.

  Soon the turtle was far from shore. Bajie fell asleep.

## ⑥

  The turtle glanced back at the Tang Monk.

> "Excuse me, but can you do me a favor?"

> "Of course," said the monk. "What do you need me to do?"

> "I have been a good Buddhist for over a thousand years," said the turtle. "But sadly I'm still just a turtle. Can you ask Buddha when I will be reborn as a human?"

  The Tang Monk nodded.

> "I will ask him."

## ⑦

  A few hours later, the turtle reached the other side of the river. The travelers stepped onto dry land.

> "Thank you," said the Tang Monk.

> "You're welcome," said the turtle. "Please don't forget to ask Buddha about me."

  The turtle disappeared under the water. The Tang Monk climbed onto the horse and the group continued on the road.

## ⑧

  As usual, Bajie soon started complaining.

> "I'm tired of walking already. I wish we could ride that turtle the whole way to the west."

> "Stop complaining," said Wukong. "Be happy that the road is flat here."

  They walked for another couple of hours. The road started to go up a mountain, getting steeper and steeper.

## ⑨

> "The road isn't flat anymore," the pig moaned.

  A cold wind whipped past them. Wujing shivered.

> "Look," said the Tang Monk, pointing.

  Higher up the mountain was a large monastery.

> "Maybe we can rest there," said the monk.

## ⑩

  Wukong looked at the monastery. A dark mist hung over it. The monkey shook his head.

> "That's a bad place. We're not going there."

> "It looks fine to me," said the monk.

> "I can see things that humans can't see," said Wukong. "And I see a dark mist hanging over that monastery. A demon could be in there."

## ⑪

  There was a sudden growling sound. Wukong looked around to see what had caused it. Bajie patted his stomach.

> "Sorry. I'm a bit hungry."

> "We're all tired and hungry," said the Tang Monk. "We should just go to that monastery."

> "No," said Wukong sharply. "Stay here. I will find food for us."

  He looked at the monk and warned him again.

> "Do not go to that monastery."

# 🇨🇳 第六十三章：问佛


## ①

  前方是一段陡峭的河岸。唐僧停下脚步，村民们纷纷躲到树后。悟空、悟净和八戒都拔出了兵器。

  一个巨大的龟壳从水中升起。

> "是一只大海龟。"八戒说。

## ②

  海龟朝悟空低下了头。

> "之前有个恶霸占了我的家和这条河，谢谢你们把他除掉了。"

  海龟又看了看村民们。

> "你们不需要造船了，我可以送行者们过河。"

## ③

> "太好了，"唐僧说。

> "我来自大唐，我知道您是谁，"海龟插话道，"您正在前往西天，向佛祖求取真经。我早就知道您早晚会过我的这条河。请爬上我的龟壳吧。"

  悟空怀疑地打量着海龟。

> "我们怎么知道可以相信你？"

## ④

> "一路上有那么多妖怪等着师父经过，"八戒补充道，"他们都想吃掉他。"

> "我保证不会伤害唐僧，"海龟说，"我想帮他到达西天。"

  一行人犹豫了一会儿，最终踏上了海龟的龟壳。海龟开始划水前行。

## ⑤

  村民们从树后走出来，朝他们挥手告别。

> "再见了，"一个女子喊道，"回来的时候请来看我们啊，"另一个人也喊道。

> "一定会的，我保证，"唐僧喊道。

  不一会儿，海龟已经离岸很远了。八戒睡着了。

## ⑥

  海龟回头望向唐僧。

> "请问，能帮我一个忙吗？"

> "当然可以，"唐僧说，"你需要我做什么？"

> "我已经做了一千多年的好佛教徒了，"海龟说，"可惜我还是只能做一只乌龟。你能帮我问问佛祖，我什么时候才能转世为人吗？"

  唐僧点了点头。

> "我会帮你问的。"

## ⑦

  几个小时后，海龟到达了河对岸。行者们踏上了陆地。

> "谢谢你，"唐僧说。

> "不客气，"海龟说，"请别忘了帮我问佛祖那件事。"

  海龟消失在水中。唐僧翻身上马，一行人继续赶路。

## ⑧

  不出所料，八戒又开始抱怨了。

> "走路太累了，真希望能骑那只海龟一路到西天。"

> "别抱怨了，"悟空说，"这段路平坦，你应该高兴才对。"

  又走了几个钟头，道路开始往山上走，越来越陡。

## ⑨

> "路不平坦了，"八戒哼哼道。

  一阵冷风吹过他们，悟净打了个寒颤。

> "看，"唐僧指着前方说。

  高处有一座大寺院。

> "也许我们可以在那里歇一歇，"唐僧说。

## ⑩

  悟空看着那座寺院，上面笼罩着一团黑雾。猴子摇了摇头。

> "那是个不好的地方，我们不能去。"

> "我看没什么问题啊，"唐僧说。

> "我能看到凡人间看不到的东西，"悟空说，"我看到那座寺院上空飘着一团黑雾，里面可能有妖怪。"

## ⑪

  突然传来一声咕噜声。悟空四下张望，想看看是什么发出的声音。八戒拍了拍肚子。

> "不好意思，我有点饿了。"

> "我们又累又饿，"唐僧说，"我们还是去那座寺院吧。"

> "不行，"悟空断然说道，"你们待在这里，我去给大家找吃的。"

  他看着唐僧，再次警告道。

> "千万不要去那座寺院。"
